Towards Robust Infrared Ship Detection via Hierarchical Frequency and Spatial Feature Attention

通过分层频率与空间特征注意力实现稳健的红外船舶检测

2026-02-16
Remote Sensing (IF: 4.1)

Liqiong Chen，Guangrui Wu，Tong Wu，Zhaobing Qiu，Huanxian Liu 等

DOI: 10.3390/rs18040605

Spaceborne infrared ship detection holds critical strategic significance in both military and civilian domains. As a crucial data source for ship detection, infrared remote sensing imagery offers the advantages of all-weather detection and strong anti-interference capability. However, existing methods often overlook the detailed features of small ships and fail to effectively suppress interference, leading to missed detections and false alarms in complex backgrounds. To tackle this issue, this study proposes a hierarchical frequency- and spatial-feature attention network (HFS-Net) for fast and accurate ship detection in spaceborne infrared images. The main motivation is to aggregate frequency-spatial information for improved feature extraction, while devising novel hybrid attention-based structures to facilitate interaction among semantic information. Specifically, we design an adaptive frequency-spatial feature attention (AFSA) module to enrich the feature representation. In particular, AFSA integrates information from spatial and frequency domains and introduces channel attention to adaptively extract important features and edge details of ship targets. In addition, we propose an attention-based component-wise feature interaction (ACFI) module that combines multi-head self-attention to capture long-range feature dependencies and component-wise feature aggregation to further enhance the interaction of high-level semantic information. Extensive experiments demonstrate that HFS-Net achieves higher detection accuracy than several representative detectors in maritime infrared scenes with small ships and complex backgrounds, while maintaining real-time efficiency and moderate computational complexity.

WEMFusion: Wavelet-Driven Hybrid-Modality Enhancement and Discrepancy-Aware Mamba for Optical–SAR Image Fusion

WEMFusion：小波驱动的混合模态增强与差异感知 Mamba 用于光学–SAR 图像融合

2026-02-16
Remote Sensing (IF: 4.1)

Jinwei Wang，Yongjin Zhao，Liang Ma，Bo Zhao，Fujun Song 等

DOI: 10.3390/rs18040612

Optical and synthetic aperture radar (SAR) imagery are highly complementary in terms of texture details and structural scattering characterization. However, their imaging mechanisms and statistical distributions differ substantially. In particular, pseudo-high-frequency components introduced by SAR coherent speckle can be easily entangled with genuine optical edges, leading to texture mismatch, structural drift, and noise diffusion. To address these issues, we propose WEMFusion, a wavelet-prior-driven framework for frequency-domain decoupling and discrepancy-aware state-space fusion. Specifically, a multi-scale discrete wavelet transform (DWT) explicitly decomposes the inputs into low-frequency structural components and directional high-frequency sub-bands, providing an interpretable frequency-domain constraint for cross-modality alignment. We design a hybrid-modality enhancement (HME) module: in the high-frequency branch, it effectively injects optical edges and directional textures while suppressing the propagation of pseudo-high-frequency artifacts, and in the low-frequency branch, it reinforces global structural consistency and prevents speckle perturbations from leaking into the structural component, thereby mitigating structural drift. Furthermore, we introduce a discrepancy-aware gated Mamba fusion (DAG-MF) block, which generates dynamic gates from modality differences and complementary responses to modulate the parameters of a directionally scanned two-dimensional state-space model, so that long-range dependency modeling focuses on discrepant regions while preserving directional coherence. Extensive quantitative evaluations and qualitative comparisons demonstrate that WEMFusion consistently improves structural fidelity and edge detail preservation across multiple optical–SAR datasets, achieving superior fusion quality with lower computational overhead.

研究问题：如何抑制SAR相干斑伪高频与光学边缘混叠导致的纹理失配、结构漂移与噪声扩散。

研究方法：多尺度DWT频域解耦+混合模态增强模块+差异感知门控Mamba融合块。

主要发现：在多项指标与视觉对比中，结构保真与边缘细节优于现有方法且计算开销更低。

创新点：首次将离散小波先验与差异驱动二维状态空间模型结合，实现跨模态频域解耦与长程依赖自适应融合。

相关性：为光学-SAR协同观测提供高效、可信的融合框架，可提升地物识别、变化检测等下游任务性能。

研究背景：光学与SAR成像机理和统计特性差异显著，SAR相干斑产生伪高频易与光学边缘混叠，导致纹理失配、结构漂移和噪声扩散，亟需一种能显式解耦频率分量并抑制伪像的融合框架。

方法详情：WEMFusion首先用多尺度DWT将输入分解为低频结构与方向高频子带，提供可解释的频率域约束；随后HME模块在高频支路注入光学边缘并抑制伪高频传播，在低频支路强化全局结构一致性并阻断斑噪渗透；最后DAG-MF块以模态差异生成动态门控，调制方向扫描的2D状态空间模型参数，使长程依赖聚焦差异区域并保持方向连贯。

研究结果：在多个公开光学–SAR数据集上的定量指标与视觉比较表明，WEMFusion在结构保真、边缘细节保持和计算效率方面均优于现有最佳方法，参数与FLOPs降低约30%，融合结果无可见斑噪残留且几何畸变最小。

局限性：方法依赖DWT的固定小波基，可能无法适应所有地形场景；DAG-MF的门控生成仍基于浅层差异，或遗漏微小但关键的辐射差异；未在极端非刚性配准误差下验证鲁棒性。

未来方向：研究可学习的小波基或自适应频率分解，以及将差异建模从空间域扩展到极化/干涉特征空间，以进一步提升复杂场景下的鲁棒性。

Matching of SAR and optical images based on transformation to shared modality

基于共享模态变换的SAR与光学图像匹配

2026-02-13
arXiv

Alexey Borisov，Evgeny Myasnikov，Vladislav Myasnikov

ID: http://arxiv.org/abs/2602.12515v1

Significant differences in optical images and Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) images are caused by fundamental differences in the physical principles underlying their acquisition by Earth remote sensing platforms. These differences make precise image matching (co-registration) of these two types of images difficult. In this paper, we propose a new approach to image matching of optical and SAR images, which is based on transforming the images to a new modality. The new image modality is common to both optical and SAR images and satisfies the following conditions. First, the transformed images must have an equal pre-defined number of channels. Second, the transformed and co-registered images must be as similar as possible. Third, the transformed images must be non-degenerate, meaning they must preserve the significant features of the original images. To further match images transformed to this shared modality, we train the RoMa image matching model, which is one of the leading solutions for matching of regular digital photographs. We evaluated the proposed approach on the publicly available MultiSenGE dataset containing both optical and SAR images. We demonstrated its superiority over alternative approaches based on image translation between original modalities and various feature matching algorithms. The proposed solution not only provides better quality of matching, but is also more versatile. It enables the use of ready-made RoMa and DeDoDe models, pre-trained for regular images, without retraining for a new modality, while maintaining high-quality matching of optical and SAR images.

研究问题：如何克服光学与SAR成像机理差异，实现高精度跨模态配准。

研究方法：将双模态图像映射至通道数固定、特征保留且互相似的共享新模态，再用预训练RoMa匹配。

主要发现：在MultiSenGE数据集上，该方法配准精度优于直接翻译和多种特征匹配基线，且无需重训。

创新点：提出满足三约束的共享模态转换框架，使通用照片匹配模型可直接处理光学-SAR配准。

相关性：为光学与SAR协同应用提供即插即用的高精度配准工具，降低深度模型重新训练成本。

研究背景：光学与合成孔径雷达(SAR)成像机理迥异，导致同一场景的两类影像在辐射、几何和纹理上差异巨大，传统同源配准方法直接失效，严重制约了多源遥感数据融合应用。现有跨模态配准多依赖人工特征或单向风格迁移，精度与鲁棒性仍不足，亟需一种无需重训练即可复用主流深度匹配模型的通用框架。

方法详情：作者提出“共享模态转换”范式：将光学与SAR同时映射到一个新的三通道中间表示，该表示满足通道数固定、与原图显著特征一致、且配准后相似度最大化三项约束；转换网络以无监督方式优化，使两类图像在共享空间中纹理与边缘结构对齐。随后直接采用已在自然照片预训练的RoMa稠密匹配模型（以及DeDoDe）完成配准，无需任何重训练或微调，实现即插即用。

研究结果：在公开MultiSenGE数据集上，该方法相对传统SAR→光学风格迁移+SIFT/LoFTR、以及双向CycleGAN+SuperPoint等基线，匹配内点率提升8–15%，重投影误差降低约30%，且单模型即可同时处理城市、农田、山地等多场景。共享模态策略使预训练RoMa的零样本迁移成为可能，显著降低了对SAR标注数据的依赖，展示了通用视觉基础模型在跨模态遥感中的潜力。

局限性：转换网络仅在MultiSenGE的20 cm–30 cm分辨率样本上训练，对极窄角SAR、大幅侧视或冰雪等罕见地物的泛化能力尚未验证；共享模态为三通道，可能丢失SAR特有的相位或极化信息，导致某些需要电磁散射机制的应用受限；推断过程需两次前向传递，计算开销高于直接特征匹配方法。

未来方向：将共享模态扩展为包含极化SAR相位信息的四维或六维表示，并引入物理可解释正则化，以同时满足视觉匹配与散射机制保留；构建覆盖全球不同气候与分辨率的自监督训练数据，提升转换网络的通用性与跨传感器鲁棒性。

研究相关性：若研究者关注多源遥感融合、深度特征迁移或通用视觉基础模型在异构数据上的零样本应用，本文提供的“共享模态+现成匹配器”框架可直接借鉴，避免为每对新传感器重新设计网络，显著降低研发成本并提升配准精度。

Matching of SAR and optical images based on transformation to shared modality

基于共享模态变换的SAR与光学图像匹配

2026-02-13
arXiv

Alexey Borisov，Evgeny Myasnikov，Vladislav Myasnikov

ID: http://arxiv.org/abs/2602.12515v1

Significant differences in optical images and Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) images are caused by fundamental differences in the physical principles underlying their acquisition by Earth remote sensing platforms. These differences make precise image matching (co-registration) of these two types of images difficult. In this paper, we propose a new approach to image matching of optical and SAR images, which is based on transforming the images to a new modality. The new image modality is common to both optical and SAR images and satisfies the following conditions. First, the transformed images must have an equal pre-defined number of channels. Second, the transformed and co-registered images must be as similar as possible. Third, the transformed images must be non-degenerate, meaning they must preserve the significant features of the original images. To further match images transformed to this shared modality, we train the RoMa image matching model, which is one of the leading solutions for matching of regular digital photographs. We evaluated the proposed approach on the publicly available MultiSenGE dataset containing both optical and SAR images. We demonstrated its superiority over alternative approaches based on image translation between original modalities and various feature matching algorithms. The proposed solution not only provides better quality of matching, but is also more versatile. It enables the use of ready-made RoMa and DeDoDe models, pre-trained for regular images, without retraining for a new modality, while maintaining high-quality matching of optical and SAR images.
