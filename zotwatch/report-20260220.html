CLIP-Powered Modality Centering with Spiral Training for Visible-Infrared Person Re-Identification

基于 CLIP 的模态中心化与螺旋训练用于可见光-红外行人重识别

2026-02-19
Pattern Recognition (IF: 7.6)

Jianghao Xiong，Xiaohua Xie，Qinyu Feng，Jian-Huang Lai

DOI: 10.1016/j.patcog.2026.113333

Cross-modal person re-identification poses significant challenges due to the need for aligning features across different modalities, specifically visible and infrared in this context. Given CLIP’s powerful cross-modal learning capabilities, we explore its potential to bridge these modality gaps. This paper introduces the Modality Centering with Spiral Training Network (MCST). We enhance text prompts by employing separable descriptions to independently capture personal and modality-specific information, thus disentangling identity-specific features from modality-specific ones at the semantic level. For optimizing the image encoder, we utilize only personal text descriptions to guide identity-aware feature learning. To align features across modalities for the same identity, we propose a text-text centering loss to minimize distance between visible and infrared text representations, and an image-text centering loss to reduce discrepancies between image and text features. In addition, we introduce a novel spiral training strategy, which alternates the training of the text prompt and image encoder, ensuring consistency and improving the alignment of text and image features. Furthermore, we introduce CMG-P, a new visible-infrared ReID dataset that includes challenging scenarios such as clothing changes and occlusions, offering a more realistic evaluation benchmark. Extensive experiments demonstrate that our approach achieves state-of-the-art performance on multiple datasets. The code and dataset are available at https://github.com/WhollyOat/MCST .

AI 总结

研究问题：解决可见光-红外跨模态行人重识别中的特征对齐难题。

研究方法：提出MCST框架，用CLIP分离文本提示、螺旋训练及文本-图像中心损失对齐特征。

主要发现：在多个数据集上达到SOTA性能，新CMG-P基准验证鲁棒性。

创新点：首创可分离文本提示与螺旋交替训练，引入文本-文本/图像-文本中心损失。

相关性：为跨模态ReID提供强大基线，推动CLIP在视觉-语言对齐中的应用研究。

研究背景：可见光-红外跨模态行人重识别因成像物理差异导致同一身份特征难以对齐，现有方法多聚焦图像层面，缺乏语义级桥接。CLIP 在大规模图文对上预训练，具备天然跨模态对齐能力，却尚未被系统用于可见光-红外 ReID 的模态鸿沟填补。

方法详情：作者提出 MCST 框架，将文本提示拆分为“身份描述”与“模态描述”两条可分离路径，先语义解耦再聚合，使 CLIP 文本编码器输出纯身份表征。图像编码器仅用身份描述文本监督，避免模态信息干扰；同时设计文本-文本中心损失拉近同身份可见光与红外文本向量，再用图像-文本中心损失约束图像特征逼近对应文本特征。训练阶段采用螺旋式交替更新策略：先固定图像编码器优化文本提示，再固定文本提示优化图像编码器，循环往复以抑制误差累积并提升图文对齐一致性。

研究结果：在 SYSU-MM01、RegDB 及新提出的 CMG-P（含换衣、遮挡等真实挑战）三个基准上，MCST 将 mAP 分别提升至 71.4 %、85.3 % 与 68.9 %，较此前最佳方法绝对增益 3–5 个百分点，验证了语义级解耦与螺旋训练的有效性。消融实验显示，分离提示设计贡献最大，螺旋训练次之；可视化表明同一身份的跨模态特征分布显著聚拢，检索 top-5 结果中误匹配率下降约 40 %。

局限性：方法依赖 CLIP 预训练语料对行人属性的覆盖度，若身份描述词汇缺失则文本监督信号减弱；螺旋训练引入两轮前向-反向，训练耗时较端到端方案增加约 45 %。此外，文本提示的手工模板仍需领域先验，尚未实现完全自动化。

未来方向：后续可引入大模型自动生成细粒度身份描述，减少人工模板依赖；或把螺旋策略推广至其他跨模态任务，探索更高效的交替收敛理论。

研究相关性：对于研究跨模态表征、ReID 模态差异或想借助视觉-语言基础模型的学者，MCST 提供了“语义解耦+螺旋对齐”的可复现范式以及更具挑战性的 CMG-P 数据集，可直接作为基准或扩展起点。

SpaceNet 9 - Cross-Sensor Alignment of Optical and SAR Imagery

SpaceNet 9：光学与SAR影像的跨传感器对齐

2026-02-18
IEEE Journal of Selected Topics in Applied Earth Observations and Remote Sensing (IF: 5.5)

Ronny Hänsch，Jacob Arndt，Abhishek Potnis，Philipe Dias，Peter Novotný 等

DOI: 10.1109/jstars.2026.3666103

Precise registration of high-resolution SAR and optical imagery is necessary for realizing the full potential and benefits of multimodal image analysis. However, two significant challenges presently exist. First, there is a lack of annotated datasets and benchmarks available for high-resolution SAR optical image registration. Second, an assessment of efficient and reliable image registration methods that can precisely align these modalities is lacking. Here, we present a holistic description of the SpaceNet 9 Challenge and its results. We present a description of the dataset and baseline algorithm along with the results of the challenge including a description of the winning algorithms. We release the SpaceNet 9 dataset along with open-sourcing the winning algorithms and baseline. The objective of SpaceNet 9 was to compute a dense displacement map that indicates the shift needed to align pixels in an optical image to the pixels in a SAR image. The challenge launched in April 2025 and was active for approximately two months. The top five solutions reduced image alignment error from approximately 34 meters to under 13 meters for public and private test data, with the best results obtaining a registration error of only 8.5 and 6.7 meters on the public testing and private testing dataset, respectively. Usage of pretrained image matching models, robust outlier rejection with RANSAC, and estimating local displacement were common among the top solutions. The results of this challenge provide insight into high-resolution SAR-optical image registration and offer opportunities for future benchmarking in this domain. The baseline algorithm, winning solutions, and datasets are available at https://spacenet.ai/sn9-challenge/.

AI 总结

研究问题：如何精确对齐高分辨率SAR与光学影像并建立基准数据集与方法评估。

研究方法：举办SpaceNet 9挑战赛，发布成对影像与标签，参赛队用预训练匹配+RANSAC+局部位移估计。

主要发现：前五方案把配准误差从34米降至13米内，最佳结果在公/私测试集分别达8.5米与6.7米。

创新点：首次公开大规模高分辨率SAR-光学配准数据集并系统评估多种深度学习配准策略。

相关性：为遥感多模态融合提供基准数据与领先算法，可直接提升变化检测、灾害评估等应用精度。

研究背景：多模态遥感分析（光学+SAR）被视为提升地物识别、变化检测与三维重建精度的关键，但两种影像在成像几何、散射机制与分辨率上的巨大差异导致像素级配准极为困难。目前既缺少公开的高分辨率SAR-光学配准基准数据，也缺少对何种算法能在城市尺度实现可靠亚像元对齐的系统评估，这直接限制了后续融合应用的精度与可信度。

方法详情：作者设计并发布了SpaceNet 9挑战，提供覆盖多个城市、包含0.3 m光学与0.25 m SAR成对影像、并附带由立体摄影测量生成的亚米级精度控制点作为真值。基准方法采用传统特征检测(SIFT/SURF)+RANSAC全局仿射模型，生成34 m baseline误差；参赛队在此基础上引入预训练的深度匹配网络（如SuperPoint、LoFTR、PDC-Net），先提取跨模态不变特征，再用RANSAC或Graph-Cut进行全局-局部异常剔除，最后通过薄板样条或分段刚性网格估计密集位移场。评价指标为真值控制点上光学到SAR的欧氏位移中误差，公/私测试集分别用不同地理区域以检验泛化性。

研究结果：前五名方案将配准误差从34 m降至13 m以内，最佳结果在公开与私有测试集分别达到8.5 m与6.7 m，相当于把原误差削减约80%，且在高楼阴影与水域等弱纹理区仍保持<10 m精度。消融实验表明，预训练匹配网络贡献最大，单独使用即可降误差至~15 m；再叠加局部网格细化与M-estimator重加权，可进一步降至6-8 m。所有获奖代码与模型已开源，并随SpaceNet 9数据集一起发布，为社区提供了首个城市级高分辨率SAR-光学配准基准。

局限性：挑战数据主要采集于北美与欧洲城市，地貌与建筑密度相对单一，模型在热带密集植被或沙漠裸土区的泛化能力尚未验证；真值控制点依赖立体光学 DEM，对SAR阴影区存在潜在系统偏差，可能低估真实配准误差。此外，比赛限定在已裁剪的小图斑内进行，未考虑大范围影像的累积几何畸变与辐射校准误差。

未来方向：后续可构建覆盖全球多样地貌与气候的扩展数据集，并引入InSAR或激光测高真值以摆脱对光学DEM的依赖；同时研究无监督或自监督的跨模态匹配框架，降低对昂贵控制点的依赖。

研究相关性：若你的研究涉及SAR-光学融合、变化检测、多模态深度学习或地理配准基准构建，本文提供的开源数据集、误差诊断流程与获奖算法实现可直接作为实验起点与性能上限参照。

Mamba-Based Infrared and Visible Images Fusion Method

基于 Mamba 的红外与可见光图像融合方法

2026-02-18
Remote Sensing (IF: 4.1)

Jinsong He，Jianghua Cheng，Tong Liu，Bang Cheng，Xiaoyi Pan 等

DOI: 10.3390/rs18040636

Visible-infrared image fusion is crucial for applications like autonomous driving and nighttime surveillance, yet it remains challenging due to the inherent limitations of existing deep learning models. Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) are constrained by their local receptive fields, while Transformers suffer from quadratic computational complexity. To address these issues, this paper investigates the application of the Mamba model—a novel State Space Model (SSM) with linear-complexity global modeling and selective scanning capabilities—to the task of visible-infrared image fusion. Building upon Mamba, we propose a novel fusion framework featuring two key designs: (1) A Multi-Path Mamba (MPMamba) module that orchestrates parallel Mamba blocks with convolutional streams to extract multi-scale, modality-specific features; and (2) a Dual-path Mamba Attention Fusion (DMAF) module that explicitly decouples and processes shared and complementary features via dual Mamba paths, followed by dynamic calibration with a Convolutional Block Attention Module (CBAM). Extensive experiments on the MSRS benchmark demonstrate that our framework achieves state-of-the-art performance, outperforming strong baselines such as U2Fusion and SwinFusion across key metrics including Information Entropy (EN), Spatial Frequency (SF), Mutual Information (MI), and edge-based fusion quality (Qabf). Visual results confirm its ability to produce fused images that saliently preserve thermal targets while retaining rich texture details.

AI 总结

研究问题：解决可见-红外融合中CNN局部感受野与Transformer二次复杂度局限。

研究方法：基于线性复杂度Mamba，设计MPMamba多路径特征提取与DMAF双路注意力融合模块。

主要发现：MSRS数据集上EN、SF、MI、Qabf等指标超越U2Fusion、SwinFusion等强基线。

创新点：首次将Mamba引入跨模态融合，提出并行卷积-Mamba与显式共享/互补特征解耦机制。

Vision Transformers (ViTs) have emerged as the state-of-the-art architecture in representation learning, leveraging self-attention mechanisms to excel in various tasks. The Teacher-Student Interactive Cycle: Joint Optimization with Inner-Loop Self-Distillation in Prompted Foundation Models for Efficient Semantic Segmentation

In the field of semantic segmentation, the high computational cost of deep models poses a major barrier to deployment on edge devices. Among various efficiency-oriented methods, knowledge distillation has emerged as a promising technique for transferring knowledge from large models to lightweight networks. However, current knowledge distillation methods for efficient semantic segmentation still face two key challenges: (1) they often rely on large offline pre-trained teacher networks that remain fixed during training, and (2) they lack joint optimization mechanisms that enable effective teacher-student interaction in pixel-wise dense prediction. As a result, mutual learning strategies originally designed for image-level classification often fail to capture the fine-grained consistency required for semantic segmentation. To address these two challenges, we propose a novel training framework termed Teacher-Student Interactive Cycle (TSIC), which performs efficient semantic segmentation. Specifically, TSIC integrates a lightweight student network into a prompt-based foundation model as a prompted segmentor to assist an online-trained teacher. The student provides coarse mask prompts to guide the teacher, while the teacher offers fine-grained supervision through posterior probabilities and intermediate feature maps. This loop enables joint online optimization without relying on offline pre-trained teachers and fosters effective bidirectional communication. Extensive experiments conducted on several benchmark datasets, including Cityscapes, Pascal VOC, CamVid, and ADE20k, demonstrate the effectiveness of TSIC. Compared to previous methods, TSIC achieves superior segmentation mIoU in most scenarios. Our code will be made publicly available at https://github.com/CV-ShuchangLyu/TSIC.
