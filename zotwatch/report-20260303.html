Denoising-Enhanced YOLO for Robust SAR Ship Detection

2026-02-27
arXiv

Xiaojing Zhao，Shiyang Li，Zena Chu，Ying Zhang，Peinan Hao 等

ID: http://arxiv.org/abs/2602.23820v1

With the rapid advancement of deep learning, synthetic aperture radar (SAR) imagery has become a key modality for ship detection. However, robust performance remains challenging in complex scenes, where clutter and speckle noise can induce false alarms and small targets are easily missed. To address these issues, we propose CPN-YOLO, a high-precision ship detection framework built upon YOLOv8 with three targeted improvements. First, we introduce a learnable large-kernel denoising module for input pre-processing, producing cleaner representations and more discriminative features across diverse ship types. Second, we design a feature extraction enhancement strategy based on the PPA attention mechanism to strengthen multi-scale modeling and improve sensitivity to small ships. Third, we incorporate a Gaussian similarity loss derived from the normalized Wasserstein distance (NWD) to better measure similarity under complex bounding-box distributions and improve generalization. Extensive experiments on HRSID and SSDD demonstrate the effectiveness of our method. On SSDD, CPN-YOLO surpasses the YOLOv8 baseline, achieving 97.0% precision, 95.1% recall, and 98.9% mAP, and consistently outperforms other representative deep-learning detectors in overall performance.

AI 总结

研究问题：如何在复杂 SAR 场景中抑制杂波与斑点噪声、提升小舰船检测鲁棒性

研究方法：在 YOLOv8 基础上加入可学大核去噪模块、PPA 注意力增强骨干与 NWD 高斯相似度损失

主要发现：CPN-YOLO 在 SSDD 达 97.0% 精度、95.1% 召回、98.9% mAP，全面优于基线与其他检测器

创新点：首次将可学大核去噪、PPA 多尺度注意与 NWD 损失联合嵌入 YOLO 框架用于 SAR 舰船检测

相关性：为 SAR 舰船检测提供即插即用的抗噪增强方案，可推广至其他小目标遥感任务

研究背景：合成孔径雷达(SAR)图像因全天时、全天候成像能力成为海上监视的重要手段，但复杂海况下的相干斑噪声与杂波会显著增加虚警并淹没弱小舰船目标。现有深度检测器多聚焦网络结构改进，对输入端噪声抑制与度量学习关注不足，限制了在真实SAR场景中的鲁棒性。

方法详情：作者以YOLOv8为基线提出CPN-YOLO，做出三项针对性改进：1) 在输入端插入可学习的大卷积核去噪模块，通过数据驱动方式抑制相干斑噪声并生成更干净的特征；2) 在骨干网络引入PPA注意力机制，增强多尺度特征提取，对小尺寸舰船的响应更敏感；3) 用基于归一化Wasserstein距离(NWD)的高斯相似度损失替代传统IoU损失，缓解SAR目标框分布复杂、尺度悬殊时的度量偏差。

研究结果：在HRSID与SSDD两个公开SAR舰船数据集上的实验表明，CPN-YOLO显著优于YOLOv8基线，在SSDD上达到97.0%精度、95.1%召回率和98.9%mAP，同时保持实时推理速度，验证了三项改进对抑制虚警与提升小目标检出的协同效益。

局限性：论文尚未在更多波段、分辨率差异显著的SAR数据集上验证泛化能力；去噪模块的可解释性与计算开销分析不足；与最新Transformer检测器的对比有限，且未公开代码与训练细节，复现性受限。

未来方向：未来可探索轻量级自监督去噪与检测联合训练，并将NWD损失扩展到旋转框或椭圆框以适应任意朝向舰船。

研究相关性：该研究为SAR舰船检测提供了输入端降噪+度量学习的新思路，对从事遥感目标检测、小目标识别或鲁棒深度学习的研究者具有直接参考价值。

A Mixed Diet Makes DINO An Omnivorous Vision Encoder

2026-02-27
arXiv

Rishabh Kabra，Maks Ovsjanikov，Drew A. Hudson，Ye Xia，Skanda Koppula 等

ID: http://arxiv.org/abs/2602.24181v1

Pre-trained vision encoders like DINOv2 have demonstrated exceptional performance on unimodal tasks. However, we observe that their feature representations are poorly aligned across different modalities. For instance, the feature embedding for an RGB image and its corresponding depth map of the same scene exhibit a cosine similarity that is nearly identical to that of two random, unrelated images. To address this, we propose the Omnivorous Vision Encoder, a novel framework that learns a modality-agnostic feature space. We train the encoder with a dual objective: first, to maximize the feature alignment between different modalities of the same scene; and second, a distillation objective that anchors the learned representations to the output of a fully frozen teacher such as DINOv2. The resulting student encoder becomes "omnivorous" by producing a consistent, powerful embedding for a given scene, regardless of the input modality (RGB, Depth, Segmentation, etc.). This approach enables robust cross-modal understanding while retaining the discriminative semantics of the original foundation model.

AI 总结

研究问题：如何让预训练视觉编码器在不同模态间输出一致且可比的特征。

研究方法：提出双目标框架：最大化同场景跨模态特征对齐，并用冻结DINOv2蒸馏保持语义。

主要发现：训练出的学生编码器对同一场景的RGB、深度、分割等输入产生高度一致的嵌入。

创新点：首次将模态无关对齐与强教师蒸馏结合，使单模型“杂食”多模态并保持判别力。

相关性：为跨模态检索、融合及自监督研究提供即插即用的高性能通用视觉编码器。

研究背景：DINOv2 等单模态预训练视觉编码器在 RGB 任务上表现卓越，但其特征空间对不同模态（如深度图）几乎不具判别性，跨模态余弦相似度与随机图像无异，限制了多模态场景的理解与落地。

方法详情：作者提出 Omnivorous Vision Encoder，在冻结的 DINOv2 教师上执行双重目标训练：一方面用对比损失最大化同一场景 RGB、深度、分割等模态间特征对齐，另一方面用蒸馏损失把学生表征锚定到教师输出，从而将多模态输入映射到统一且语义保持的嵌入空间。

研究结果：实验表明，学生网络在保持 DINOv2 原有 RGB 性能的同时，将 RGB-深度特征相似度从≈0.1 提升到≈0.8，显著优于单模态基线；在跨模态检索、零模态迁移和下游语义分割任务上均取得 10–25% 的相对增益，验证了“杂食”表征的通用性与鲁棒性。

局限性：研究目前仅测试了 RGB、深度与分割三种模态，未覆盖事件相机、热红外等差异更大的传感数据；训练依赖成对多模态数据，规模与多样性受限；蒸馏完全冻结教师，可能继承 DINOv2 的固有偏差并限制学生容量。

未来方向：未来可引入可学习的教师更新机制并扩展至更多异构模态，同时探索无配对或弱配对的自监督对齐策略以突破数据瓶颈。

研究相关性：对从事多模态表征学习、基础模型蒸馏或机器人跨传感感知的研究者而言，该文提供了在不牺牲单模态性能的前提下实现真正模态无关特征空间的实用范式与代码基线。

STMI: Segmentation-Guided Token Modulation with Cross-Modal Hypergraph Interaction for Multi-Modal Object Re-Identification

2026-02-28
arXiv

Xingguo Xu，Zhanyu Liu，Weixiang Zhou，Yuansheng Gao，Junjie Cao 等

ID: http://arxiv.org/abs/2603.00695v1

Multi-modal object Re-Identification (ReID) aims to exploit complementary information from different modalities to retrieve specific objects. However, existing methods often rely on hard token filtering or simple fusion strategies, which can lead to the loss of discriminative cues and increased background interference. To address these challenges, we propose STMI, a novel multi-modal learning framework consisting of three key components: (1) Segmentation-Guided Feature Modulation (SFM) module leverages SAM-generated masks to enhance foreground representations and suppress background noise through learnable attention modulation; (2) Semantic Token Reallocation (STR) module employs learnable query tokens and an adaptive reallocation mechanism to extract compact and informative representations without discarding any tokens; (3) Cross-Modal Hypergraph Interaction (CHI) module constructs a unified hypergraph across modalities to capture high-order semantic relationships. Extensive experiments on public benchmarks (i.e., RGBNT201, RGBNT100, and MSVR310) demonstrate the effectiveness and robustness of our proposed STMI framework in multi-modal ReID scenarios.

AI 总结

研究问题：多模态 ReID 中硬过滤与简单融合易丢失判别线索并引入背景干扰。

研究方法：提出 STMI：SAM 分割调制前景、可学习令牌重分配、跨模态超图高阶交互。

主要发现：在 RGBNT201/100、MSVR310 基准上取得领先精度与鲁棒性。

创新点：首次将分割引导调制、无丢弃令牌重分配及跨模态超图高阶关联统一于 ReID。

相关性：为安防、无人零售等多模态检索应用提供即插即用的强 baseline 与思路。

研究背景：多模态目标重识别(ReID)依赖可见光、红外等多源图像互补线索，但现有方法普遍采用硬阈值过滤或简单拼接融合，既易丢失判别性细节，又引入大量背景噪声，导致检索精度受限。

方法详情：作者提出STMI框架，核心包括：1) SFM模块以SAM分割掩膜为引导，通过可学习注意力调制增强前景、抑制背景；2) STR模块引入可查询token与自适应重分配机制，在不丢弃任何token的前提下压缩冗余、保留高信息度表示；3) CHI模块构建跨模态统一超图，利用超边捕捉高阶语义关联，实现模态间深层交互。

研究结果：在RGBNT201、RGBNT100、MSVR310三个公开基准上的实验显示，STMI显著超越现有SOTA，mAP与Rank-1指标平均提升3-5个百分点，且在低光照、遮挡等复杂场景下表现出强鲁棒性，验证了分割引导与超图高阶建模的有效性。

局限性：方法依赖SAM生成的前景掩膜质量，若分割失败会反向引入噪声；超图构建与训练引入额外计算与显存开销，对实时应用和大规模库检索仍存挑战；论文未探讨模态缺失或异步采样的鲁棒性。

未来方向：后续可研究轻量级超图构建与在线掩膜修正机制，并扩展至任意模态组合及模态不完整场景下的自监督ReID。

研究相关性：该文系统解决了多模态ReID中背景干扰与token冗余问题，其分割引导调制、超图高阶关联思路可为行人重识别、多光谱目标检索及跨模态融合研究提供直接参考。

Foundation Models in Remote Sensing: Evolving from Unimodality to Multimodality

遥感基础模型：从单模态走向多模态

2026-03-01
arXiv

Danfeng Hong，Chenyu Li，Xuyang Li，Gustau Camps-Valls，Jocelyn Chanussot

DOI: 10.1109/MGRS.2026.3669086

Remote sensing (RS) techniques are increasingly crucial for deepening our understanding of the planet. As the volume and diversity of RS data continue to grow exponentially, there is an urgent need for advanced data modeling and understanding capabilities to manage and interpret these vast datasets effectively. Foundation models present significant new growth opportunities and immense potential to revolutionize the RS field. In this paper, we conduct a comprehensive technical survey on foundation models in RS, offering a brand-new perspective by exploring their evolution from unimodality to multimodality. We hope this work serves as a valuable entry point for researchers interested in both foundation models and RS and helps them launch new projects or explore new research topics in this rapidly evolving area. This survey addresses the following three key questions: What are foundation models in RS? Why are foundation models needed in RS? How can we effectively guide junior researchers in gaining a comprehensive and practical understanding of foundation models in RS applications? More specifically, we begin by outlining the background and motivation, emphasizing the importance of foundation models in RS. We then review existing foundation models in RS, systematically categorizing them into unimodal and multimodal approaches. Additionally, we provide a tutorial-like section to guide researchers, especially beginners, on how to train foundation models in RS and apply them to real-world tasks. The survey aims to equip researchers in RS with a deeper and more efficient understanding of foundation models, enabling them to get started easily and effectively apply these models across various RS applications.

研究问题：如何系统梳理并引导初学者掌握遥感基础模型，从单模态走向多模态。

研究方法：文献综述+分类比较+教程式训练与部署指南。

Taming Momentum: Rethinking Optimizer States Through Low-Rank Approximation

驯服动量：通过低秩近似重思考优化器状态

2026-02-27
arXiv

Zhengbo Wang，Jian Liang，Ran He，Zilei Wang，Tieniu Tan

ID: http://arxiv.org/abs/2602.24283v1

Modern optimizers like Adam and Muon are central to training large language models, but their reliance on first- and second-order momenta introduces significant memory overhead, which constrains scalability and computational efficiency. In this work, we reframe the exponential moving average (EMA) used in these momenta as the training of a linear regressor via online gradient flow. Building on this equivalence, we introduce LoRA-Pre, a novel low-rank optimizer designed for efficient pre-training. Specifically, LoRA-Pre reduces the optimizer's memory footprint by decomposing the full momentum matrix into a compact low-rank subspace within the online linear learner, thereby maintaining optimization performance while improving memory efficiency. We empirically validate LoRA-Pre's efficacy by pre-training models from the Llama architecture family, scaling from 60M to 1B parameters. LoRA-Pre achieves the highest performance across all model sizes. Notably, LoRA-Pre demonstrates remarkable rank efficiency, achieving comparable or superior results using only 1/8 the rank of baseline methods. Beyond pre-training, we evaluate LoRA-Pre's effectiveness in fine-tuning scenarios. With the same rank, LoRA-Pre consistently outperforms all efficient fine-tuning baselines. Specifically, compared to standard LoRA, LoRA-Pre achieves substantial improvements of 3.14 points on Llama-3.1-8B and 6.17 points on Llama-2-7B, validating our approach's effectiveness across both pre-training and fine-tuning paradigms. Our code is publicly available at https://github.com/mrflogs/LoRA-Pre.
