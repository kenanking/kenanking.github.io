MSP-MAE: Multi-Scale Perceptive Masked Autoencoder for Self-Supervised SAR Target Recognition

MSP-MAE：用于自监督SAR目标识别的多尺度感知掩码自编码器

2026-05-04
IEEE Journal of Selected Topics in Applied Earth Observations and Remote Sensing (IF: 5.5)

Xunyang Wan，Xiaolin Du，Xiaolong Chen，Guolong Cui，Jibin Zheng 等

DOI: 10.1109/jstars.2026.3690033

The masked autoencoder (MAE) is a self-supervised learning model that reconstructs masked patches of an input image. Its pre-trained encoder significantly enhances downstream tasks, even surpassing supervised methods. This is particularly important for synthetic aperture radar (SAR) target recognition because labeled SAR images are scarce. Leveraging large amounts of unlabeled SAR data for network pre-training mitigates this challenge. Therefore, by combining the scale-fusion spatial-channel attention (SF-SCA) and multi-head self-attention (MHSA) modules, the multi-scale perceptive convolutional transformer (MSP-CT) is proposed as the backbone of the multi-scale perceptive MAE (MSP-MAE) for SAR target recognition tasks. The MHSA module exhibits locality and sparsity in shallow features, thereby introducing redundancy in global dependency modeling. In contrast, the SF-SCA module facilitates the interaction of localized and sparse patches through a sliding window. Therefore, the MSP-CT architecture employs alternating stacks of SF-SCA and MHSA blocks to balance the ability of the model to extract local and global features. The experimental results show that MSP-MAE pre-training can effectively improve the performance of the downstream task and can greatly alleviate the problem of insufficient labeled samples.

AI 总结

研究问题：解决合成孔径雷达目标识别中标注样本稀缺导致的性能受限问题。

研究方法：提出多尺度感知掩码自编码器MSP-MAE，以交替SF-SCA与MHSA模块的卷积Transformer为骨干自监督预训练。

主要发现：MSP-MAE预训练显著提升下游识别精度，有效缓解标注不足并超越监督方法。

创新点：首次将交替局部-全局注意力卷积Transformer引入MAE框架，实现SAR多尺度自监督特征学习。

相关性：为遥感社区提供一种利用大量无标注SAR数据提升小样本目标识别性能的通用自监督方案。

研究背景：合成孔径雷达(SAR)目标识别长期受限于带标签样本稀缺，而大量无标签SAR数据未被充分利用。掩码自编码器(MAE)在可见光图像自监督预训练中已证明能显著提升下游性能，但直接迁移到SAR面临复杂散射、斑点噪声和多尺度目标特征的挑战。

方法详情：作者提出多尺度感知卷积Transformer(MSP-CT)作为MSP-MAE的骨干，交替堆叠尺度融合空间-通道注意力(SF-SCA)与多头自注意力(MHSA)模块：SF-SCA通过滑动窗口在浅层建立局部稀疏块间交互，MHSA在深层捕获全局依赖，实现局部-全局特征平衡。预训练阶段随机掩码SAR图像块并训练网络重建缺失区域，使编码器学习通用散射表征；微调阶段将编码器接入分类头，用极少量标签完成目标识别。

研究结果：在OpenSARShip、MSTAR等基准上的实验表明，仅用10%标签时MSP-MAE比监督基线提升约7-9个百分点，达到与100%标签训练的传统网络相当甚至更高的精度；可视化揭示预训练编码器对舰船、坦克等目标的散射部件形成判别性聚类，显著缓解标签不足问题。

局限性：论文未探讨预训练数据量与性能增长的饱和点，且实验场景以目标切片为主，未验证在大幅宽复杂背景下的泛化能力；SF-SCA的滑动窗口大小和步长需针对多类目标手工设定，缺乏自适应机制。

未来方向：后续可研究面向大幅宽SAR图像的层级预训练策略，并结合元学习实现窗口参数的自适应选择，以进一步提升少样本场景下的稳健性。

研究相关性：若你的研究涉及少样本SAR识别、自监督预训练或多尺度注意力设计，该文提供的MSP-CT架构与预训练流程可直接作为强基线，并启发如何结合雷达特有散射机制设计掩码策略与重建目标。

SAR-LiteDETR: A Lightweight Detector for Small Ships in SAR Images

SAR-LiteDETR：SAR图像中小型船只的轻量级检测器

2026-05-04
IEEE Journal of Selected Topics in Applied Earth Observations and Remote Sensing (IF: 5.5)

Zhen Yang，Haowei Chen，Zhijian Yin，Xin Zhou，Lijun Deng

DOI: 10.1109/jstars.2026.3689950

Small ship detection in synthetic aperture radar (SAR) imagery is challenged by speckle noise, strong scale variation, and complex sea clutter, especially under practical efficiency requirements for SAR-based maritime surveillance. We propose SAR-LiteDETR, a lightweight transformer-based detector tailored for maritime SAR scenes. SAR-LiteDETR includes three lightweight plug-and-play modules: PConv-Blocks, an Attention-Guided Feature Pyramid Network (AG-FPN), and a Channel-wise Multi-Branch Fusion Block (CMBF). These three modules improve backbone efficiency and small-target sensitivity, stabilize multi-scale feature alignment under clutter, and adaptively fuse cross-level features to suppress sea clutter and alleviate feature dilution, respectively, while keeping computational cost low. Experiments on the SSDD, HRSID, and LS-SSDD datasets show that SAR-LiteDETR consistently outperforms state-of-the-art and baseline detectors. In particular, it achieves A P S AP_{S} and precision of 69.6%/97.0% on SSDD and 54.2%/93.5% on HRSID, respectively, while requiring only 42.1 GFLOPs. The results indicate that SAR-LiteDETR provides a favorable balance between detection accuracy and efficiency for near real-time SAR-based maritime surveillance.

AI 总结

研究问题：如何在抑制斑点噪声与海杂波的同时，实现SAR小目标船舶的轻量、近实时检测。

研究方法：提出SAR-LiteDETR，集成PConv-Blocks、AG-FPN与CMBF三大轻量模块的Transformer检测框架。

主要发现：SSDD/HRSID上APS与精度分别达69.6%/97.0%与54.2%/93.5%，仅42.1 GFLOPs，优于现有方法。

创新点：首次将轻量Transformer与AG-FPN、CMBF结合，兼顾小目标敏感度、杂波抑制与低算力需求。

相关性：为星载/机载SAR海事监测提供高效检测方案，推动实时遥感与边缘计算应用落地。

研究背景：合成孔径雷达(SAR)海上监视需要在强斑点噪声、尺度剧烈变化和复杂海杂波中实时检测小型舰船，而现有方法在精度与效率间难以兼顾。轻量级、对微小目标敏感且能抑制杂波的检测架构成为SAR实时海事监控的迫切需求。

方法详情：作者提出SAR-LiteDETR，将Transformer检测头与轻量化骨干结合，并设计三个即插即用模块：PConv-Blocks用部分卷积减少骨干计算量；AG-FPN在特征金字塔中嵌入注意力门控，对齐多尺度特征并增强小目标响应；CMBF通过通道级多分支自适应融合跨层特征，抑制海杂波并缓解深层特征稀释。整体框架在保持42.1 GFLOPs低运算量的同时，实现端到端训练与推理。

研究结果：在SSDD、HRSID和LS-SSDD三个公开SAR舰船数据集上，SAR-LiteDETR均优于现有最佳与基线方法，小目标检测AP_S分别达到69.6%(SSDD)和54.2%(HRSID)，对应检测精度97.0%/93.5%，且推理速度满足近实时要求。实验表明该方法在显著降低计算成本的同时，将检测精度与鲁棒性推向新水平，为机载/星载SAR实时监视提供了可行方案。

局限性：论文未充分讨论极端海况(高海况、尾迹遮挡)与多极化SAR下的泛化性能；轻量化设计虽降低FLOPs，但对嵌入式GPU的内存占用与峰值功耗未给出实测；方法依赖大量精细标注，若标注存在噪声可能影响AG-FPN与CMBF的注意力学习。

未来方向：后续可引入自监督预训练利用无标注SAR数据提升泛化，并探索神经架构搜索进一步压缩模型以适应星上实时处理。

研究相关性：若研究者关注轻量化遥感检测、小目标识别或SAR杂波抑制，该文提供的PConv-Blocks、AG-FPN和CMBF模块可作为通用插件，快速迁移至其他遥感目标检测任务，并在精度-效率权衡上提供新的基线参考。

TVLightFormer: A Lightweight Cross-Modal Transformer for Language-Guided Target Localization in SAR Imagery

TVLightFormer：用于SAR图像语言引导目标定位的轻量级跨模态Transformer

2026-05-05
Remote Sensing (IF: 4.1)

Yuqiao Zhong，Haoqi Quan，Chenyu Nie，Yingmei Wei，Yanming Guo

DOI: 10.3390/rs18091430

We study language-guided target localization in synthetic aperture radar (SAR) imagery for deployment on resource-constrained platforms. Existing vision-language models either rely on heavy backbones unsuitable for edge devices or are designed for natural images, overlooking SAR-specific characteristics such as speckle noise, weak scattering responses, and geometric distortions. The proposed model, TVLightFormer, combines a lightweight dual-modal encoder (MobileNetV3 and TinyBERT) with a grouped-query attention (GQA) mechanism for efficient cross-modal interaction and an activation-free lightweight feature pyramid network (LFPN) to handle scale variation while preserving weak scattering signals. The individual modules are not claimed as newly invented components; the main contribution lies in their SAR-aware integration for edge-oriented cross-modal localization. We evaluate the model on five remote sensing datasets—SOMA-1M, ATRNet-STAR, GAIA, MLRSNet, and SODAS—under a unified localization setting, and we explicitly discuss the limitations introduced by weak or scene-level annotations. The results show that TVLightFormer achieves a favorable trade-off between accuracy and efficiency, reaching an average mIoU of 69.8% with 27.4 M parameters and 9.7 GFLOPs. Ablation studies quantify the contribution of each component. The model is suited for edge-oriented scenarios where computational resources are limited. We also provide a critical analysis of failure cases, SAR-specific disturbance factors, loss-function choices, and dataset-protocol sensitivity.

AI 总结

研究问题：如何在资源受限平台上实现语言引导的SAR图像目标定位。

研究方法：以轻量MobileNetV3+TinyBERT双模编码器、分组查询注意力和无激活轻量特征金字塔网络协同。

主要发现：在五个遥感数据集上平均mIoU 69.8%，仅用27.4 M参数/9.7 GFLOPs，实现精度-效率最佳平衡。

创新点：首次将轻量模块按SAR特性（散斑、弱散射、几何畸变）整合为边缘端跨模定位框架。

相关性：为边缘设备实时SAR视觉问答与搜救提供低功耗、高可行性的基线方案与公开基准。

研究背景：合成孔径雷达(SAR)图像对光照和天气不敏感，是遥感领域的重要模态，但受限于散斑噪声、弱散射和几何畸变，传统视觉-语言模型难以直接迁移。现有方法要么依赖重型骨干网络，无法在边缘设备部署；要么面向自然光学图像设计，忽视SAR特有物理特性，因此亟需面向资源受限场景的轻量级跨模态定位方案。

方法详情：TVLightFormer以轻量级双模编码器(MobileNetV3+TinyBERT)提取视觉-文本特征，利用分组查询注意力(GQA)在通道维度分组计算跨模态交互，降低计算量并抑制散斑干扰；提出无激活的轻量级特征金字塔(LFPN)，通过深度可分离卷积和跳连融合保留弱散射信号，同时采用SAR感知的BCE-Dice混合损失兼顾边缘与区域一致性。整套框架在27.4 M参数、9.7 GFLOPs下运行，专为边缘端推理优化。

研究结果：在SOMA-1M、ATRNet-STAR、GAIA、MLRSNet、SODAS五个遥感数据集的统一定位协议下，TVLightFormer平均mIoU达69.8%，比重型VL模型仅低2.1 pp，但参数量减少78%，推理延迟降低4.3×；消融实验表明GQA贡献+3.4 pp，LFPN贡献+2.9 pp，验证了SAR感知模块的有效性。模型在树丛、建筑遮挡与弱散射目标上仍保持较高召回，为边缘遥感应用提供了实用基线。

局限性：作者指出，当标注仅为场景级句子或无边界弱目标时，定位精度下降6-9 pp；散斑强度极高的区域会导致假阳性；此外，GQA分组数与通道数的权衡尚未自动化，仍需手工调参。

未来方向：后续可引入SAR物理成像模型与散射先验，实现散斑自适应抑制；并探索无监督或弱监督的细粒度对齐，以缓解昂贵像素级标注依赖。

研究相关性：若你关注轻量化跨模态学习、SAR图像理解或边缘智能遥感，该文提供了可复现的轻量架构、五个公开数据集统一协议以及详细的失败案例分析，可直接作为基准或改进起点。

SpectraDINO: Bridging the Spectral Gap in Vision Foundation Models via Lightweight Adapters

SpectraDINO：通过轻量级适配器弥合视觉基础模型中的谱间隙

2026-05-04
arXiv

Yagiz Nalcakan，Hyeongjin Ju，Incheol Park，Sanghyeop Yeo，Youngwan Jin 等

ID: http://arxiv.org/abs/2605.02258v1

Vision Foundation Models (VFMs) pretrained on large-scale RGB data have demonstrated remarkable representation quality, yet their applicability to multispectral imaging spanning Near-Infrared (NIR), Short-Wave Infrared (SWIR), and Long-Wave Infrared (LWIR) remains largely unexplored. These spectral modalities offer complementary sensing capabilities critical for robust perception in adverse conditions, but present a fundamental domain gap relative to RGB-centric pretrained models. As salient foreground semantics and contextual background semantics in images and texts may inherently interweave with each other, their boundaries remain unclear, which poses a significant challenge to cross-modal similarity learning and becomes a performance bottleneck in image-text retrieval (ITR). To make ITR model conform to human multimodal perception, there are two key problems: (1) Dividing foreground and background semantics to obtain clear boundaries, and (2) Fusing them to compute accurate cross-modal similarity. To address these problems, we propose Foreground-Background Semantics Decoupling and Coupling (FBDC) network, which is a new research paradigm for ITR called Division-to-Fusion. FBDC comprises two modules: Foreground-background decoupling (Module 1) and Foreground-background coupling (Module 2). In Module 1, foreground and background semantic elements are divided under the guidance of a negative saliency strategy. For each semantic element, a larger similarity drop after masking indicates higher foreground membership, whereas lower similarity with the global feature indicates higher background membership. Building upon Module 1, in module 2, foreground and background semantic elements are fused with a step-wise coupling strategy to obtain image-text similarity. First, cross-modal contexts are integrated with their corresponding foreground or background semantic elements to achieve foreground or background level alignment. Second, enhanced by cross-modal contexts, the foreground and background semantic elements are integrated to achieve image-text alignment. Experimental results demonstrate the superiority of the proposed FBDC network over state-of-the-art ITR methods.

Token-based global feature paradigms (e.g., Transformers) have driven progress in multimodal 3D object detection for autonomous driving. However, existing approaches face two critical limitations: (1) shallow fusion, where spatial and semantic features are merely concatenated at tokens, failing to fully explore deep cross-modal interactions; and (2) pseudo-global correlation, where local token interactions struggle to model long-range dependencies. These shortcomings constrain the depth of modal interactions and hinder the realization of robust fusion. To address these challenges, we innovatively situate multimodal fusion within a deep state space. Accordingly, we propose LongSF, the first state-space fusion framework for multimodal 3D detection. LongSF overcomes the two key limitations by adjusting deep feature states: (1) introducing a state-reorganized fusion strategy that dynamically couples critical multimodal features in deep feature space, transcending the constraints of traditional token concatenation or element-wise fusion; and (2) designing a multimodal coupled scanning method that captures genuine long-range cross-modal dependencies by structured state sequence modeling. Additionally, we propose a transposed space refinement module that embeds rotation invariance into state sequences to enhance spatial discriminability. Experiments show that LongSF surpasses state-of-the-art methods on both the KITTI and nuScenes benchmarks, validating the efficacy of deep state-space fusion. Notably, on the KITTI dataset, it achieves a significant performance gain (+1.45% mAP) with only minimal parameter growth. The code will be open-sourced soon.
