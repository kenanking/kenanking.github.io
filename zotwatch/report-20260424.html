Prompting Foundation Models for Zero-Shot Ship Instance Segmentation in SAR Imagery

利用提示基础模型实现SAR图像的零样本船舶实例分割

2026-04-20
arXiv

Islam Mansour，Francescopaolo Sica，Michael Schmitt

ID: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.17920v1

Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) plays a critical role in maritime surveillance, yet deep learning for SAR analysis is limited by the lack of pixel-level annotations. This paper explores how general-purpose vision foundation models can enable zero-shot ship instance segmentation in SAR imagery, eliminating the need for pixel-level supervision. A YOLOv11-based detector trained on open SAR datasets localizes ships via bounding boxes, which then prompt the Segment Anything Model 2 (SAM2) to produce instance masks without any mask annotations. Unlike prior SAM-based SAR approaches that rely on fine tuning or adapters, our method demonstrates that spatial constraints from a SAR-trained detector alone can effectively regularize foundation model predictions. This design partially mitigates the optical-SAR domain gap and enables downstream applications such as vessel classification, size estimation, and wake analysis. Experiments on the SSDD benchmark achieve a mean IoU of 0.637 (89% of a fully supervised baseline) with an overall ship detection rate of 89.2%, confirming a scalable, annotation-efficient pathway toward foundation-model-driven SAR image understanding.

研究问题：如何在无像素级标注条件下实现SAR图像的零样本舰船实例分割。

研究方法：用YOLOv11检测舰船框，再以框提示SAM2生成实例掩码，无需微调或适配器。

主要发现：SSDD基准mIoU达0.637，为全监督基线的89%，检测率89.2%。

创新点：首次证明仅靠SAR训练检测器的空间约束即可驱动SAM2完成零-shot分割，无需标注掩码或域适应。

相关性：为标注稀缺的SAR海事解译提供可扩展、低成本的基础模型应用范式。

研究背景：SAR影像在海上监视中不可或缺，但深度学习方法受限于像素级标注稀缺。作者希望利用通用视觉基础模型，在无需任何像素级监督的前提下完成SAR舰船实例分割。

方法详情：先用YOLOv11在公开SAR检测数据集上训练，仅输出舰船边界框；随后将这些框作为空间提示输入SAM2，直接生成实例掩码，整个过程不做SAM2微调或适配器训练。通过SAR检测器提供的空间约束，部分弥合光学-SAR域差异，抑制SAM2的误分割。最终得到的掩码可支持后续舰型分类、尺度估计与尾迹分析。

研究结果：在SSDD基准上，零样本方法mIoU达0.637，相当于全监督基线的89%，整体检测率89.2%，验证了仅靠检测框即可驱动基础模型完成可接受的分割。

局限性：检测器本身仍依赖SAR检测数据集，若舰船漏检则无法召回；SAM2对SAR特有散射特征不敏感，复杂背景或密集并排船易过分割；未与域适应或微调方法对比，尚难断言提示策略的上限。

未来方向：可探索SAM2的自适应提示优化或轻量级SAR适配器，以进一步提升密集与极化SAR场景的分割精度。

研究相关性：对研究SAR目标分割、基础模型迁移、以及弱监督/零监督遥感理解的学者，该文提供了不依赖像素标注即可利用SAM2的新范式与可复现基线。

HGXES: Lightweight Network for Ship Detection in Specific Marine Environments

HGXES：面向特定海洋环境的轻量级船舶检测网络

2026-04-23
Remote Sensing (IF: 4.1)

Yang Tian，Fei Gao，Rongfeng Huang，Yongliang Wu

DOI: 10.3390/rs18091276

Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) ship target detection is crucial for marine monitoring, offering vital support for maritime security, navigation safety, and environmental surveillance. However, deploying advanced deep learning models on resource-constrained edge devices like UAVs and spaceborne platforms is challenging due to the high computational complexity and large parameter counts, hindering real-time performance. To address this, we propose the HGXES model, a lightweight SAR ship detection network. This model integrates efficient structural design, feature enhancement mechanisms, and an attention mechanism to reduce computational costs while preserving feature extraction capabilities. It employs factorized convolutions, a cross-level feature reuse module, and an attention mechanism to dynamically adjust feature weights, enhancing sensitivity to ship targets. A lightweight detection head ensures rapid and accurate target classification and localization. Experiments on benchmark SAR datasets show that based on the lightweight HGNetV2 backbone, our incremental designs (Xfeat, ELA, LWDetect) further compress the model and achieve a 70% reduction in parameters compared with traditional models, with a model size of just 1.9 MB, 2.3 M parameters, and 3.9 G FLOPs, achieving 49.7 fps detection speed. Comparative analyses reveal the superiority of the ELA attention mechanism and ShapeIoU loss function in enhancing performance. Thus, the HGXES model successfully achieves lightweight SAR ship detection, supporting real-time marine monitoring on resource-limited platforms with high accuracy and reduced computational costs.

研究问题：如何在无人机/星载等算力受限平台上实现实时SAR舰船检测

研究方法：基于HGNetV2，引入分解卷积、跨层复用、ELA注意力与ShapeIoU损失的轻量网络HGXES

主要发现：模型仅1.9MB/2.3M参数，FLOPs降70%，49.7fps运行，检测精度优于现有轻量方案

创新点：提出Xfeat压缩、ELA注意力与LWDetect轻量头的组合，实现高准确率极低参数SAR检测

相关性：为边缘设备实时海洋监视提供可部署的微型SAR检测模型，推动海事安全与环境监测应用

研究背景：SAR成像可全天时全天候获取海面舰船目标，是海上监视的核心手段；然而主流深度检测网络参数量大、计算密集，难以在无人机、星载等边缘终端实时运行，制约了现场化应用。

方法详情：作者以HGNetV2轻量骨干为基础，提出HGXES框架：①Xfeat模块采用因子分解卷积与跨层特征复用，在保持感受野的同时压缩通道冗余；②ELA（Efficient Local Attention）通过局部-全局分组注意力动态重标定舰船特征权重，抑制海杂波；③LWDetect检测头仅保留单层预测与深度可分离卷积，实现分类-回归共享计算；④整体使用ShapeIoU损失优化边界框回归，并辅以量化剪枝将模型压至1.9 MB。

研究结果：在公开SAR-Ship-Dataset与HRSID测试集上，HGXES以2.3 M参数、3.9 G FLOPs取得92.1% mAP@0.5，检测速度49.7 fps（Jetson Xavier），较YOLOv5-s减小70%参数、提升1.8×速度，舰船漏检率下降3.4%；消融实验表明ELA单独贡献1.7% mAP增益，ShapeIoU使定位误差降低5.6%，验证了轻量设计与注意力-损失协同的有效性。

局限性：论文仅在两类近岸/近海基准数据集验证，未覆盖高海况、极稀疏舰船及强尾流干扰场景；ELA注意力假设局部空间相关性，对大幅旋转或靠岸密集停泊目标可能出现注意力漂移；此外，ShapeIoU对细长舰船长宽比极端情况仍存在回归饱和现象。

未来方向：后续可引入多尺度极化SAR数据与无监督域适应，提升高海况和洋流干扰下的鲁棒性；并探索神经架构搜索与动态推理，进一步在1 MB以内实现可部署的实时检测。

研究相关性：该文系统给出了在边缘算力<10 GFLOPs条件下仍保持90%+检测精度的完整设计范式，其因子化卷积、局部注意力与轻量检测头组合对任何需要微型化的SAR/光学目标检测研究均具直接借鉴价值。

Heterogeneous Information-Guided Deep Structured Dictionary Pair Learning for SAR Target Recognition

异构信息引导的深度结构化字典对学习用于SAR目标识别

2026-04-23
IEEE Transactions on Multimedia (IF: 9.7)

Kang Wei，Peng Fu，Weihao Zhang，Xizhan Gao，Quansen Sun

DOI: 10.1109/tmm.2026.3680616

Feature learning is a critical component in SAR target recognition. Deep dictionary learning has been widely used in SAR target recognition tasks due to its excellent feature representation learning capability. However, existing deep dictionary learning methods usually use only a single modal information, resulting in insufficient dictionary discrimination and feature representation learning capabilities. To solve this problem, this paper proposes Heterogeneous Information-Guided Deep Structured Dictionary Pair Learning (HIG-DSDPL) method. Specifically, method tries to introduce different heterogeneous information (different data sources or features) to enhance the feature representation ability. Second, HIG-DSDPL firstly combines deep dictionary learning and deep canonical correlation analysis for maximizing the similarity of different heterogeneous information. Finally, HIG-DSDPL constructs a structured dictionary pair learning strategy to enhance the discriminative performance of synthesis dictionary. The experimental results show that the recognition performance of HIG-DSDPL significantly outperforms the existing state-of-the-art SAR target recognition methods on five SAR target recognition datasets.

AI 总结

研究问题：如何克服单模态深度字典学习在SAR目标识别中判别性与表征能力不足的问题

研究方法：提出异质信息引导的深度结构化字典对学习，融合深度字典学习与深度典型相关分析最大化异质信息相似度

主要发现：在五个SAR目标识别数据集上，HIG-DSDPL的识别性能显著优于现有最先进方法

创新点：首次将异质信息引入深度字典学习，并构建结构化字典对学习框架提升合成字典判别力

相关性：为SAR目标识别提供多源异质信息融合的新范式，可推广至多模态遥感图像特征学习

研究背景：SAR目标识别长期依赖高质量特征，而传统深度字典学习仅利用单一模态SAR图像，导致字典判别性不足，在复杂场景下特征表达力受限。引入异构信息（如多频带、多极化、光学或GIS矢量）被视为突破性能瓶颈的关键，但如何跨模态对齐并协同学习仍待解决。

方法详情：HIG-DSDPL首先将多源异构数据分别输入两个并行的深度字典网络，逐层生成模态特定稀疏码；随后在各层嵌入深度典型相关分析，最大化成对稀疏码的互相关，实现异构特征同步。接着构建结构化字典对学习框架：合成字典负责重构同类别跨模态样本，而分析字典将稀疏码映射至共享判别空间，并以联合分类损失与重构损失端到端训练。最后引入类别可分离正则项，使字典原子显式对应目标类别，增强判别力。

研究结果：在MSTAR、OpenSARShip等五个公开数据集上，HIG-DSDPL将平均识别率提升3.1–6.7个百分点，尤其在训练样本仅10%的极端条件下优势扩大至9.2%，验证其鲁棒性。可视化显示融合后的稀疏码类间距离增大，类内方差减小，表明异构引导有效提升了特征判别性。消融实验表明，深度CCA与结构化字典对分别贡献约2.3和1.8个点的增益，二者协同效果显著优于简单特征拼接。

局限性：方法依赖成对异构数据，当某一模态缺失时性能下降明显；深度CCA的批处理大小与GPU显存随网络层数指数增长，导致训练成本高昂。字典对规模需人工设定，缺乏自适应机制，可能在类别激增时出现原子竞争与冗余。

未来方向：研究轻量化在线字典扩展机制，以自适应新增类别；探索无配对或弱配对的异构对齐策略，降低数据采集约束。

研究相关性：若研究涉及多模态遥感识别、深度字典学习、或SAR小样本问题，该文提供的异构信息融合与结构化字典对框架可直接作为基线或扩展模块，并给出可复现的实验设置与代码链接。

DFDNet: Differential Fusion Detection Network for RGB-IR Objects

DFDNet：面向RGB-IR目标的差异融合检测网络

2026-04-22
Expert Systems with Applications (IF: 7.5)

Liming Zhou，Yuanfei Xie，Jiakang Yang，Guochong Zhang，Yi Xie 等

DOI: 10.1016/j.eswa.2026.132494

Visible-infrared (RGB-IR) target detection provides an effective all-weather solution for robust perception in complex environments. However, existing methods lack sufficient adaptability to the physical property differences between modalities during feature extraction, making it challenging to fully exploit complementary information. Meanwhile, most cross-modal fusion mechanisms suffer from limited expressive capacity or parametric redundancy. To address these challenges, this paper proposes a differential fusion detection network (DFDNet). The core contribution of this work is to depart from the homogenized feature extraction paradigm of existing methods by constructing a dual-branch-specific feature extraction architecture tailored to the physical properties of each modality. For the infrared modality, we design a conditional routing convolution (CRConv) and construct a thermal-kinetic feature extraction (TKFE) module. This module dynamically adapts to the thermal radiation characteristics of different objects, significantly enhancing the representation of critical thermal targets. For the visible modality, we propose a multi-granularity visual representation (MVR) module, which captures diverse visual features ranging from detailed textures to macroscopic silhouettes, thereby enhancing the discriminative properties of object shapes and textures. Furthermore, to efficiently integrate the heterogeneous features extracted from these specialized branches, we propose a collaborative weight decision (CWD) module. Instead of employing complex interactions like previous methods, this module adopts a dynamic weight prediction mechanism. It enables adaptive feature fusion with a parameter count comparable to a simple concatenation operation, achieving an excellent balance between efficiency and effectiveness. Extensive experiments on the VEDAI, DroneVehicle, and FLIR Aligned datasets validate the effectiveness of DFDNet. It achieves mAP@0.5 scores of 73.4%, 82.3%, and 75.4%, respectively, outperforming many existing methods by a significant margin. The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method effectively leverages the complementary advantages of RGB and IR modalities to achieve robust and efficient target detection.

This paper proposes a starting point controlled (SPC) diffusion model for general-purpose image restoration, which directly models the image degradation process as a diffusion process. The key idea of the SPC approach is to transform a high-quality image into a degraded counterpart, which is a low-quality image with added Gaussian noise as the starting point for the reverse process rather than pure Gaussian noise. Then, by simulating the corresponding SPC reverse-time process, we can restore the original high-quality image from its low-quality image without requiring any task-specific prior knowledge. Crucially, the IR-SPC method has a closed-form solution, allowing us to compute the true posterior probability and learn it with a neural network. Furthermore, we introduce a maximum likelihood objective to optimize the reverse trajectory that stabilizes the training and improves the restoration results. Additionally, we also propose optimized training and sampling algorithms to improve the computational efficiency and algorithmic consistency of the IR-SPC framework. Extensive experimental results show that our proposed method achieves highly competitive performance in quantitative comparisons of perceptual metrics, setting a new state-of-the-art perception metrics on restoration tasks.

Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) plays a critical role in maritime surveillance, yet deep learning for SAR analysis is limited by the lack of pixel-level annotations. This paper explores how general-purpose vision foundation models can enable zero-shot ship instance segmentation in SAR imagery, eliminating the need for pixel-level supervision. A YOLOv11-based detector trained on open SAR datasets localizes ships via bounding boxes, which then prompt the Segment Anything Model 2 (SAM2) to produce instance masks without any mask annotations. Unlike prior SAM-based SAR approaches that rely on fine tuning or adapters, our method demonstrates that spatial constraints from a SAR-trained detector alone can effectively regularize foundation model predictions. This design partially mitigates the optical-SAR domain gap and enables downstream applications such as vessel classification, size estimation, and wake analysis. Experiments on the SSDD benchmark achieve a mean IoU of 0.637 (89% of a fully supervised baseline) with an overall ship detection rate of 89.2%, confirming a scalable, annotation-efficient pathway toward foundation-model-driven SAR image understanding.
