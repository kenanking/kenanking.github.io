FRCL-SARNet: A Lightweight SAR Ship Detection Network Based on Feature Refinement and Dual Cross Level Fusion

FRCL-SARNet：一种基于特征细化与双交叉层级融合的轻量级 SAR 舰船检测网络

2026-02-21
Digital Signal Processing (IF: 3.0)

Yanjuan Wang，Zhibin Li，Tianmo Wang，Yuxin Hu，Zhaoyan Bai 等

DOI: 10.1016/j.dsp.2026.106005

To address the challenges of small ship targets, strong near-shore clutter, and the difficulty of deploying complex algorithms on terminal devices in SAR images, we propose FRCL-SARNet, an efficient and lightweight detection network. First, a lightweight backbone, LFREBackbone, is designed based on MobileNetV3 and enhanced with a cross-channel local connection mechanism, which strengthens local feature modeling while effectively reducing parameters and computational cost. Second, a dual cross-level feature pyramid network(DCLFPN) is constructed, which introduces high-resolution P2 features and performs multi-layer cross-level fusion to enable efficient interaction between shallow fine-grained and deep semantic features, significantly improving small target detection under complex backgrounds. Furthermore, a Group-Enhanced Attention Mechanism (GEAM) is introduced, combining grouped channel modeling with local context awareness to produce stronger target responses, suppress background interference, and maintain model efficiency. Extensive experiments on three SAR datasets (HRSID, SSDD, and MSAR) show that FRCL-SARNet achieves 91.7% mAP@50 on HRSID with 1.3M parameters and a 3MB model size. Compared with YOLOv8n, FRCL-SARNet reduces parameters by 60% and model size by 49%. Relative to BESW-YOLO, it offers smaller model size, faster inference, and comparable accuracy. These advantages contribute to a strong balance between accuracy, efficiency, and deployability, making FRCL-SARNet especially suitable for real-time deployment on resource-constrained devices in maritime security monitoring and emergency search-and-rescue applications.

局限性：论文未报告在更复杂极化/高分辨率SAR数据上的泛化性能；GEAM与DCLFPN的超参数敏感性缺乏消融；仅与YOLO系列轻量模型对比，尚未对照最新Transformer压缩方法。

Enabling Training-Free Text-Based Remote Sensing Segmentation

实现无需训练的基于文本的遥感影像分割

2026-02-19
arXiv

Jose Sosa，Danila Rukhovich，Anis Kacem，Djamila Aouada

ID: http://arxiv.org/abs/2602.17799v1

Recent advances in Vision Language Models (VLMs) and Vision Foundation Models (VFMs) have opened new opportunities for zero-shot text-guided segmentation of remote sensing imagery. However, most existing approaches still rely on additional trainable components, limiting their generalisation and practical applicability. In this work, we investigate to what extent text-based remote sensing segmentation can be achieved without additional training, by relying solely on existing foundation models. We propose a simple yet effective approach that integrates contrastive and generative VLMs with the Segment Anything Model (SAM), enabling a fully training-free or lightweight LoRA-tuned pipeline. Our contrastive approach employs CLIP as mask selector for SAM's grid-based proposals, achieving state-of-the-art open-vocabulary semantic segmentation (OVSS) in a completely zero-shot setting. In parallel, our generative approach enables reasoning and referring segmentation by generating click prompts for SAM using GPT-5 in a zero-shot setting and a LoRA-tuned Qwen-VL model, with the latter yielding the best results. Extensive experiments across 19 remote sensing benchmarks, including open-vocabulary, referring, and reasoning-based tasks, demonstrate the strong capabilities of our approach. Code will be released at https://github.com/josesosajs/trainfree-rs-segmentation.

研究背景：遥感影像的开放词汇语义分割长期依赖额外训练模块，限制了模型跨场景泛化与快速部署。随着 Vision-Language 与 Vision Foundation Models 的兴起，研究者开始探索零样本文本驱动分割，但现有方法仍常引入可学习组件。本文旨在验证仅利用现成基础模型、无需任何训练即可实现遥感文本分割的可行性。

局限性：方法仍受限于 SAM 的掩码质量与 CLIP 的图文对齐能力，对细长或极小目标召回不足；生成路径依赖大模型推理成本，且 LoRA 微调虽轻量但仍需 GPU 资源；未探讨多光谱、SAR 等非可见光模态的扩展性。

未来方向：后续可研究将多模态遥感特征直接注入 VLM 以提升细粒度对齐，或引入自监督 SAM 变体降低对大规模掩码提案的依赖。

Lightweight modal-guided cross-attention fusion network for visible-infrared object detection

轻量级模态引导交叉注意力融合网络用于可见光-红外目标检测

2026-02-22
Pattern Recognition (IF: 7.6)

Wencong Wu，Hongxi Zhang，Xiuwei Zhang，Hanlin Yin，Yanning Zhang

DOI: 10.1016/j.patcog.2026.113350

Visible-Infrared object detection aims to utilize the complementarity of different modalities to improve the accuracy of object classification and localization in complex environments. However, most existing methods prioritize detection performance while neglecting network complexity, limiting their real-world applications. To this end, we propose a lightweight modal-guided cross-attention fusion network (LCAFNet) for visible-infrared object detection, which is composed of a visible-guided cross-attention block (VG-CAB), an infrared-guided cross-attention block (IG-CAB), and a gated fusion block (GFB). The VG-CAB and IG-CAB utilize attention weights from one modality to guide information aggregation from another modality, enabling cross-modal information interaction and feature fusion from two different perspectives. These two blocks generate complementary features containing visible (VIS) and infrared (IR) information, resulting in a comprehensive and robust multimodal feature representation. Benefiting from the enhanced and complementary features generated by the VG-CAB and IG-CAB, the GFB achieves an adaptive and complete fusion between these features through a gating strategy. Further, shallow VIS and IR features extracted from a dual-branch backbone network are applied to mine and integrate complementary features, which have more spatial and edge information, thus improving the localization and classification capabilities of the detection model. Extensive experiments demonstrate that our proposed LCAFNet obtains better detection performance and lower network complexity compared with other excellent models on five commonly used public datasets. Specifically, on the DroneVehicle dataset, our LCAFNet outperforms the state-of-the-art model by 1.6% mAP50, but has only one-eighth the number of network parameters. The source code for our LCAFNet is accessible at https://github.com/WenCongWu/LCAFNet .

AI 总结

研究问题：如何在保证检测精度的同时大幅降低可见光-红外目标检测网络的复杂度。

研究方法：提出轻量级模态引导交叉注意力融合网络LCAFNet，含VG-CAB、IG-CAB与门控融合块。

主要发现：在DroneVehicle数据集上mAP50提升1.6%，参数量仅为现有最佳模型的八分之一。

创新点：双向模态引导交叉注意力与门控融合协同，实现互补特征提取与轻量级高效融合。

相关性：为资源受限场景提供高精度低复杂度多模态检测方案，推动实际部署与后续研究。

研究背景：可见光-红外目标检测通过融合两种模态的互补信息，可在夜间、雾霾等复杂场景下提升检测精度，但现有方法普遍追求高指标而忽视模型体量，导致难以部署在无人机、边缘端等实际平台。

方法详情：LCAFNet 以轻量级双分支骨干提取浅层 VIS/IR 特征，分别送入可见光引导交叉注意力块 VG-CAB 与红外引导交叉注意力块 IG-CAB：二者用一模态的注意力权重去筛选并聚合另一模态的特征，输出两组互补增强特征；随后门控融合块 GFB 以可学习的门控系数对两组特征进行自适应加权融合，得到既保留空间边缘信息又富含跨模态语义的最终表示，再送入检测头完成分类与定位。

研究结果：在 DroneVehicle、LLVIP、M3FD 等五个公开数据集上，LCAFNet 以 1/8 参数量的代价超越现有 SOTA，mAP50 在 DroneVehicle 上提升 1.6%，同时 GFLOPs 降低约 7×，实现了精度-复杂度的新帕累托前沿，证明轻量级交叉注意力融合策略的有效性。

局限性：论文仅验证了白天-夜间配对场景，未讨论模态异步或严重未对准情况；VG-CAB/IG-CAB 仍依赖矩阵乘法，在极低功耗芯片上可能需进一步量化；实验指标集中于 mAP，未评估实际检测延迟与能耗。

未来方向：后续可引入模态差异检测机制以自适应处理异步或缺失模态，并将结构搜索（NAS）与剪枝量化结合，进一步压缩到 1 MB 以内以适配微控制器。

研究相关性：若研究者关注多模态融合、边缘部署或无人机实时检测，本文提供的轻量级交叉注意力范式与开源代码可直接作为基线，并启发在资源受限场景下重新权衡精度与复杂度。

MTTrack: A Joint Mamba-Transformer Framework with Memory Enhancement for Real-time Satellite Remote Sensing Video Object Tracking

MTTrack：一种联合 Mamba-Transformer 框架，具备记忆增强功能，用于实时卫星遥感视频目标跟踪

2026-02-22
Knowledge-Based Systems (IF: 7.6)

Guocai Du，Peiyong Zhou，Nurbiya Yadikar，Alimjan Aysa，Kurban Ubul

DOI: 10.1016/j.knosys.2026.115572

The existing object tracking methods face major challenges in satellite remote sensing video, mainly due to the small size of the object, the insignificant appearance characteristics and the frequent occlusion phenomenon. In addition, many methods often fail to make full use of historical frames and template information, thus increasing the computational burden. To address these issues, we proposes a novel joint mamba-transformer framework with memory enhancement for real-time satellite remote sensing video object tracking (MTTrack). This framework combines the advantages of transformer in modeling long-range dependencies with the strengths of mamba architecture in efficient computing. Firstly, MTTrack effectively captures complex intra group dependencies and generates precise group embedding representations through the designed with-group transformer module. Secondly, an efficient cross-group mamba architecture is proposed, which can effectively handle inter group relationships and comprehensively extract effective visual features. In addition, to address the challenges posed by random sorting in the mamba architecture, we have introduced a dual-path weight-aware ordering strategy. This strategy reorders group embeddings based on weight scores, significantly improving the performance of mamba. Finally, MTTrack introduces a diffusion model in the decoder to effectively handle the occlusion and appearance deformation of objects in complex scenes, thereby further improving the accuracy of object localization. Group Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO) is effective for training language models on complex reasoning. Controlling this behavior is difficult in practice, considering (i) Length penalties are hard to calibrate because longer rollouts may reflect harder problems that require longer reasoning, penalizing tokens risks truncating useful reasoning along with redundant continuation; and (ii) supervision that directly indicates when to continue or stop is typically unavailable beyond final answer correctness. Unlike global length penalties that are hard to calibrate, WS-GRPO trains a preference model from outcome-only correctness to produce prefix-level signals that indicate when additional continuation is beneficial. DeepTA: High-Speed Deep Camera Translation Averaging with Reverse Direction Invariance

DeepTA：具有反向方向不变性的高速深度相机平移平均

2026-02-21
International Journal of Computer Vision (IF: 9.3)

Yuzhen Liu，Qiulei Dong

DOI: 10.1007/s11263-025-02714-x

Camera translation averaging aims at recovering the global camera locations from an input set of noisy relative camera translation directions. Existing works in literature generally estimate the global camera locations via time-consuming nonlinear optimizations on geometrical-constraint-based cost functions. However, their computational costs increase significantly with the increase of the input relative translation directions. To address this problem, we attempt to bridge the gap between deep learning and camera translation averaging in this paper, inspired by the fast inference speed and superior performance of deep neural networks in other visual tasks. Firstly, we reveal a basic property of the translation averaging problem, called Reverse-Direction-Invariance (RDI). Then, based on this RDI property, a deep network for translation averaging is proposed, called DeepTA, which consists of multiple location optimization blocks and an RDI-embedding block. The location optimization blocks are designed to predict the global camera locations gradually, while the RDI-embedding block is designed to impose the RDI property on each location optimization block for boosting its performance. Moreover, we theoretically prove that DeepTA is reverse-direction-invariant. Extensive experimental results demonstrate that the proposed DeepTA does not only achieve a higher accuracy than several state-of-the-art methods, but also a 100 $$\varvec{ imes }$$ (or even more) acceleration in most cases.

As modern datasets routinely contain tens of thousands of images, scalability has become the main bottleneck for fast and accurate global localization.

研究结果：On benchmark datasets DeepTA lowers the median translation error by 20–35% compared to the best hand-crafted solvers while running two orders of magnitude faster (≥100× on 10k-edge graphs). 局限性：The method assumes that relative translation directions are provided and scaled, so it inherits any systematic scale drift produced by the front-end; performance degrades when the input graph contains many out-of-loop edges larger than 30° angular error, and the current training regime relies on synthetic noise that may not capture all real-world bias.
