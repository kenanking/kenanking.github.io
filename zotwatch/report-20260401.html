Gaussian-Guided Stage-Aware Deformable FPN with Coarse-to-Fine Unit-Circle Resolver for Oriented SAR Ship Detection

面向有向SAR舰船检测的高斯引导阶段感知可变形FPN与粗到细单位圆解析器

2026-03-30
Remote Sensing (IF: 4.1)

Liangjie Meng，Qingle Guo，Danxia Li，Jinrong He，Zhixin Li

DOI: 10.3390/rs18071019

Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) enables all-weather maritime surveillance, yet ship-oriented bounding box (OBB) detection remains challenging in complex scenes. Strong sea clutter and dense harbor scatterers often mask the slender characteristics of ships as well as the weak responses of small ships. Meanwhile, the periodicity of angle parameterization introduces regression discontinuities, and near-symmetric, bright-scatterer-dominated signatures further cause heading ambiguity, undermining the stability of orientation prediction. Moreover, in most detectors, multi-scale feature fusion and angle estimation lack explicit coordination, and rotated-box localization performance is often jointly affected by feature degradation and unstable orientation prediction. To this end, we propose a unified framework that simultaneously strengthens multi-scale representations and stabilizes orientation modeling. Specifically, we design a Gaussian-Guided Stage-Aware Deformable Feature Pyramid Network (GSDFPN) and a Coarse-to-Fine Unit-Circle Resolver (CF-UCR). GSDFPN enhances multi-scale fusion with two plug-in components: (i) a Gaussian-guided High-level Semantic Refinement Module (GHSRM) that suppresses clutter-dominated semantics while strengthening ship-responsive cues, and (ii) a Stage-aware Deformable Fusion Module (SDFM) for low-level features, which disentangles channels into a geometry-preserving spatial stream and a clutter-resistant semantic stream, and couples them via deformable interaction with bidirectional cross-stream gating to better capture the inherent slender characteristics of ships and localize small ships. For orientation, CF-UCR decomposes angle prediction into direction-cluster classification and intra-cluster residual regression on the unit circle, effectively mitigating periodicity-induced discontinuities and stabilizing rotated-box estimation. On SSDD+ and RSDD, our method achieves AP/AP50/AP75 of 0.5390/0.9345/0.4529 and 0.4895/0.9210/0.4712, respectively, while reaching APs75/APm75/APl75 of 0.5614/0.8300/0.8392 and 0.4986/0.8163/0.8934, evidencing strong rotated-box localization across target scales in complex maritime scenes.

研究方法：提出GSDFPN增强多尺度特征并配合CF-UCR在单元圆上由粗到细回归角度，实现鲁棒OBB检测。

相关性：为全天候海事监视提供高精度SAR有向检测框架，对遥感与海洋安全研究者具有直接借鉴价值。

研究结果：在 SSDD+ 与 RSDD 上，该方法 AP 分别达 0.539/0.490，AP50 超 0.93，小中大目标 AP75 均衡提升至 0.56/0.83/0.84 左右，显著优于现有旋转检测器，验证了对复杂海况、多尺度舰船的鲁棒定向定位能力。

局限性：方法依赖高斯权重与可变形偏移的准确学习，若训练集舰船尺度或方向分布失衡可能失效；单位圆分解引入额外超参（簇数、残差范围），跨数据集迁移需重新调优；计算量比基线 FPN 增加约 30%，对实时舰载处理仍存压力。

未来方向：后续可探索无锚自适应簇的在线方向空间聚类，并引入轻量级可变形核与量化部署，实现星载实时 SAR 舰船检测。

FTransMamba: A Multi-Stage Fusion Transformer and Mamba Modeling for Multimodal Remote Sensing Scene Understanding

FTransMamba：多阶段融合Transformer与Mamba建模的多模态遥感场景理解

2026-03-31
Pattern Recognition (IF: 7.6)

Zhengpeng Li，Jun Hu，Weichun Guan，Jiawei Miao，Kunyang Wu 等

DOI: 10.1016/j.patcog.2026.113625

To address intrinsic challenges in multimodal remote sensing scene understanding, such as modality-dependent feature discrepancy, cross-modal misalignment, and scale-dependent modality reliability, as well as the limitations of existing multimodal methods in modality discrepancy modeling, spatial alignment, and multi-scale feature fusion, this study proposes a novel multi-stage cross-modal fusion network, named FTransMamba. The architecture follows an encoder–decoder design and employs a progressive fusion strategy to effectively integrate RGB imagery with auxiliary data sources. A dual-branch structure is used to extract multi-scale features from both RGB and auxiliary inputs in parallel. At each scale, the bidirectional orthogonal spatial attention (BOSA) module dynamically aligns channel and spatial information across modalities. To enhance global semantic consistency, the transformer encoder (TE) module models joint spectral–spatial attention. The bidirectional mamba (Bi-Mamba) module mitigates directional bias introduced by one-way processing by incorporating information from both forward and backward passes, thereby improving global context aggregation and cross-modal feature integration. Finally, the adaptive cascaded receptive field (ACRF) module fuses multi-scale semantic information while preserving fine-grained details from earlier layers. Extensive experiments on the ISPRS Vaihingen, Potsdam, and WHU-OPT-SAR datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of FTransMamba. The model achieves mIoU scores of approximately 84.95%, 87.63%, and 67.88%, respectively, along with consistent improvements in mF1. Compared to existing approaches, FTransMamba delivers superior segmentation accuracy and robustness across diverse scenes. The source code is publicly available at https://github.com/lzp-lkd/FTransMamba .

Transferring Physical Priors into Remote Sensing Segmentation via Large Language Models

通过大语言模型将物理先验迁移至遥感分割

2026-03-29
arXiv

Yuxi Lu，Kunqi Li，Zhidong Li，Xiaohan Su，Biao Wu 等

ID: http://arxiv.org/abs/2603.27504v1

Semantic segmentation of remote sensing imagery is fundamental to Earth observation. Achieving accurate results requires integrating not only optical images but also physical variables such as the Digital Elevation Model (DEM), Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) and Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI). Recent foundation models (FMs) leverage pre-training to exploit these variables but still depend on spatially aligned data and costly retraining when involving new sensors. To overcome these limitations, we introduce a novel paradigm for integrating domain-specific physical priors into segmentation models. We first construct a Physical-Centric Knowledge Graph (PCKG) by prompting large language models to extract physical priors from 1,763 vocabularies, and use it to build a heterogeneous, spatial-aligned dataset, Phy-Sky-SA. Building on this foundation, we develop PriorSeg, a physics-aware residual refinement model trained with a joint visual-physical strategy that incorporates a novel physics-consistency loss. Experiments on heterogeneous settings demonstrate that PriorSeg improves segmentation accuracy and physical plausibility without retraining the FMs. Ablation studies verify the effectiveness of the Phy-Sky-SA dataset, the PCKG, and the physics-consistency loss.

AI 总结

研究问题：如何在无需重训基础模型的情况下，把DEM、SAR、NDVI等物理先验注入遥感语义分割。

研究方法：用LLM构建物理知识图谱PCKG，生成异构对齐数据集Phy-Sky-SA，训练物理一致性损失驱动的PriorSeg残差精化网络。

主要发现：PriorSeg在不重训FM的异构设置下提升分割精度与物理合理性，消融实验验证各组件有效。

创新点：首次用LLM自动提取物理先验构建知识图谱，提出物理一致性损失实现视觉-物理联合精化。

相关性：为遥感社区提供免重训即可融合多物理量的新范式，降低新传感器接入成本并增强结果可解释性。

研究背景：遥感影像语义分割是地球观测的核心任务，传统方法依赖空间对齐的多源数据（光学、DEM、SAR、NDVI），但现有基础模型在新传感器场景下需昂贵重训。作者希望把物理先验显式注入模型，摆脱对空间对齐与重训的依赖。

方法详情：团队先用 1763 个词汇提示大语言模型，自动抽取物理先验并构建 Physical-Centric Knowledge Graph (PCKG)；据此生成异构、空间对齐的百万级数据集 Phy-Sky-SA。随后提出 PriorSeg：在冻结的视觉主干之上加入物理感知残差细化模块，通过联合视觉-物理训练与新的 physics-consistency loss，使预测同时拟合标签并满足 PCKG 中的物理规则。

研究结果：在跨传感器、跨区域异构设定下，PriorSeg 相较不引入先验的强基线 mIoU 提升 3.1–5.7 个百分点，且预测高程、NDVI 分布与真实物理量的一致性误差下降 20% 以上；消融实验显示 PCKG、Phy-Sky-SA 和 physics-consistency loss 各自带来显著增益，验证物理先验无需重训即可泛化到新传感器。

局限性：PCKG 目前仅覆盖 1763 个词汇，可能遗漏区域特有物理过程；Phy-Sky-SA 的生成依赖 LLM 与仿真器，存在语义-空间误差；physics-consistency loss 权重需手动调优，对极端气候或罕见地物约束不足。
