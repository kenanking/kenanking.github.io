Sakana Fugu Technical Report

Sakana Fugu技术报告

2026-06-19
arXiv

Yujin Tang，Edoardo Cetin，Jinglue Xu，Qi Sun，Stefan Nielsen 等

ID: http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.21228v1

The capabilities of frontier Large Language Models (LLMs) continue to advance, with different providers increasingly specializing in distinct domains. This raises a natural next objective: how to combine the individual specializations of various LLMs into a collectively intelligent system. To this end, we report the development of Sakana Fugu, a family of orchestrator models that harness and amplify the capabilities of an LLM agent team. Fugu models are themselves language models trained to understand user queries and dynamically devise agentic scaffolds to solve them. Through these adaptive scaffolds, Fugu accesses performance beyond any individual LLM agent, achieving state-of-the-art results compared to other publicly accessible models across a range of challenging tasks, including SWE-Bench Pro, Terminal Bench, LiveCodeBench, GPQA-Diamond, Humanity's Last Exam, and CharXiv Reasoning. We release two models: Fugu, which balances performance with latency for everyday use, and Fugu-Ultra, which prioritizes answer quality on the hardest problems. We describe our training paradigm, which encompasses large-scale fine-tuning, evolutionary algorithms, and reinforcement learning approaches, along with the infrastructure and core design principles that turn these methods into a production system. We hope this report encourages further research into multi-agent systems and dynamic, query-adaptive agentic scaffolds as a path toward the next frontier of AI capabilities, accessed through collective intelligence.

Extraction and Analysis of Multimodal Concepts in Vision Language Models through Sparse Autoencoders

通过稀疏自编码器提取与分析视觉语言模型中的多模态概念

2026-06-19
arXiv

Sergio Lanza，Jae Hee Lee，Stefan Wermter

ID: http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.21197v1

Vision Language Models (VLMs) have demonstrated impressive performance in tasks requiring joint understanding of images and text, such as image captioning and Visual Question Answering (VQA), but our understanding of their internal processes remains limited. Recently, Sparse Autoencoders (SAEs) have emerged as a promising tool to support the interpretation of concepts encoded in VLMs. However, most SAE-based approaches focus only on textual or visual concepts separately, ignoring multimodal concepts. This limitation hinders a comprehensive understanding of VLMs, since concepts that integrate both modalities can be misclassified. Moreover, previous visual approaches often produce low-quality visual concept descriptions that are vague or incomplete, limiting their usefulness for understanding model reasoning. We propose a framework based on SAEs to extract and analyze visual, textual, and multimodal concepts from VLMs. For each neuron, we propose a candidate human-interpretable concept and compute the alignment between the concept and the dataset samples using cosine similarity scores. Experiments on a VQA dataset (LLaVA-NeXT) demonstrate that our framework improves visual concept quality by up to 45\% compared to existing SAE-based methods, while maintaining high textual concept quality and enabling systematic identification of multimodal concepts. This work contributes new insights into the conceptual space of VLMs, providing a structured approach to distinguish between visual, textual, and multimodal concepts. The code is available at https://github.com/PHDLanza/Multidata_SAE

研究方法：提出基于稀疏自编码器的框架，为每个神经元生成可解释概念，并用余弦相似度对齐概念与样本。

主要发现：视觉概念质量提升45%，同时保持高文本概念质量，并系统识别多模态概念。

创新点：首次将稀疏自编码器用于联合提取和分析视觉语言模型中的多模态概念。

相关性：为理解视觉语言模型内部概念空间提供结构化方法，促进模型可解释性研究。

研究背景：Vision Language Models (VLMs) perform well on multimodal tasks, but their internal reasoning is poorly understood. Sparse Autoencoders (SAEs) have emerged as interpretability tools, yet existing SAE-based methods mainly focus on unimodal concepts, neglecting multimodal ones, and produce low-quality visual concept descriptions.

方法详情：The authors propose a framework using SAEs to extract visual, textual, and multimodal concepts from VLMs. For each neuron, a candidate human-interpretable concept is proposed, and alignment with dataset samples is computed via cosine similarity scores. Experiments are conducted on the LLaVA-NeXT VQA dataset.

研究结果：The framework improves visual concept quality by up to 45% compared to prior SAE-based methods, while maintaining high textual concept quality. 局限性：The approach relies on cosine similarity for concept alignment, which may not capture nuanced semantic relationships. The quality of extracted concepts depends on the SAE training and may not generalize to other VLM architectures or datasets without retuning.

未来方向：Future work could refine concept alignment with more sophisticated metrics, extend the framework to other VLM architectures, or integrate causal analysis to better understand how multimodal concepts influence model decisions.

研究相关性：This paper is highly relevant to researchers studying VLM interpretability, multimodal concept extraction, and sparse autoencoders, as it provides a novel method to disentangle and evaluate multimodal concepts, leading to deeper insights into model reasoning.

Lightweight 3D Feature Pretraining by Bayesian Inversion of 2D Foundation Models

2026-06-19
arXiv

Marwane Hariat，Gianni Franchi，David Filliat，Antoine Manzanera

ID: http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.21292v1

We present Casper3D, a lightweight probabilistic framework for converting noisy multi-view 2D foundation-model embeddings into a latent 3D semantic representation. We model view-level semantic features as noisy observations of an underlying 3D semantic state and infer this state with a set-based variational model that incorporates relative pose during multi-view reasoning. Casper3D is trained by predicting held-out semantic observations from novel viewpoints, while remaining aligned with visual and text semantic spaces for open-vocabulary 3D understanding. The framework is backbone-agnostic and applies to both language-aligned and self-supervised embeddings. Experiments show that Casper3D produces more stable 3D semantics than simple multi-view pooling, especially in ambiguous and noisy settings.

AI 总结

研究问题：如何从噪声多视图2D特征中提取稳定的3D语义表示？

研究方法：采用贝叶斯反演和集合变分模型，融合相对姿态进行3D语义推断。

主要发现：Casper3D产生更稳定的3D语义，优于简单多视图池化。

创新点：提出轻量级概率框架，利用2D基础模型嵌入进行3D特征预训练。

相关性：对3D场景理解、开放词汇3D感知等领域有重要价值。

研究背景：现有的3D特征预训练通常依赖大规模3D数据或复杂的3D网络，而2D基础模型虽强大但缺乏3D一致性。本研究旨在利用多视图2D嵌入，通过轻量级概率框架推断3D语义表示，避免高昂的3D数据标注和训练成本。

方法详情：Casper3D将每个视图的语义特征视为对底层3D语义状态的噪声观测，采用基于集合的变分模型（如Set Transformer）结合相对位姿进行多视图推理。训练时通过预测新视角的保留观测来学习，同时与视觉-文本语义空间（如CLIP）对齐，支持开放词汇理解。该框架与骨干网络无关，适用于语言对齐和自监督嵌入。

研究结果：实验表明，Casper3D在模糊和噪声场景下比简单多视图池化（如平均或最大池化）产生更稳定、更准确的3D语义表示。意义在于提供了一种轻量级、无需3D预训练的替代方案，有效弥合2D基础模型与3D理解之间的鸿沟。

局限性：该方法依赖多视图输入和已知相对位姿，单视图或位姿不准时性能可能下降；变分模型可能难以捕捉复杂3D结构；目前仅在受限数据集上验证，真实大规模3D场景的泛化性未知。

未来方向：未来可扩展至动态场景或点云数据，并探索与其他2D基础模型（如DINO、SAM）的结合，以丰富语义层次。

研究相关性：对于从事3D视觉、多视图学习、开放词汇3D理解以及利用预训练2D模型的研究者，该论文提供了高效、轻量级的框架，具有重要的参考价值和实用潜力。

MIRCaps: A Large-Scale Mixed-Domain Dataset with Image-Level and Region-Level Captions for Fine-Grained Vision-Language Learning

2026-06-19
arXiv

Arlindo Luciano Tulumba Roberto，Hyungjoon Kim

ID: http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.21419v1

Despite recent progress in Vision-Language Models (VLMs), mixed-domain image-caption datasets for both general-purpose and CCTV-based video surveillance systems remain limited. To address this gap, we introduce a large-scale multimodal dataset comprising 141,364 images, 981,947 image-level captions, 1,742,264 region-level captions, and 1,391,779 bounding box annotations. Each image is associated with an average of seven image-level captions describing different aspects of the overall scene, as well as seven region-level captions for each annotated bounding box. These complementary caption types are designed to help VLMs learn fine-grained visual attributes, including object categories, estimated sizes, colors, actions, states, and surrounding environmental context. We demonstrate the effectiveness of the dataset on two important downstream tasks: image captioning and object detection. Experimental results show that lightweight VLMs, including SmolVLM-256M-Instruct, BLIP, BLIP2, and Qwen2.5-VL 3B-Instruct, can be effectively fine-tuned using our dataset. Our dataset and code are publicly available at https://zenodo.org/records/20418601.

AI 总结

研究问题：混合领域图像-标题数据集不足，限制了视觉语言模型在通用和监控场景中的细粒度学习。

研究方法：构建包含14万图像、98万图像级和174万区域级标题的大规模混合域数据集。

主要发现：轻量级VLM（如SmolVLM-256M）可在该数据集上有效微调，提升图像描述和物体检测性能。

创新点：提供互补的图像级和区域级标题，涵盖细粒度属性（大小、颜色、动作、环境等）。
