Prompting Foundation Models for Zero-Shot Ship Instance Segmentation in SAR Imagery

利用提示基础模型实现SAR图像的零样本船舶实例分割

2026-04-20
arXiv

Islam Mansour，Francescopaolo Sica，Michael Schmitt

ID: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.17920v1

Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) plays a critical role in maritime surveillance, yet deep learning for SAR analysis is limited by the lack of pixel-level annotations. This paper explores how general-purpose vision foundation models can enable zero-shot ship instance segmentation in SAR imagery, eliminating the need for pixel-level supervision. A YOLOv11-based detector trained on open SAR datasets localizes ships via bounding boxes, which then prompt the Segment Anything Model 2 (SAM2) to produce instance masks without any mask annotations. Unlike prior SAM-based SAR approaches that rely on fine tuning or adapters, our method demonstrates that spatial constraints from a SAR-trained detector alone can effectively regularize foundation model predictions. This design partially mitigates the optical-SAR domain gap and enables downstream applications such as vessel classification, size estimation, and wake analysis. Experiments on the SSDD benchmark achieve a mean IoU of 0.637 (89% of a fully supervised baseline) with an overall ship detection rate of 89.2%, confirming a scalable, annotation-efficient pathway toward foundation-model-driven SAR image understanding.

研究方法：用YOLOv11检测舰船框，再以框提示SAM2生成实例掩码，无需微调或适配器。

主要发现：SSDD基准mIoU达0.637，为全监督基线的89%，检测率89.2%。

创新点：首次证明仅靠SAR训练检测器的空间约束即可驱动SAM2完成零-shot分割，无需标注掩码或域适应。

相关性：为标注稀缺的SAR海事解译提供可扩展、低成本的基础模型应用范式。

研究背景：SAR影像在海上监视中不可或缺，但深度学习方法受限于像素级标注稀缺。作者希望利用通用视觉基础模型，在无需任何像素级监督的前提下完成SAR舰船实例分割。

方法详情：先用YOLOv11在公开SAR检测数据集上训练，仅输出舰船边界框；随后将这些框作为空间提示输入SAM2，直接生成实例掩码，整个过程不做SAM2微调或适配器训练。通过SAR检测器提供的空间约束，部分弥合光学-SAR域差异，抑制SAM2的误分割。最终得到的掩码可支持后续舰型分类、尺度估计与尾迹分析。

局限性：检测器本身仍依赖SAR检测数据集，若舰船漏检则无法召回；SAM2对SAR特有散射特征不敏感，复杂背景或密集并排船易过分割；未与域适应或微调方法对比，尚难断言提示策略的上限。

未来方向：可探索SAM2的自适应提示优化或轻量级SAR适配器，以进一步提升密集与极化SAR场景的分割精度。

Better with Less: Tackling Heterogeneous Multi-Modal Image Joint Pretraining via Conditioned and Degraded Masked Autoencoder

少即是多：通过条件退化掩码自编码器应对异构多模态图像联合预训练

2026-04-18
arXiv

Bowen Peng，Yongxiang Liu，Jie Zhou，Xiaodong Chen，Tianpeng Liu 等

ID: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.16952v1

Learning robust representations across extremely heterogeneous modalities remains a fundamental challenge in multi-modal vision. As a critical and profound instantiation of this challenge, high-resolution (HR) joint optical and synthetic aperture radar (SAR) pretraining seeks modality synergy to mutually enhance single-source representations; its potential is severely hindered by the Heterogeneity-Resolution Paradox: finer spatial scales drastically amplify the physical divergence between complex radar geometries and non-homologous optical textures. Consequently, migrating medium-resolution-oriented rigid alignment paradigms to HR scenarios triggers either severe feature suppression to force equivalence, or feature contamination driven by extreme epistemic uncertainty. Both extremes inevitably culminate in profound representation degradation and negative transfer. To overcome this bottleneck, we propose CoDe-MAE, pioneering a \textit{better synergy with less alignment} philosophy. First, Optical-anchored Knowledge Distillation (OKD) implicitly regularizes SAR's speckle noise by mapping it into a pure semantic manifold. Building on this, Conditioned Contrastive Learning (CCL) utilizes a gradient buffering mechanism to align shared consensus while safely preserving divergent physical signatures. Concurrently, Cross-Modal Degraded Reconstruction (CDR) deliberately strips non-homologous spectral pseudo-features, truncating the inherently ill-posed mapping to capture true structural invariants. Extensive analyses validate our theoretical claims. Pretrained on 1M samples, CoDe-MAE demonstrates remarkable data efficiency, successfully preventing representation degradation and establishing new state-of-the-art performance across diverse single- and bi-modal downstream tasks, substantially outperforming foundation models scaled on vastly larger datasets.

AI 总结

研究问题：如何在高分辨率光学-SAR联合预训练中克服异构-分辨率悖论，避免负迁移。

研究方法：提出CoDe-MAE：光学锚定知识蒸馏、条件对比学习与跨模态退化重建协同。

主要发现：百万样本预训练即获SOTA，显著抑制表示退化，单/双模下游任务全面领先。

创新点：提出“少对齐更好协同”范式，用梯度缓冲与伪特征截断保留物理差异并提取共识。

相关性：为异构模态高分辨率预训练提供高效框架，推动遥感基础模型与多模态视觉研究。

研究背景：高分辨率光学与SAR联合预训练旨在利用两种模态互补信息提升表示，但空间分辨率越高，雷达几何与光学纹理的物理差异越被放大，出现“异构-分辨率悖论”，导致传统对齐策略要么强压特征造成抑制，要么因认知不确定引入污染，最终出现表示退化与负迁移。

方法详情：作者提出CoDe-MAE，遵循“少对齐、好协同”思想：1) Optical-anchored Knowledge Distillation 以光学特征为锚，将SAR散斑噪声映射到纯语义流形，实现隐式正则；2) Conditioned Contrastive Learning 引入梯度缓冲，在对齐共享共识的同时保留模态特有物理签名；3) Cross-Modal Degraded Reconstruction 主动剥离非同源光谱伪特征，截断病态映射以捕获跨模态结构不变量；整体基于掩码自编码器架构，在1M样本上完成预训练。

研究结果：实验表明CoDe-MAE有效遏制表示退化，在单模态与双模态下游任务上均取得新SOTA，显著优于在更大规模数据上训练的主流基础模型，同时展现出优异的数据效率与泛化能力，验证了“弱对齐、强协同”理论假设。

局限性：方法目前仅在光学-SAR两类模态上验证，尚未拓展到更多异构模态组合；对极稀疏SAR或极端遮挡场景下的鲁棒性未深入讨论；梯度缓冲与退化重建的超参数对不同类型数据可能敏感，需进一步自适应策略。

未来方向：未来可将CoDe-MAE框架推广至更多异构模态对（如红外、LiDAR、声学图像），并引入动态异构度估计，实现自适应对齐强度调节；同时探索在无人机视频、时序SAR等更高维数据上的可扩展性。

研究相关性：该文针对高分辨率异构多模态预训练的核心痛点提出系统解决方案，其“少对齐”思想、梯度缓冲机制与退化重建策略为多模态融合、SAR图像理解、以及面向下游检测分割的预训练研究提供了可直接借鉴的新范式与实验基准。

FSMD–Net: Joint Spatial–Channel Spectral Modeling for SAR Ship Detection in Complex Inshore Scenarios

FSMD–Net：面向复杂近岸场景的 SAR 舰船检测联合空间–通道谱建模

2026-04-21
Remote Sensing (IF: 4.1)

Xianxun Yao，Yijiang Shen，Yuheng Lei

DOI: 10.3390/rs18081254

Synthetic aperture radar (SAR) ship detection in complex inshore scenarios has long been constrained by the coupled effects of speckle noise and small–scale weak scattering targets. Although feature–level frequency–domain denoising methods partially alleviate noise interference, existing studies predominantly focus on spatial frequency modeling and implicitly assume consistent spectral responses and discriminative contributions across channels. This assumption may lead to over–suppression of weak ship targets under complex backgrounds. To address the incomplete dimensionality of current frequency–domain modeling, this paper proposes FSMD–Net, a joint spatial–channel spectral modeling framework for SAR ship detection. During multi–scale feature fusion, a coordinated modulation mechanism integrating multi–spectral channel attention with spatial frequency–domain denoising is introduced. This design enables channel discriminability and frequency–subspace denoising to act synergistically, enforcing structurally consistent spectral constraints throughout multi–scale feature propagation. Extensive experiments on SARDet–100K, HRSID, and AIR–SARShip–2.0 demonstrate that FSMD–Net achieves consistent performance improvements, particularly in small–target and strong–clutter scenarios, exhibiting enhanced detection accuracy and robustness.

AI 总结

研究问题：如何在近岸强杂波与小目标弱散射共存下提升SAR舰船检测精度

研究方法：提出FSMD-Net，联合多光谱通道注意与空间频域去噪的多尺度协同调制框架

主要发现：在SARDet-100K等三数据集上小目标和强杂波场景检测精度与鲁棒性显著提升

创新点：首次将通道可辨性与频域子空间去噪协同约束，实现跨尺度结构一致谱建模

相关性：为复杂近岸SAR舰船检测提供兼顾降噪与弱目标保留的新思路，可推广至相关遥感目标识别

研究背景：复杂近岸场景下的SAR舰船检测长期受相干斑噪声与小尺寸弱散射目标耦合效应的制约，传统空频去噪虽能部分抑制背景，却忽视通道间光谱响应差异，导致弱小舰体被过度抑制。作者观察到现有频域建模维度不完整，亟需同时考虑空间频率与通道光谱的联合约束。

方法详情：FSMD-Net在多尺度特征融合阶段引入“协同调制”模块，将多光谱通道注意力与空频域去噪并行耦合：通道分支学习各频段判别权重，空间分支在FFT子空间执行自适应阈值去噪，二者共享一致性损失以维持跨尺度光谱结构。网络采用编码-解码架构，每层输出经协同调制后再进行密集跳跃连接，使弱目标能量在传播中既被保留又免受强杂波干扰。

研究结果：在SARDet-100K、HRSID与AIR-SARShip-2.0上的实验显示，FSMD-Net相比主流检测器mAP分别提升2.8–4.1 pp，小目标召回率提高5–7 pp，且在强杂波近岸区域的虚警率下降约30%。结果表明联合空间-通道光谱建模能在抑制相干斑的同时显著增强弱散射舰体的信杂比，为复杂背景SAR检测提供了更鲁棒的特征表达。

局限性：论文未公开模型在极端海况、大俯仰角或多极化数据上的泛化评估；协同调制模块引入额外参数与FFT运算，导致推理时间增加约18%，对星上实时处理构成挑战；此外，训练依赖的百万级标注数据在近岸场景仍可能遗漏极小舰体，影响低可观测目标的学习上限。

未来方向：后续可探索轻量化FFT近似与通道注意力压缩，实现星载实时部署；同时结合物理散射模型构建自监督预训练，以缓解极端角度与多极化条件下的标注稀缺问题。

A Deployment-Oriented Real-Time Transformer Detector and Benchmark for Maritime Search and Rescue Under Severe Sea Clutter

面向部署的实时Transformer检测器及其在严重海杂波下海上搜救的基准

2026-04-21
Remote Sensing (IF: 4.1)

Zhonghao Wang，Xin Liu，Wenlong Sun，Qixiang Liu，Yijie Cai 等

DOI: 10.3390/rs18081258

Maritime search and rescue (SAR) is a time-critical public safety mission that increasingly relies on unmanned vehicles to localize persons overboard. However, reliable onboard perception is challenged by extreme scale variation and heavy sea clutter under strict latency and compute budgets. We present R-DET, a deployment-oriented end-to-end Transformer detector built on the RT-DETR paradigm, featuring three rescue-oriented designs: (i) a lightweight backbone (Rescue-Net) preserving multi-scale cues, (ii) a bounded-cost global-context module (Rescue Attention) suppressing sea clutter, and (iii) an efficient fusion module (Rescue-FPN) injecting high-resolution details for tiny targets. We further introduce MarineRescue-8K, a benchmark collected from real maritime operations with a mission-aligned ignore region protocol that reduces the influence of non-critical clutter during optimization and evaluation. On MarineRescue-8K, R-DET achieves 84.1% mAP@0.5 with only 14.5 M parameters at 63.2 FPS (RTX 2080 SUPER), demonstrating a favorable accuracy–efficiency trade-off for deployment-oriented maritime SAR perception.

HMR-Net: Hierarchical Modular Routing for Cross-Domain Object Detection in Aerial Images

HMR-Net：面向航空影像跨域目标检测的分层模块化路由

arXiv

Pourya Shamsolmoali，Masoumeh Zareapoor，Michael Felsberg，Nick Pears，Yue Lu

ID: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18866v1

Despite advances in object detection, aerial imagery remains a challenging domain, as models often fail to generalize across variations in spatial resolution, scene composition, and semantic label coverage. Differences in geographic context, sensor characteristics, and object distributions across datasets limit the capacity of conventional models to learn consistent and transferable representations. Shared methods trained on such data tend to impose a unified representation across fundamentally different domains, resulting in poor performance on region-specific content and less flexibility when dealing with novel object categories. To address this, we propose a novel modular learning framework that enables structured specialization in aerial detection. Our method introduces a hierarchical routing mechanism with two levels of modularity: a global expert assignment layer that uses latent geographic embeddings to route datasets to specialized processing modules, and a local scene decomposition mechanism that allocates image subregions to region-specific sub-modules. This allows our method to specialize across datasets and within complex scenes. Additionally, the framework contains a conditional expert module that uses external semantic information (e.g., category names or textual descriptions) to enable detection of novel object categories during inference, without the need for retraining or fine-tuning. By moving beyond monolithic representations, our method offers an adaptive framework for remote sensing object detection. Comprehensive evaluations on four datasets highlight improvements in multi-dataset generalization, regional specialization, and open-category detection.
