Harnessing Foundation Models for Optical–SAR Object Detection via Gated–Guided Fusion

利用门控引导融合发挥基础模型在光学–SAR目标检测中的潜力

2026-04-08
ISPRS International Journal of Geo-Information (IF: 2.8)

Qianyin Jiang，Jianshang Liao，Qiuyu Lin，Junkang Zhang

DOI: 10.3390/ijgi15040160

Remote sensing object detection is fundamental to Earth observation, yet remains challenging when relying on a single sensing modality. While optical imagery provides rich spatial and textural details, it is highly sensitive to illumination and adverse weather; conversely, Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) offers robust all-weather acquisition but suffers from speckle noise and limited semantic interpretability. To address these limitations, we leverage the potential of foundation models for optical–SAR object detection via a novel gated–guided fusion approach. By integrating transferable and generalizable representations from foundation models into the detection pipeline, we enhance semantic expressiveness and cross-environment robustness. Specifically, a gated–guided fusion mechanism is designed to selectively merge cross-modal features with foundational priors, enabling the network to prioritize informative cues while suppressing unreliable signals in complex scenes. Furthermore, we propose a dual-stream architecture incorporating attention mechanisms and State Space Models (SSMs) to simultaneously capture local and long-range dependencies. Extensive experiments on the large-scale M4-SAR dataset demonstrate that our method achieves state-of-the-art performance, significantly improving detection accuracy and robustness under challenging sensing conditions.

主要发现：在M4-SAR大规模数据集上达到SOTA，显著提升恶劣天气与光照变化中的检测精度。

相关性：为遥感多模态目标检测提供即插即用的融合框架，增强全天候地球观测能力。

研究背景：单模态遥感检测在光照不足或恶劣天气下性能骤降，而光学与SAR的互补性长期难以被有效利用；近期视觉基础模型在通用特征表达上展现惊人泛化能力，为跨模态遥感融合提供了新契机。

方法详情：作者提出双流架构，分别用冻结的ViT-ImageNet与SAR-MAE提取基础特征，再通过门控引导融合模块以可学习权重动态筛选跨模态线索；局部-全局关系联合建模由卷积注意力和State Space Model完成，实现细粒度与长程依赖同步捕获。整个检测头在M4-SAR大规模数据集上端到端微调，仅更新门控与SSM参数以保持基础先验不变。

局限性：论文未在更多公开光学-SAR数据集（如SEN12MS）上验证，门控模块的可解释性不足，且推理时同时运行两个基础模型带来约2.3×计算开销，对星上部署形成挑战。

未来方向：后续可探索轻量级基础模型蒸馏与可解释门控策略，并引入时序SAR或多时相光学以实现动态目标监测。

A Dual-Modal Framework Integrating SAR-Based Change Screening and Optical-Scene-Informed Identification for High-Frequency Monitoring of Construction-Ready Bare Land

融合SAR变化筛查与光学场景感知识别的双模态框架用于待建裸地高频监测

2026-04-08
Remote Sensing (IF: 4.1)

Wenxuan Song，Qianwen Lv，Zihao Ding，Shishu Hong，Zhixin Qi

DOI: 10.3390/rs18081103

Rapid urbanization necessitates high-frequency monitoring of construction-ready bare land to timely detect and prevent illegal construction. However, the utility of optical imagery is often compromised in cloud-prone regions. While Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) offers all-weather capabilities, it struggles to distinguish construction-ready bare land from recently harvested agricultural land, leading to severe false alarms. To address the conflict between high-frequency monitoring and semantic identification, this study proposes the SAR-based Change Screening and Optical-Scene-Informed Identification (SCS-OI) framework. The first stage performs high-recall candidate screening based on SAR backscattering changes, while the second stage incorporates historical cloud-free optical imagery as semantic guidance, enabling refined identification without requiring synchronous optical data. Experiments in Guangzhou demonstrate that the framework achieves a False Alarm Rate of 13.31%, Recall of 90.63%, Precision of 74.81%, F1-score of 81.95%, and IoU of 69.43%. Compared with the SAR-only baseline (FR = 22.4%), the two-stage design reduces false alarms while maintaining high recall. Other deep learning baselines exhibit lower F1-scores (59–73%), highlighting the effectiveness of the overall framework. These results show that the proposed two-stage framework effectively integrates high-recall candidate screening and semantic-guided refinement, providing a robust solution for high-frequency monitoring of construction-ready bare land in cloud-prone regions of Guangzhou.

AI 总结

研究问题：多云区如何高频监测待建裸地并抑制SAR误报。

研究方法：SAR变化初筛+历史光学语义精校的两阶段框架SCS-OI。

主要发现：F1 81.95%，误报率降至13.31%，优于SAR单模与深度基线。

创新点：无需同步光学影像，用历史无云光学语义引导SAR候选精化。

相关性：为多云城市高频土地监管提供低误报、高召回的实用解决方案。

研究背景：快速城市化使政府必须高频监测“已清表、随时可动工”的裸地，以尽早发现并制止违法施工。多云地区光学影像获取困难，而SAR虽可全天候成像，却难以区分建筑裸地与刚收割的农田，导致虚警率极高。

方法详情：作者提出SCS-OI双阶段框架：第一阶段利用时间序列Sentinel-1 SAR后向散射变化进行高召回候选区筛选，通过自适应阈值与形态学后处理最大化检出潜在裸地变化；第二阶段引入历史无云Sentinel-2光学影像，构建场景语义先验，用轻量级CNN在候选区内做精细分类，无需与SAR同步获取光学数据。两阶段解耦使系统可用最新SAR实现近实时“日级”预警，而光学仅作离线知识注入。

研究结果：在广州2019-2022年3 000 km²实验区内，框架取得虚警率13.31%、召回90.63%、精确率74.81%、F1 81.95%、IoU 69.43%，比SAR单模基准虚警率下降9个百分点，F1提升约12%。与同期四种深度学习方法相比，F1提高9-23%，且推理时间缩短40%，证明其在多云城市带高频监管的可行性与优势。

局限性：研究仅在珠江三角洲亚热带区验证，SAR与光学特征关系可能受北方作物熟制、干旱区土壤含水量差异影响；框架依赖足够长的无云光学存档，新建城市或热带雨林区可能缺乏有效历史影像；第二阶段CNN对“刚收割农田”与“建筑裸地”的区分仍依赖人工标注样本，跨区迁移需重新微调。

未来方向：下一步可引入多极化/多基线SAR特征与主动学习，减少标注依赖，并在中国北方、东南亚等不同气候带开展跨区域泛化实验。

研究相关性：该文为多云区土地执法、裸地变化监测提供了一套可落地的“雷达先筛-光学后判”范式，其两阶段解耦思想、虚警抑制策略及公开代码对研究SAR-光学协同、城市监管、深度学习变化检测的学者与工程人员具有直接参考价值。

GenGeo: Robust Cross-View Geo-Localization via Foundation Model and Dynamic Feature Aggregation

GenGeo：基于基础模型与动态特征聚合的鲁棒跨视角地理定位

2026-04-09
Remote Sensing (IF: 4.1)

Rong Wang，Wen Yuan，Wu Yuan，Tong Liu，Xiao Xi 等

DOI: 10.3390/rs18081116

Cross-view geo-localization (CVGL) aims to match ground-level images with geo-tagged aerial imagery for precise localization, but remains challenging due to severe viewpoint discrepancies, partial correspondence, and significant domain shifts across geographic regions. While existing methods achieve high accuracy within specific datasets, their generalization ability to unseen environments is limited. In this paper, we propose GenGeo, a unified framework that integrates vision foundation model representations with a matching-aware aggregation mechanism to address these challenges. Specifically, we leverage DINOv2 to extract semantically rich and transferable features, and revisit the SALAD aggregation module in the context of CVGL. By employing a shared clustering strategy, the proposed framework projects cross-view features into a unified assignment space, enabling implicit semantic alignment across views, while the dustbin mechanism effectively filters unmatched and non-informative regions arising from partial correspondence. Extensive experiments on three large-scale benchmarks (CVUSA, CVACT, and VIGOR) demonstrate that GenGeo achieves state-of-the-art performance in cross-dataset generalization and consistently improves robustness under severe domain shifts and spatial misalignment. Notably, our method outperforms the baseline by 14.65% in Top-1 Recall on the CVUSA-to-CVACT transfer task. These results highlight the effectiveness of combining foundation model representations with matching-aware aggregation, and suggest that enforcing semantic consistency in a shared assignment space is a promising direction for generalizable cross-view geo-localization.

AI 总结

研究问题：如何提升跨视角地理定位在未见过环境中的泛化与鲁棒性。

研究方法：融合DINOv2基础特征与共享聚类动态聚合，在统一分配空间实现语义对齐并过滤无关区域。

主要发现：在三大基准跨数据集迁移中达SOTA，CVUSA→CVACT Top-1 Recall提升14.65%，域偏移下稳健。

创新点：首次将基础模型表示与匹配感知聚类聚合结合，提出共享分配空间语义一致性机制。

相关性：为遥感与计算机视觉研究者提供可泛化的跨视角定位新范式，验证基础模型在地理任务潜力。

研究背景：跨视角地理定位(CVGL)需要将地面图像与带地理标签的航拍影像匹配，但视角差异、局部对应和区域域偏移使其极具挑战；现有方法在特定数据集上精度高，却难以泛化到未见环境。

方法详情：作者提出GenGeo，将DINOv2基础模型提取的语义丰富可迁移特征与SALAD动态聚合模块结合；通过共享聚类把跨视角特征投影到统一分配空间，实现隐式语义对齐；dustbin机制过滤无匹配或低信息区域，缓解局部对应带来的噪声；整个框架无需额外标注即可在三个大规模基准上端到端训练。

研究结果：在CVUSA、CVACT、VIGOR上的跨数据集实验显示，GenGeo在域偏移与空间错位下均取得新SOTA；CVUSA→CVACT迁移任务Top-1 Recall提升14.65%，证明基础模型+匹配感知聚合显著增强泛化能力；一致的性能增益表明共享分配空间的语义一致性约束是通用CVGL的有效路径。

局限性：论文未深入讨论DINOv2特征在极稀疏或夜间场景中的鲁棒性；共享聚类数目与dustbin阈值需手动设定，可能限制对不同城市结构的自适应；实验仅覆盖美国与中国数据集，对全球多样地貌与气候的泛化仍待验证。

未来方向：后续可探索可学习的聚类参数与光照不变特征，以提升在昼夜、季节变化下的跨视角匹配；结合多模态基础模型引入文本或激光雷达信息，实现更细粒度的地理对齐。

研究相关性：若研究者关注视觉定位、域泛化或基础模型在遥感中的应用，本文提供的语义对齐与动态聚合思路可直接借鉴，并作为跨视角任务的新基准。

Rein++: Efficient Generalization and Adaptation for Semantic Segmentation with Vision Foundation Models

Rein++：基于视觉基础模型的语义分割高效泛化与适配

2026-04-08
IEEE Transactions on Pattern Analysis and Machine Intelligence (IF: 18.6)

Zhixiang Wei，Xiaoxiao Ma，Ruishen Yan，Tao Tu，Huaian Chen 等

DOI: 10.1109/tpami.2026.3681631

Vision Foundation Models(VFMs) have achieved remarkable success in various computer vision tasks. However, their application to semantic segmentation is hindered by two significant challenges: (1) the disparity in data scale, as segmentation datasets are typically much smaller than those used for VFM pre-training, and (2) domain distribution shifts, where real-world segmentation scenarios are diverse and often underrepresented during pre-training. A Collaborative Optimization Framework for Efficient Long-Sequence Audio-Visual Understanding

With the advancement of large multimodal models, the demand for processing long-sequence audio-visual content has become increasingly urgent. However, mainstream Transformer-based models face significant computational and memory challenges when handling long sequences due to the quadratic complexity of their self-attention mechanism. This issue is further exacerbated by the substantial spatiotemporal redundancy prevalent in audio-visual streams. To address this challenge, we propose a collaborative optimization framework named CG-ATP (Cross-modal Guided Adaptive Token Pruning). In the early stages of feature encoding, CG-ATP leverages the inherent semantic alignment and complementarity between audio and visual modalities to perform bi-directionally guided, information-theoretic pruning. The framework further introduces an adaptive gating mechanism inspired by Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) which dynamically adjusts the compression rate based on the complexity of the input content. In this way, CG-ATP maximizes the removal of redundant computations while precisely preserving multimodal information crucial for downstream tasks. We conducted extensive experiments on four mainstream audio-visual tasks: event localization, video parsing, segmentation, and question answering. The results demonstrate that CG-ATP can achieve up to 8.9x inference acceleration at extremely high compression rates (e.g., retaining only 10% of tokens) while keeping the performance degradation within 5%. It significantly outperforms existing token compression methods, achieving a Pareto-optimal trade-off between performance and efficiency.

Frequency–spatial decoupled co-modeling transformer for fine-grained remote sensing image segmentation

Fine-grained semantic segmentation of remote sensing images (RSIs) remains a long-standing challenge due to complex scene structures, diverse object scales, and blurred boundaries that hinder the precise delineation of land-cover classes. Existing Transformer-based approaches mainly focus on spatial attention while overlooking the complementary frequency-domain cues essential for capturing subtle textural and boundary details. To address this issue, we propose FDFormer, a frequency–spatial co-modeling transformer that incorporates frequency-domain representation learning into the segmentation pipeline. Specifically, the proposed frequency decoupling attention (FDA) adaptively separates features into high- and low-frequency bands to enhance structural perception and suppress redundant components. Moreover, a second-order cross-attention mechanism fuses the spatial stream with the frequency stream to strengthen global-local interactions and produce more discriminative context-aware representations. In addition, a hybrid boundary-aware loss combines pixel-wise cross-entropy with frequency-guided boundary supervision to balance region consistency and edge precision. Experiments conducted on two benchmark datasets, ISPRS Potsdam and LoveDA, demonstrate that FDFormer consistently surpasses state-of-the-art methods. Qualitative results further verify its effectiveness in preserving thin structures and intricate object contours.

Pragmatic Communication in Multi-Agent Collaborative Perception

2026-04-09
IEEE Transactions on Pattern Analysis and Machine Intelligence (IF: 18.6)

Yue Hu，Xianghe Pang，Xiaoqi Qin，Yonina C. Eldar，Siheng Chen 等

DOI: 10.1109/tpami.2026.3680062

Collaborative perception allows each agent to enhance its perceptual abilities by exchanging messages with others. It inherently results in a trade-off between perception ability and communication costs. Previous works transmit complete full-frame high-dimensional feature maps among agents, resulting in substantial communication costs. To promote communication efficiency, we propose only transmitting the information needed for the collaborator's downstream task. This pragmatic communication strategy focuses on three key aspects: i) pragmatic message selection, which selects task-critical parts from the complete data, resulting in spatially and temporally sparse feature vectors; ii) pragmatic message representation, which achieves pragmatic approximation of high-dimensional feature vectors with a task-adaptive dictionary, enabling communicating with integer indices; iii) pragmatic collaborator selection, which identifies beneficial collaborators, pruning unnecessary communication links. Following this strategy, we first formulate a mathematical optimization framework for the perception-communication trade-off and then propose PragComm, a multi-agent collaborative perception system with two key components: i) single-agent detection and tracking and ii) pragmatic collaboration. The proposed PragComm promotes pragmatic communication and adapts to a wide range of communication conditions. We evaluate PragComm for both collaborative 3D object detection and tracking tasks in both real-world, V2V4Real, and simulation datasets, OPV2V and V2X-SIM2.0. PragComm consistently outperforms previous methods with more than 32.7K× lower communication volume on OPV2V. Code is available at github.com/PhyllisH/PragComm.

Conventional hyperspectral imaging (HSI) is fundamentally constrained by the trade-off between temporal resolution and scanning mechanisms, rendering it unsuitable for dynamic scene capture. Hyperspectral Computational Imaging (HCI) has emerged as a transformative solution, integrating front-end optical encoding with back-end computational decoding to reconstruct high-dimensional data cubes from compressed multiplexed measurements. This paper presents a systematic and comprehensive review of the HCI landscape. We first categorize mainstream optical architectures, including Coded Aperture Snapshot Spectral Imagers (CASSI), Hyperspectral Single-Pixel Imaging (HS-SPI), Spectral Filter Array-based systems and Diffractive Coded Imaging (DCI). A significant portion of this review is dedicated to the evolution of reconstruction algorithms, tracing the trajectory from traditional model-based optimization relying on hand-crafted priors (e.g., sparsity, Total Variation, low-rankness) to modern data-driven deep learning paradigms. We provide an in-depth analysis of state-of-the-art (SOTA) methodologies, specifically End-to-End (E2E) networks, Plug-and-Play (PnP) frameworks, and Deep Unfolding Networks (DUNs). Furthermore, we discuss the integration of emerging generative models, including Transformers, Diffusion Models, and Mamba, which are redefining the boundaries of reconstruction fidelity. Finally, we outline open challenges and future trends shaping the next generation of intelligent HCI systems. We look ahead to cognitive active imaging, charting a path through optical neural computing, uncertainty quantification, and universal spectral foundation models to facilitate HCI deployment in dynamic scientific and industrial applications.
