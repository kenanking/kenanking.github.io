Multi-Dimensional Attention Autocorrelation-Based Small Sea-Surface Target Detection

基于多维注意力自相关的海面小目标检测

2026-06-04
IEEE Transactions on Aerospace and Electronic Systems (IF: 5.7)

Yuhang Zhu，Lu Shen，Hongtao Su，Ze Li，Ruixing Yang

DOI: 10.1109/taes.2026.3700123

The detection of small sea-surface targets remains challenging due to their low signal-to-clutter-plus-noise ratios (SCNR), and low Doppler frequencies. Conventional adaptive detectors fail to accurately model sea clutter, resulting in high false alarm rates and reduced detection probability. Feature-based methods avoid explicit clutter modeling but struggle to extract sufficiently discriminative features and generally require long observation durations. Although deep learning (DL) methods offer a promising alternative, most existing DL detectors rely on single-range-cell feature extraction, limiting their ability to distinguish weak targets from complex clutter backgrounds. In practice, sea clutter across adjacent range cells is typically independently and identically distributed (i.i.d.). Consequently, the autocorrelation features of a target-containing cell under test will exhibit distinct discrepancies compared with those of reference cells. Accordingly, we propose a detection method that leverages both the temporal correlation of the echo signal within each range cell and the similarity of their autocorrelation information between the cell under test and reference cells. While standard Transformers are adept at sequence processing, their inherent architecture cannot explicitly model such intra-cell autocorrelation. To realize the proposed mechanism, we introduce a neural network framework that employs a Multi-Dimensional Attention Autocorrelation (MDAttAC) module for explicit feature extraction. Subsequently, a standard Transformer is cascaded to evaluate the spatial similarity of these robust features across range cells. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method significantly enhances detection performance, reduces reliance on long observation durations, and exhibits superior generalization capability.

Cross-Modality Feature Fusion Based on Structured State Space Duality for Multimodal Image Registration Network

基于结构化状态空间对偶性的跨模态特征融合用于多模态图像配准网络

2026-06-02
arXiv

Zhikang Li，Yan Wu，Xin Hu，Yi Dai，Ming Li

ID: http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.03341v1

In multi-modal image registration, the primary challenge lies in shared structural information extraction. Compared to Transformers, Structured State Space Duality (SSD) offers greater global structural feature extraction with higher efficiency during training and inference. Inspired by these advantages, we propose a novel algorithm for multi-modal image registration, named RegNetMamba-2. Our algorithm incorporates SSD into coarse-to-fine matching process to extract local and global structural features effectively. Firstly, SSD is applied in three different scales for multi-modal feature extraction in our network. To strengthen local representation, we pay more attention on foreground edge and structural information by feature scaling function of SSD. Secondly, for shared feature extraction of input images and multi-modal feature fusion in all scales, we propose cross-modality feature fusion model based on SSD, consisting of Cross-Modality feature Interaction (CMI) module and Multi-Scale feature Fusion (MSF) module. CMI module is designed for cross-modality feature extraction of each scale by SSD in cross form. MSF module is designed to employ a progressive upward fusion in feature-level to obtain fine features, consisting of multi-modal features in all scales. Following coarse-to-fine, the features in 1/8 scale from CMI and 1/2 scale from MSF are collected to calculate matching probability scores. Then we respectively establish matching process by correspondences of pixel-wise. Extensive experiments demonstrate that comparing with state-of-the-art deep-learning based algorithms, RegNetMamba-2 has achieved good effects in both performance and efficiency for multi-modal image registration on the following datasets: VIS-SAR (OSDataset), VIS-IR (LGHD/RoadSence) and VIS-NIR (RGB-NIR sense).

研究方法：基于结构化状态空间对偶性（SSD）的跨模态特征融合网络RegNetMamba-2。

主要发现：在多个多模态数据集上，性能和效率均优于当前最先进的深度学习方法。

创新点：首次将SSD引入多模态配准，设计跨模态交互与多尺度融合模块实现粗到细匹配。

相关性：为多模态图像配准提供了高效的结构特征提取新范式，推动遥感、医学等应用发展。

研究背景：多模态图像配准的核心挑战在于提取共享的结构信息。现有的方法如Transformers虽然能提取全局特征，但训练和推理效率较低。受结构化状态空间对偶（SSD）的启发，本文提出了一种高效且性能优越的配准算法RegNetMamba-2。

方法详情：RegNetMamba-2将SSD集成到从粗到细的匹配过程中，以不同尺度提取局部和全局结构特征。首先，使用SSD在三种尺度上进行多模态特征提取，并通过特征缩放函数增强前景边缘和结构信息。其次，提出了基于SSD的跨模态特征融合模型，包括跨模态交互（CMI）模块用于每尺度交叉特征提取，以及多尺度融合（MSF）模块用于渐进式上采样融合。最后，从CMI的1/8尺度和MSF的1/2尺度收集特征计算匹配概率，并通过像素级对应建立匹配。

研究结果：在VIS-SAR、VIS-IR和VIS-NIR等多个数据集上的实验表明，RegNetMamba-2在性能和效率上均优于当前最先进的深度学习方法。该方法能有效提取共享结构信息，并在训练和推理中保持高效率。主要意义在于证明了SSD在多模态图像配准中的潜力，并为全局结构特征提取提供了新范式。

局限性：论文中未详细讨论SSD在不同模态组合下的鲁棒性，例如当模态差异极大时是否仍能稳定提取共享特征。此外，模型的计算开销分析仅对比了Transformers，未与其他轻量级架构（如MobileNet）对比，可能忽略更高效的基线。

未来方向：未来可以探索将SSD与其他注意力机制或卷积网络结合，以处理更复杂的多模态场景（如医学影像）。同时，可进一步优化特征缩放函数以适应不同模态的纹理和几何变化。

研究相关性：该论文为多模态配准领域提供了新的SSD框架，对于研究图像融合、匹配以及高效深度学习架构的研究者具有重要参考价值，尤其是对探索状态空间模型在视觉任务中的应用具有启发意义。

Optical-Guided Neural Collapse for SAR Few-Shot Class Incremental Learning

光学引导的神经坍塌用于SAR小样本类增量学习

2026-06-03
arXiv

Fan Zhang，Sijin Zheng，Fei Ma，Qiang Yin，Yongsheng Zhou 等

ID: http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.04528v1

Few-shot class-incremental learning (FSCIL) in synthetic aperture radar imagery presents unique challenges due to severe data scarcity and SAR-specific variability. In particular, strong azimuth sensitivity in SAR induces large intra-class variation and inter-class confusion, and FSCIL sequential updates further lead to catastrophic forgetting of previously learned classes. Inspired by neural collapse, we propose an optical-guided SAR FSCIL framework, which derives orthogonal feature subspaces from a data-rich optical ATR dataset and uses them as geometric priors to guide SAR feature learning. SAR features are projected onto these orthogonal subspaces via principal angle constraints, effectively transferring discriminative structure from the optical to the SAR domain. Specifically, our projection loss and the classifier loss optimized with a frozen simplex-ETF geometry jointly induce neural collapse by concentrating features around class means while maintaining large inter-class angles. We evaluate the approach on a benchmark comprising an optical ATR dataset and a SAR ATR dataset with 24 target classes, organized into a base training session and seven incremental sessions. Compared with recent FSCIL methods including NCFSCIL and so on, our method achieves the highest final accuracy and a favorable trade-off between final performance and performance degradation. Moreover, neural collapse metrics show improved intra-class compactness and inter-class separability, indicating that the learned features more closely approximate the ideal simplex-ETF geometry.

研究问题：解决SAR图像中少样本增量学习的灾难性遗忘和类间混淆问题。

主要发现：在24类SAR数据集上达到最高最终精度，性能退化小，类内紧凑且类间分离。

创新点：创新地利用光学先验正交子空间与神经崩溃几何联合引导SAR增量学习。

相关性：为SAR少样本增量学习提供了多模态知识迁移的有效新方案。

研究背景：合成孔径雷达图像中的少样本类增量学习面临数据稀缺和SAR特有变异性（如方位角敏感性导致的类内差异大和类间混淆）的挑战，且增量更新会引发灾难性遗忘。本研究受神经坍缩启发，利用光学ATR数据集中的丰富数据构建正交特征子空间作为几何先验，引导SAR特征学习。

方法详情：提出光学引导的SAR FSCIL框架，从光学ATR数据集提取正交特征子空间，通过主角约束将SAR特征投影到这些子空间，从而将光学域中的判别结构迁移到SAR域。具体采用投影损失和分类器损失（基于冻结的单纯形ETF几何）联合优化，使特征围绕类均值集中并保持大类间角度，诱导神经坍缩。

研究结果：在包含24个目标类的光学ATR和SAR ATR基准上，经过基础训练和7个增量会话，该方法相比NCFSCIL等最新方法取得了最高最终精度，并在最终性能与性能退化之间实现了良好权衡。神经坍缩度量显示类内紧凑性和类间可分性提升，特征更接近理想单纯形ETF几何。

局限性：该方法依赖光学ATR数据集，可能对光学与SAR域间差异较大的场景泛化性不足；此外，正交子空间的构建需要额外光学数据，实际应用中可能难以获取。实验仅在特定基准上验证，未探索更复杂场景（如更多类别或更少基类）下的性能。

未来方向：未来可探索无需外部光学数据的自监督正交子空间生成方法，或研究多模态融合策略以增强SAR特征对方位角变化等SAR特有变异的鲁棒性。

STARS: Shared-specific Translation and Alignment for missing-modality Remote Sensing Semantic Segmentation

STARS：面向缺失模态的遥感语义分割的共享-特定转换与对齐

2026-06-04
International Journal of Applied Earth Observation and Geoinformation (IF: 8.6)

Tong Wang，Xiaodong Zhang，Guanzhou Chen，Jiaqi Wang，Chenxi Liu 等

DOI: 10.1016/j.jag.2026.105378

Multimodal remote sensing technology significantly enhances the understanding of surface semantics by integrating heterogeneous data such as optical images, Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR), and Digital Surface Models (DSM). However, in practical applications, the missing of modality data (e.g., optical or DSM) is a common and severe challenge, which leads to performance decline in traditional multimodal fusion models. Existing methods for addressing missing modalities still face limitations, including feature collapse and overly generalized recovered features. To address these issues, we propose STARS ( S hared-specific T ranslation and A lignment for missing-modality R emote S ensing), a robust semantic segmentation framework for incomplete multimodal inputs. STARS is built on two key designs. First, we introduce an asymmetric alignment mechanism with bidirectional translation and stop-gradient, which effectively prevents feature collapse and reduces sensitivity to hyperparameters. Accelerating permafrost degradation is driving the rapid expansion of thaw slumps (TS) across the Northern Hemisphere, posing increasing risks to ecosystems, infrastructure, and the global carbon cycle. However, reliable TS identification at continental to hemispheric scales remains challenging due to strong environmental heterogeneity, fragmented remote sensing workflows, and limited cross-regional transferability of existing methods. Here, we address the cross-regional transferability gap in hemispheric TS identification by developing a reproducible framework that couples cloud-native multi-source data fusion in Google Earth Engine (GEE) with a modular PyTorch-based deep learning pipeline. The framework operationalizes transferability through three coordinated components: (1) a physically interpretable channel design that integrates Sentinel-1, Sentinel-2, and topographic data to encode TS-relevant processes; (2) a structure-preserving feature representation using PCA-guided dimensionality reduction and SNIC-based superpixel segmentation to suppress noise while maintaining geomorphic consistency; and (3) a standardized cross-regional training and ablation strategy that identifies model configurations maximizing generalization rather than region-specific accuracy. Evaluations across the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau (QTP), the Arctic, and the broader Northern Hemisphere demonstrate that late-summer observations and fused optical–SAR channels consistently outperform single-sensor inputs. The LinkNet-EfficientNet-B7 configuration achieves intersection-over-union (IOU) scores of 0.802 in the QTP, 0.770 in the Arctic, and 0.776 at hemispheric scale. Cross-regional transfer experiments further confirm robust generalization, with the hemispheric model maintaining IOU values exceeding 0.63 across distinct permafrost domains without region-specific retraining. These results indicate that transferable TS mapping is not primarily determined by model complexity, but by the consistency between process-informed feature representation and cross-regional training design. The proposed GEE-PyTorch framework provides a computationally efficient and reproducible foundation for operational monitoring of thaw-induced geomorphic disturbances.
