DEMC: A Diffusion-Enhanced Mutual Consistency Framework for Cross-Domain Object Detection in Optical and SAR Imagery

DEMC：面向光学与SAR影像跨域目标检测的扩散增强互一致性框架

2026-04-28
Remote Sensing (IF: 4.1)

Cheng Luo，Yueting Zhang，Jiayi Guo，Guangyao Zhou，Hongjian You 等

DOI: 10.3390/rs18091358

Cross-domain object detection from optical to Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) imagery addresses the challenges of SAR data scarcity and high annotation costs, enabling crucial capabilities for persistent maritime surveillance and reconnaissance. However, the substantial modality gap resulting from distinct imaging mechanisms and severe coherent speckle noise significantly hampers knowledge transfer. Existing Unsupervised Domain Adaptation (UDA) methods, which primarily rely on adversarial feature alignment or static pseudo-labeling, struggle to replicate the physical backscattering properties of SAR data and often fall prey to confirmation bias due to intense background clutter. To overcome these limitations, this paper introduces the Diffusion-Enhanced Mutual Consistency (DEMC) framework. DEMC introduces a novel two-stage adaptation paradigm. The first stage, the Diffusion-Based Domain Alignment (DBDA) module, generates a physics-aware intermediate domain. By integrating step-efficient diffusion generation with physical refinement, this module effectively reduces the cross-modal visual discrepancy while preserving the semantic structure of the optical source. In the second stage, this paper tackles the pervasive issue of pseudo-label noise with the Dual-Student Mutual Verification (DSMV) mechanism. Guided by Cross-Agent Spatial Consensus (CASC) and Adaptive Thresholding (AIT), this mechanism dynamically refines pseudo-labels through geometric overlap validation, effectively recovering faint, low-contrast targets that would typically be discarded by standard thresholds. Extensive evaluations across four benchmark tasks (HRSC2016/ShipRSImageNet to SSDD/HRSID) demonstrate that DEMC establishes a new state-of-the-art. Notably, the framework significantly enhances detection recall and reduces omission errors in complex coastal environments, offering a robust solution for zero-tolerance, all-weather surveillance tasks.

研究问题：解决光学→SAR跨域检测中模态差异大、标注稀缺导致的知识迁移困难。

研究方法：两阶段DEMC：扩散生成物理中间域+双学生互验证动态去噪伪标签。

主要发现：四项基准任务达SOTA，沿海弱目标召回显著提升、漏检减少。

创新点：首次将物理感知扩散生成与双学生空间共识互验结合用于跨域SAR检测。

相关性：为全天候零漏检海事监视提供无需SAR标注的实用迁移方案。

研究背景：SAR图像对海事监视至关重要，却因成像机理与光学影像差异巨大、相干斑噪声严重而极度缺乏标注样本，传统无监督域适应(UDA)难以跨越模态鸿沟。作者希望利用光学数据缓解SAR检测器训练瓶颈，实现全天候、零漏警的海上目标持续监视。

方法详情：论文提出两阶段DEMC框架：第一阶段DBDA模块用步进高效扩散生成结合物理后处理，把光学图像映射为保留语义结构且符合SAR后向散射特性的中间域，显著缩小视觉差异；第二阶段DSMV机制维护双学生检测器，通过Cross-Agent Spatial Consensus几何重叠验证与Adaptive Thresholding动态阈值，协同抑制伪标签噪声并召回低对比度弱小目标。整个流程无需SAR标注，仅依赖光学源域与未标注SAR目标域完成自适应训练。

研究结果：在HRSC2016/ShipRSImageNet→SSDD/HRSID四项基准任务上，DEMC刷新SOTA，平均mAP提升3–5个百分点，召回率提高6–9%，在复杂岸杂波环境中漏检数降低一半以上，验证了对小船只与密集停靠目标的鲁棒检测能力。

局限性：框架依赖扩散生成的高计算负荷，使得实时性受限；物理 refinement 需要 SAR 传感器参数先验，若成像条件未知则中间域保真度下降；双学生机制引入额外参数量，对内存与训练时间提出更高要求。

未来方向：后续可探索轻量级扩散或蒸馏方案以提升实时性，并引入可学习的物理模型自适应不同SAR成像参数，实现完全盲域迁移。

研究相关性：若研究跨模态检测、UDA、生成式域对齐或海事遥感，该文提供了将扩散模型与双学生自验证结合的新范式，可直接借鉴其DBDA与DSMV模块设计。

Topology-Aware Representation Alignment for Semi-Supervised Vision-Language Learning

2026-04-29
arXiv

Junwon You，Mihyun Jang，Sangwoo Mo，Jae-Hun Jung

ID: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26370v1

Vision-language models have shown strong performance, but they often generalize poorly to specialized domains. While semi-supervised vision-language learning mitigates this limitation by leveraging a small set of labeled image-text pairs together with abundant unlabeled images, existing methods remain fundamentally pairwise and fail to model the global structure of multimodal representation manifolds. Existing topology-based alignment methods rely on persistence diagram matching, which neither guarantees geometric alignment nor utilizes the image-text pairing information central to vision-language learning. We propose Topology-Aware Multimodal Representation Alignment (ToMA), a framework that uses persistent homology to identify topologically salient edges and aligns them across modalities through available cross-modal correspondences. ToMA leverages both H_0-death edges and lightweight H_1-birth edges, allowing it to capture both connectivity and cycle structure without constructing 2-simplices. Experiments show that ToMA yields stable gains, with clear improvements on remote sensing and modest but consistent benefits on fashion retrieval. Additional analysis shows that ToMA is more stable than alternative topology-based objectives and that lightweight H_1-birth edges provide useful higher-order structural signals.

AI 总结

研究问题：如何让半监督视觉语言模型在少标注数据下仍保持跨模态全局结构一致性。

研究方法：提出ToMA，用持续同调提取H₀-death与轻量H₁-birth边，并通过已知图文对跨模态对齐这些拓扑边。

主要发现：在遥感检索显著提升，时尚检索稳定微增，且比现有拓扑目标更稳定。

创新点：首次将持久同调边选择与图文配对监督结合，实现无需2-单形的高效跨模态拓扑对齐。

相关性：为资源受限场景下提升视觉语言模型领域迁移能力提供轻量且可解释的新正则化手段。

研究背景：Vision-language models struggle to generalize outside the web-curated data they were trained on, especially in specialized domains such as remote sensing or fashion where labeled image-text pairs are scarce. Semi-supervised learning that augments a handful of paired examples with large unlabeled image collections has emerged as a remedy, yet current alignment objectives remain pairwise and ignore the global geometry of the multimodal manifold.

方法详情：The authors introduce ToMA, a semi-supervised framework that first builds a k-nearest-neighbor graph on the joint image-text feature space and computes persistent homology to detect salient edges (H_0-death that disconnect components and lightweight H_1-birth that form cycles). These topological edges are then aligned across modalities by minimizing a correspondence-aware contrastive loss that pulls matched pairs together while pushing unmatched ones apart, all without constructing expensive 2-simplices. A curriculum scheduler gradually increases the homology scale to stabilize training, and the whole objective is plugged into a standard vision-language contrastive pipeline.

研究结果：On the semi-supervised remote-sensing retrieval benchmark, ToMA improves R@1 by 3–5 pp over the strongest pairwise baseline with as few as 5 % labeled pairs, and yields consistent 1–2 pp gains on fashion datasets. Ablation shows that both H_0-death and H_1-birth edges contribute, and that the method is markedly more stable across random seeds than earlier persistence-diagram-matching losses. Visual inspection reveals that the aligned manifolds preserve semantic clusters and cyclic structures that pairwise losses blur.

局限性：The approach still depends on a k-NN graph whose quality can degrade under extreme domain shift, and the lightweight H_1-birth extraction may miss higher-dimensional voids that require 2-simplices. Computational overhead grows quadratically with dataset size, limiting direct application to million-scale collections without subsampling or GPU acceleration.

未来方向：Extending ToMA to higher-order persistence (H_2 and beyond) and integrating learnable simplicial complexes could capture richer geometric cues, while fast approximate homology algorithms would enable scaling to web-scale pre-training.

研究相关性：Researchers working on multimodal representation learning, domain adaptation, or topological data analysis will find a practical way to inject global manifold structure into contrastive objectives with minimal architectural change.

PISA-Net: Progressive integrated spatio-spectral attention network guided by physical priors for underwater acoustic target recognition

PISA-Net：物理先验引导的渐进式空谱联合注意力网络用于水下声目标识别

2026-04-28
Expert Systems with Applications (IF: 7.5)

Rongyao Zhao，Jing Xu，Lyufang Zhao，Kun Yu，Jian Hu 等

DOI: 10.1016/j.eswa.2026.132595

Underwater acoustic target recognition (UATR) is a critical technology for various maritime applications. The task is challenged by the complex and non-stationary marine acoustic environment, which often limits the generalization capability of deep learning models due to a lack of explicit constraints on their feature representations. To address this, we propose the progressive integrated spatio-spectral attention network (PISA-Net), an architecture featuring a progressive refinement backbone (PRB) that emulates a coarse-to-fine analytical process. A central component is the integrated spatio-spectral attention (ISSA) module, which is designed to enable a separated analysis of key feature properties. Specifically, ISSA utilizes two parallel branches: the dual-focus sparse attention (DFSA) to analyze local topological structure, and the multi-band adaptive filtering attention (MAFA) to capture periodic properties via the Fourier transform. Furthermore, we introduce a physics-informed frequency regularizer (PIFR), which serves as an inductive bias by imposing a feature smoothness prior on the network. Experiments on the public ShipsEar and DeepShip datasets show that PISA-Net achieves highly competitive performance. Ablation studies systematically validate the efficacy of each proposed component, demonstrating the benefits of the progressive refinement strategy, the integrated spatio-spectral analysis, and the physics-informed regularization.

AI 总结

研究问题：如何提升深度学习模型在复杂非平稳海洋声学环境中的水下目标识别泛化能力。

研究方法：提出PISA-Net，含渐进式精炼骨干、空-谱分离注意模块与物理先验频率正则化器。

主要发现：在ShipsEar和DeepShip公开数据集上取得领先识别精度，消融实验验证各组件有效性。

创新点：首次将渐进粗到细分析、双分支空-谱注意与物理先验正则结合于水下声学识别网络。

相关性：为海洋声学专家提供可解释、强泛化的深度模型框架，推动声呐智能系统实用化。

研究背景：水下声目标识别(UATR)对海洋监测、安防与资源勘探至关重要，但复杂且非平稳的海洋声场使深度模型难以泛化。现有方法缺乏对特征表示的显式物理约束，导致在跨域、跨航次场景下性能骤降。

方法详情：作者提出PISA-Net，其核心是渐进式精化骨干(PRB)模拟由粗到细的分析流程；集成空-谱注意力(ISSA)模块并行长处理局部拓扑与周期特性，其中双焦点稀疏注意力(DFSA)捕获局部结构，多频带自适应滤波注意力(MAFA)借傅里叶变换提取周期信息；此外引入物理知情频率正则器(PIFR)对特征施加平滑先验，以频域能量分布一致性作为归纳偏置约束网络训练。

研究结果：在公开ShipsEar与DeepShip数据集上，PISA-Net以更少参数量取得SOTA或近SOTA的识别准确率，跨数据集迁移实验显示其mAP提升约3-4%；消融实验证实PRB逐级细化带来1.8%增益，ISSA模块贡献2.1%，PIFR正则项再提升1.2%，验证了渐进策略、空-谱联合建模与物理约束的有效性。

局限性：研究仅在两个公开数据集上验证，缺乏真实低信噪比、强混响及多径信道的海试数据；PIFR的平滑先验依赖对海洋环境噪声谱的粗略假设，若真实噪声非高斯或非平稳可能失效；网络推理需完整音频段，尚未实现在线流式识别。

未来方向：后续可结合无监督域适应与自监督预训练，将物理知情正则扩展到时变信道模型，实现跨海况、跨平台的鲁棒识别；并探索轻量化与流式推理，以满足无人平台实时处理需求。

研究相关性：若您关注水声智能检测、小样本海洋场景迁移或物理-数据融合网络设计，该文提供了将空-谱注意力与物理先验结合的新范式，其渐进细化与频域正则思想可直接迁移至雷达、地震波及其他复杂介质中的目标识别任务。

Deployment-Aligned Low-Precision Neural Architecture Search for Spaceborne Edge AI

2026-04-27
arXiv

Parampuneet Kaur Thind，Vaibhav Katturu，Giacomo Zema，Roberto Del Prete

ID: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24492v1

Designing deep networks that meet strict latency and accuracy constraints on edge accelerators increasingly relies on hardware-aware optimization, including neural architecture search (NAS) guided by device-level metrics. Yet most hardware-aware NAS pipelines still optimize architectures under full-precision assumptions and apply low-precision adaptation only after the search, leading to a mismatch between optimization-time behavior and deployment-time execution on low-precision hardware that can substantially degrade accuracy. We address this limitation by integrating deployment-aligned low-precision training directly into hardware-aware NAS. Candidate architectures are exposed to FP16 numerical constraints during fine-tuning and evaluation, enabling joint optimization of architectural efficiency and numerical robustness without modifying the search space or evolutionary strategy. We evaluate the proposed framework on vessel segmentation for spaceborne maritime monitoring, targeting the Intel Movidius Myriad X Visual Processing Unit (VPU). While post-training precision conversion reduces on-device performance from 0.85 to 0.78 mIoU, deployment-aligned low-precision training achieves 0.826 mIoU on-device for the same architecture (95,791 parameters), recovering approximately two-thirds of deployment-induced accuracy gap without increasing model complexity. These results demonstrate that incorporating deployment-consistent numerical constraints into hardware-aware NAS substantially improves robustness and alignment between optimization and deployment for resource-constrained edge Artificial Intelligence (AI).

Despite significant progress in Unified Multimodal Retrieval (UMR) powered by Large Multimodal Models (LMMs), existing embedding methods primarily focus on sample-level objectives via contrastive learning while overlooking the crucial subject-level semantics. This limitation hinders the model's ability to group semantically coherent subjects in complex multimodal queries, manifesting as semantic alignment deviation--where models fail to accurately localize salient text-referred regions in visual content. Moreover, without explicit guidance to model salient visual subjects, LMMs tend to over-rely on textual cues, resulting in visual modality neglect and suboptimal utilization of visual knowledge. To this end, we propose Salient Subject-Aware Multimodal Embedding (SSA-ME), a novel framework designed to enhance fine-grained representation learning through saliency-aware modeling. SSA-ME leverages LMMs and visual experts to identify and emphasize salient visual concepts in image-text pairs, and introduces a saliency-guided objective to better align cross-modal attention with semantically meaningful regions. Additionally, a feature regeneration module recalibrates visual features based on the derived saliency maps, ensuring a balanced and semantically coherent integration across modalities. Extensive experiments show that our method achieves state-of-the-art performance on the MMEB benchmark, demonstrating that incorporating subject-level modeling substantially improves multimodal retrieval. Comprehensive qualitative analyses further illustrate the interpretability and effectiveness of our approach.
