Optical-Guided Neural Collapse for SAR Few-Shot Class Incremental Learning

光学引导的神经坍塌用于SAR小样本类增量学习

2026-06-03
arXiv

Fan Zhang，Sijin Zheng，Fei Ma，Qiang Yin，Yongsheng Zhou 等

ID: http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.04528v1

Few-shot class-incremental learning (FSCIL) in synthetic aperture radar imagery presents unique challenges due to severe data scarcity and SAR-specific variability. In particular, strong azimuth sensitivity in SAR induces large intra-class variation and inter-class confusion, and FSCIL sequential updates further lead to catastrophic forgetting of previously learned classes. Inspired by neural collapse, we propose an optical-guided SAR FSCIL framework, which derives orthogonal feature subspaces from a data-rich optical ATR dataset and uses them as geometric priors to guide SAR feature learning. SAR features are projected onto these orthogonal subspaces via principal angle constraints, effectively transferring discriminative structure from the optical to the SAR domain. Specifically, our projection loss and the classifier loss optimized with a frozen simplex-ETF geometry jointly induce neural collapse by concentrating features around class means while maintaining large inter-class angles. We evaluate the approach on a benchmark comprising an optical ATR dataset and a SAR ATR dataset with 24 target classes, organized into a base training session and seven incremental sessions. Compared with recent FSCIL methods including NCFSCIL and so on, our method achieves the highest final accuracy and a favorable trade-off between final performance and performance degradation. Moreover, neural collapse metrics show improved intra-class compactness and inter-class separability, indicating that the learned features more closely approximate the ideal simplex-ETF geometry.

Long-Aperture ISAR Imaging of Complex Motion Ships via a Joint Adjacent-Order Coefficient Distribution

基于联合相邻阶系数分布对复杂运动船舶的长孔径ISAR成像

2026-06-05
IEEE Transactions on Geoscience and Remote Sensing (IF: 8.6)

Qun Yan，Jixiang Fu，Pengwei Lan，Xiaoyu Xue，Guang-Cai Sun 等

DOI: 10.1109/tgrs.2026.3700325

Under long synthetic apertures, the range migration and phase errors of echoes from complex motion ship targets exhibit pronounced three-dimensional spatially variant characteristics, making it difficult to achieve unified focusing of all scattering points. To address this issue, the echo signal of each ship scattering center is modeled as a two-dimensional polynomial phase signal, which can be individually focused to obtain a well-focused image. However, as the synthetic aperture time increases, the polynomial order of the signal rises, leading to a dramatic growth in computational burden for conventional parameter-search-based methods, thereby limiting their applicability to fast imaging in practical scenarios. To overcome this limitation, a novel long-aperture ISAR imaging algorithm for complex motion ships via a joint adjacent-order coefficient distribution is proposed. In this algorithm, a new joint adjacent-order coefficient distribution is first proposed for the analysis of two-dimensional Nth-order polynomial phase signals. This distribution characterizes the coherent accumulation property of the signal on the joint Nth–(N-1)th order coefficient plane, where the polynomial coefficients can be directly estimated from the peak locations. The proposed distribution can be efficiently implemented using two one-dimensional interpolations and two fast Fourier transforms. Furthermore, image-domain filtering and a range-blocking strategy are incorporated to further enhance the robustness and computational efficiency of the proposed imaging algorithm. Simulation and real measured results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.

局限性：算法对信号模型假设（如二维多项式相位信号）的准确性敏感，实际中非理想运动或噪声可能影响系数估计精度。此外，联合阶数分布方法在极高阶数（如四阶以上）时，仍需进一步评估其计算量与峰值检测的可靠性。

T-SAR-JEPA: Self-Supervised Temporal Anomaly Detection in SAR Amplitude Stacks via Latent Prediction

T-SAR-JEPA: 基于潜在预测的SAR幅度堆栈自监督时间异常检测

2026-06-04
arXiv

Kerod Woldesenbet，Abem Woldesenbet

ID: http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05700v1

We present T-SAR-JEPA, a self-supervised framework for temporal anomaly detection in SAR amplitude stacks via latent prediction. A ViT-Base/16 encoder from SAR-JEPA is domain-adapted on 39,300 Capella patches using local masked reconstruction with gradient feature prediction. A temporal transformer with sinusoidal time encoding forecasts future latent states from K=7 acquisitions, with progressive unfreezing substantially reducing validation loss. The model operates on amplitude alone; InSAR coherence serves exclusively as independent pseudo-ground-truth. On the DFC 2026 dataset (300 time-series, three AOIs), T-SAR-JEPA achieves ROC-AUC of 77.0% on the Hawaii eruption window, outperforming RX, PaDiM, Linear AR, and LSTM baselines (~50%). Spatial coherence of 99.9% (p < 0.001, permutation test) confirms structured detections. Code: https://github.com/TerraLatent/t-sar-jepa

Dual Prompt Learning for Unsupervised Degradation-Resistant Image Fusion

面向无监督抗退化图像融合的双重提示学习

2026-06-05
IEEE Transactions on Multimedia (IF: 9.7)

Borui Li，Yong Ma，Fan Fan，Xiaoguang Mei，Jiayi Ma

DOI: 10.1109/tmm.2026.3700777

Mining source information and resisting degradation in multi-modal image fusion is of great challenge. Current approaches suffer from inaccurate mining prior and poor scene generalization, limiting their ability to handle the extreme condition image fusion. In this paper, we propose a dual degradation-resistant prompt learning fusion framework, termed as DRFusion, to address these issues. Firstly, we design the Dual Prompt Degradation-Resistant Network (DPDNet), which leverages large language-vision model to perform multi-level mining perception. To be detailed, it adopts the large-scale Contrastive Language-Image Pretraining (CLIP) to assist in extracting degraded features through dual guidance of semantics and perception. The prior knowledge in CLIP serves as a bridge between high-quality open-world images and learnable prompts, solving the problem of lacking paired data in multi-modal image fusion tasks with degradation. We propose ProMML, a unified adaptation framework built on a frozen CLIP backbone, which improves information flow through progressive representation fusion. ProMML integrates Progressive Layer Fusion (PLF), Cross-Modal Fusion (CMF), and a Dynamic Prompt Generator (DPG) over learnable layer-wise representation tokens. PLF aggregates these tokens by cross-layer attention, CMF performs lightweight bidirectional fusion over representation tokens, and DPG provides auxiliary dynamic soft-prompt regularization by combining image-conditioned prompts with a shared prompt pool. Experiments on 11 datasets show competitive performance, achieving an average harmonic mean of 81.63% for base-to-novel generalization and 70.33% average accuracy for cross-dataset transfer, while remaining competitive on ImageNet domain generalization variants. Ablations verify that the three components are complementary and that multi-level fusion is important for novel-class generalization.
