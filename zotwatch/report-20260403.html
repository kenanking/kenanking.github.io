NASTaR: A NovaSAR-Based Automated Ship Target Recognition Dataset

NASTaR：基于 NovaSAR 的自动舰船目标识别数据集

2026-04-02
IEEE Geoscience and Remote Sensing Letters (IF: 4.4)

Benyamin Hosseiny，Kamirul Kamirul，Odysseas Pappas，Alin Achim

DOI: 10.1109/lgrs.2026.3679941

We introduce the NovaSAR Automated Ship Target Recognition (NASTaR) dataset. This dataset comprises of 3415 ship patches extracted from NovaSAR S-band imagery, with labels matched to AIS data. It includes distinctive features such as 23 unique classes, inshore/offshore separation, and an auxiliary wake dataset for patches where ship wakes are visible. We validated the dataset’s applicability across prominent ship-type classification scenarios using benchmark deep learning models. Results demonstrate over 60% accuracy for classifying four major ship types, over 70% for a three-class scenario, more than 75% for distinguishing cargo from tanker ships, and over 87% for identifying fishing vessels. The NASTaR dataset is available at https://doi.org/10.5523/bris.2tfa6x37oerz2lyiw6hp47058, while relevant codes for benchmarking and analysis are available at https://github.com/benyaminhosseiny/nastar.

AI 总结

研究问题：构建首个基于NovaSAR S波段图像的自动舰船目标识别公开数据集并验证其可用性。

研究方法：从NovaSAR影像裁剪3415个带AIS标签的舰船切片，划分23类并附尾迹子集，用主流深度学习模型基准测试。

主要发现：在四分类、三分、货/油轮二分及渔船识别任务中分别达60%、70%、75%、87%以上精度。

创新点：首次发布含S-band SAR、AIS匹配、23细类及尾迹辅助的NASTaR数据集，填补公开SAR舰船细粒度识别空白。

相关性：为SAR舰船分类与检测研究提供真实S-band基准数据，推动海事监控与深度学习模型发展。

研究背景：当前公开的S波段合成孔径雷达(SAR)船舶目标数据集稀缺，而S波段对海洋大气扰动具有更好穿透性，对近岸船舶监视价值高；作者希望填补该频段带标签数据空白，为自动船舶目标识别(ATR)提供基准。

方法详情：研究利用英国NovaSAR-S获取的S波段影像，通过同步AIS报文实现目标关联，截取3415张船舶切片并人工校正标签；依据AIS船型字段划分23类，同时标注近岸/离岸属性，另对可见尾流的切片建立辅助尾流子集；最后采用ResNet50、VGG16等典型CNN在四类、三类、货-油两类和渔船-非渔船任务上进行训练与五折交叉验证，以Top-1准确率和混淆矩阵评估。

研究结果：实验显示四类主要船型(货船、油船、渔船、客船)分类准确率>60%，三类场景>70%，货-油区分>75%，渔船识别>87%，证明NASTaR可支撑S波段SAR船舶ATR研究；尾流子集初步实验表明尾流纹理可额外提升2-3%精度；数据集已公开，提供标准化划分与代码，便于后续复现与对比。

局限性：样本在23类细粒度上极度不平衡，部分稀有船型样本<20，限制深度模型性能；AIS匹配仅保留成功关联目标，未考虑AIS关闭或 spoofing 船舶，引入潜在标签噪声；影像分辨率约6m，对小型艇只细节刻画有限。

未来方向：后续可引入多源S波段时序影像进行多视角融合，并采用长尾学习与弱监督方法缓解类别不平衡；结合尾流物理特征与船舶散射特征设计混合模型，有望进一步提升细粒度识别准确率。

研究相关性：若研究者关注SAR船舶检测与识别、S波段遥感应用、小样本学习或海事监视数据集构建，该文提供了目前稀缺的S波段带标签数据与基准结果，可直接用于算法验证与对比。

Light-ResKAN: A Parameter-Sharing Lightweight KAN with Gram Polynomials for Efficient SAR Image Recognition

Light-ResKAN：基于 Gram 多项式的参数共享轻量级 KAN 高效 SAR 图像识别方法

2026-04-02
arXiv

Pan Yi，Weijie Li，Xiaodong Chen，Jiehua Zhang，Li Liu 等

DOI: 10.1109/JSTARS.2026.3678852

Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) image recognition is vital for disaster monitoring, military reconnaissance, and ocean observation. However, large SAR image sizes hinder deep learning deployment on resource-constrained edge devices, and existing lightweight models struggle to balance high-precision feature extraction with low computational requirements. The emerging Kolmogorov-Arnold Network (KAN) enhances fitting by replacing fixed activations with learnable ones, reducing parameters and computation. Inspired by KAN, we propose Light-ResKAN to achieve a better balance between precision and efficiency. First, Light-ResKAN modifies ResNet by replacing convolutions with KAN convolutions, enabling adaptive feature extraction for SAR images. Second, we use Gram Polynomials as activations, which are well-suited for SAR data to capture complex non-linear relationships. Third, we employ a parameter-sharing strategy: each kernel shares parameters per channel, preserving unique features while reducing parameters and FLOPs. Our model achieves 99.09%, 93.01%, and 97.26% accuracy on MSTAR, FUSAR-Ship, and SAR-ACD datasets, respectively. Experiments on MSTAR resized to $1024 \times 1024$ show that compared to VGG16, our model reduces FLOPs by $82.90 \times$ and parameters by $163.78 \times$. This work establishes an efficient solution for edge SAR image recognition.

AI 总结

研究问题：如何在资源受限边缘设备上实现高精度低算力的SAR图像识别

研究方法：将ResNet卷积替换为共享参数的Gram多项式KAN卷积

主要发现：在三大SAR数据集达99.09%/93.01%/97.26%精度，比VGG16减82.9×FLOPs与163.8×参数

创新点：首次把参数共享Gram多项式KAN卷积引入轻量化SAR识别网络

相关性：为边缘计算场景提供超轻量高精度SAR图像识别新基准

研究背景：SAR图像在灾害、军事与海洋监测中不可或缺，但其高分辨率导致模型体量大，难以在边缘端实时运行；现有轻量网络又常牺牲精度。

方法详情：作者以ResNet为骨架，将卷积层替换为KAN卷积，使激活函数本身可学习；进一步把激活函数参数化为Gram多项式，以更好拟合SAR数据的非线性统计特性；同时在每个通道内让卷积核共享同一组激活参数，在保留通道特异性的同时压缩参数量与FLOPs。

研究结果：在MSTAR、FUSAR-Ship、SAR-ACD上分别取得99.09%、93.01%、97.26%的识别准确率；当MSTAR放大到1024×1024时，相比VGG16，Light-ResKAN的FLOPs降低82.9倍，参数量降低163.8倍，证明在极高分辨率下仍保持轻量与高精度。

局限性：论文仅在三个公开数据集上验证，缺乏更复杂背景、更大规模或跨传感器数据的泛化测试；Gram多项式阶数与通道共享策略对性能-压缩比的最优权衡尚未系统探讨；KAN卷积的硬件友好性与推理延迟未给出实测。

未来方向：后续可探索可学习的多项式阶数、跨通道稀疏共享及针对SAR特性的专用硬件加速，以进一步压缩并提升边缘部署速度。

研究相关性：若研究者关注轻量遥感识别、可学习激活函数或边缘AI，该文提供了将KAN理论落地到SAR任务的完整范例与可复现指标。

Unified Restoration-Perception Learning: Maritime Infrared-Visible Image Fusion and Segmentation

统一复原-感知学习：海上红外-可见光图像融合与分割

2026-03-30
arXiv

Weichao Cai，Weiliang Huang，Biao Xue，Chao Huang，Fei Yuan 等

ID: http://arxiv.org/abs/2603.28414v1

Marine scene understanding and segmentation plays a vital role in maritime monitoring and navigation safety. However, prevalent factors like fog and strong reflections in maritime environments cause severe image degradation, significantly compromising the stability of semantic perception. Existing restoration and enhancement methods typically target specific degradations or focus solely on visual quality, lacking end-to-end collaborative mechanisms that simultaneously improve structural recovery and semantic effectiveness. Moreover, publicly available infrared-visible datasets are predominantly collected from urban scenes, failing to capture the authentic characteristics of coupled degradations in marine environments. To address these challenges, the Infrared-Visible Maritime Ship Dataset (IVMSD) is proposed to cover various maritime scenarios under diverse weather and illumination conditions. Building upon this dataset, a Multi-task Complementary Learning Framework (MCLF) is proposed to collaboratively perform image restoration, multimodal fusion, and semantic segmentation within a unified architecture. The framework includes a Frequency-Spatial Enhancement Complementary (FSEC) module for degradation suppression and structural enhancement, a Semantic-Visual Consistency Attention (SVCA) module for semantic-consistent guidance, and a cross-modality guided attention mechanism for selective fusion. Experimental results on IVMSD demonstrate that the proposed method achieves state-of-the-art segmentation performance, significantly enhancing robustness and perceptual quality under complex maritime conditions.

AI 总结

研究问题：解决海上雾、反光等退化导致红外-可见光图像语义分割不稳定的问题。

研究方法：构建IVMSD数据集，提出MCLF统一网络，集成FSEC去退化、SVCA语义引导与跨模态注意力融合。

主要发现：在IVMSD上实现SOTA分割精度，显著提升复杂海况下的鲁棒性与感知质量。

创新点：首次将复原-融合-分割协同纳入端到端多任务框架，并发布面向真实海上退化场景的双模数据集。

相关性：为海上监控与导航提供兼顾视觉增强与语义可靠性的新基准与方法，推动多模态海洋视觉研究。

研究背景：海雾、强反光等耦合退化使可见光与红外图像同时出现低对比度、细节丢失，直接削弱海事监控与导航的语义分割可靠性；现有复原或融合方法多针对单一退化或仅优化视觉质量，缺乏在统一框架内协同恢复结构与语义的手段。

方法详情：作者构建首个面向海事场景的红外-可见光船舶数据集IVMSD，涵盖多种天气与光照耦合退化；提出多任务互补学习框架MCLF，将去雾、融合与分割联合训练，其中FSEC模块在频率-空间双域抑制退化并增强结构，SVCA模块利用分割语义一致性引导视觉重建，跨模态引导注意力实现选择性融合，端到端共享特征以互相正则化。

局限性：数据集规模与场景类别仍有限，尚未覆盖夜间大浪、暴雨等更恶劣海况；统一损失权重依赖手工设定，跨任务最优平衡策略尚未探讨；框架计算量较大，边缘端实时部署尚未验证。

研究相关性：对从事多模态融合、恶劣天气视觉复原或海事智能感知的学者，该文提供了首个海事耦合退化基准与联合优化范式，可直接作为实验对比与算法扩展的基础。

PMDet: Patch-Aware Enhancement and Fusion for Multispectral Object Detection

PMDet：面向多光谱目标检测的块感知增强与融合

2026-04-02
Remote Sensing (IF: 4.1)

Jie Li，Chenhong Sui，Jing Wang，Jun Zhou

DOI: 10.3390/rs18071068

Multispectral object detection addresses the limitations of single-modal approaches by fusing complementary information from visible and infrared images, thereby improving robustness in complex environments. However, the inter-modal representations are inherently misaligned due to sensing discrepancies, and the complementary cues they provide are often imbalanced, making it difficult to exploit modality-specific information effectively. Moreover, directly merging features from different modalities can introduce noise and artifacts that deteriorate the detection performance. To this end, this paper proposes a patch-aware enhancement and fusion network for multispectral object detection (PMDet). This method employs a dual-stream backbone equipped with the patch-aware Feature Enhancer (FE) module for cross-modal features alignment and enhancement. FE not only reinforces the feature representation of key regions but also helps to suppress local noise and enhance the model’s perception of fine textures and differences. Building on these enriched features, the patch-based Feature Aggregator (FA) module allows for efficient inter-modal feature interaction and semantic fusion with noise resistance. Specifically, both FE and FA modules leverage the shifted-patch design to preserve computational efficiency while enabling long-range modeling. In this regard, PMDet couples multi-scale cross-modal semantic enhancement with deep semantic fusion to form a stable and discriminative multimodal representation pipeline. Experiments on FLIR, LLVIP, and VEDAI demonstrate that the method outperforms mainstream approaches in detection accuracy and robustness, and ablation studies further verify the effectiveness of each module.

DOI: 10.1109/tip.2026.3678014

Prompt learning has emerged as an effective strategy for adapting vision-language models (VLMs) which injects learnable semantic prompts into VLMs to guide the alignment between visual and textual representations. Although existing methods have shown strong performance across various tasks, they usually focus on the representative class-level samples and overlook the atypical and hard samples in visual feature space, which hinders generalization of VLMs. To address this issue, we propose the concept of dynamic boundary prototype, which highlights ambiguous samples that are far from the class centroid and is updated at each epoch. Accordingly, we propose a Distribution-Aware Prompt Learning (DAPL) framework to calibrate the distribution of visual feature space via the definition, optimization, and updating of dynamic boundary prototypes. Firstly, we introduce Boundary-Centroid Pulling to optimize the intra-class distribution by progressively reducing the distance between boundary and centroid prototypes, thereby enhancing structural consistency within each class. Secondly, to further enhance inter-class separability, a distance-weighted contrastive loss that places greater emphasis on distinguishing adjacent classes is designed, facilitating more effective fine-grained discrimination. Thirdly, we apply Low-Rank Adaptation Fine-Tuning to adapt the vision encoder through targeted modifications to its self-attention layers. Additionally, we adopt a progressive training strategy for stable optimization. DAPL is compatible with mainstream prompt learning methods such as CoOp, CoCoOp and PromptKD, and consistently improves their average performance across 11 benchmark datasets.

In Zero-Shot Learning (ZSL), embedding-based methods learn a visual–semantic mapping that leverages the attribute knowledge of seen classes to predict the attributes of unseen classes, enabling knowledge transfer from seen to unseen classes. As a result, models trained on seen classes often struggle to accurately recognize attributes in unseen classes, limiting their generalization ability. To address these challenges, we propose DAPZSL, a dual adversarial perturbation framework that enhances the robustness of visual–semantic mappings through Feature-Level Adversarial Perturbation (FAP) and improves the model’s generalization ability via Weight-Level Adversarial Perturbation (WAP). Specifically, FAP generates semantically perturbed adversarial samples at the feature level, and incorporating these samples during training encourages the model to learn more robust visual–semantic mappings that are resilient to semantic variations, which improves attribute recognition on unseen classes. Meanwhile, WAP introduces adversarial perturbations into the model’s weight space, promoting a flatter loss landscape that alleviates overfitting to seen classes and enhances generalization. Extensive experiments on multiple benchmark datasets—including AWA2, SUN, and CUB—demonstrate that DAPZSL significantly outperforms existing ZSL models.
