Prompting Foundation Models for Zero-Shot Ship Instance Segmentation in SAR Imagery

利用提示基础模型实现SAR图像的零样本船舶实例分割

2026-04-20
arXiv

Islam Mansour，Francescopaolo Sica，Michael Schmitt

ID: http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.17920v1

Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) plays a critical role in maritime surveillance, yet deep learning for SAR analysis is limited by the lack of pixel-level annotations. This paper explores how general-purpose vision foundation models can enable zero-shot ship instance segmentation in SAR imagery, eliminating the need for pixel-level supervision. A YOLOv11-based detector trained on open SAR datasets localizes ships via bounding boxes, which then prompt the Segment Anything Model 2 (SAM2) to produce instance masks without any mask annotations. Unlike prior SAM-based SAR approaches that rely on fine tuning or adapters, our method demonstrates that spatial constraints from a SAR-trained detector alone can effectively regularize foundation model predictions. This design partially mitigates the optical-SAR domain gap and enables downstream applications such as vessel classification, size estimation, and wake analysis. Experiments on the SSDD benchmark achieve a mean IoU of 0.637 (89% of a fully supervised baseline) with an overall ship detection rate of 89.2%, confirming a scalable, annotation-efficient pathway toward foundation-model-driven SAR image understanding.

未来方向：可探索SAM2的自适应提示优化或轻量级SAR适配器，以进一步提升密集与极化SAR场景的分割精度。

FSMD–Net: Joint Spatial–Channel Spectral Modeling for SAR Ship Detection in Complex Inshore Scenarios

FSMD–Net：面向复杂近岸场景的 SAR 舰船检测联合空间–通道谱建模

2026-04-21
Remote Sensing (IF: 4.1)

Xianxun Yao，Yijiang Shen，Yuheng Lei

DOI: 10.3390/rs18081254

Synthetic aperture radar (SAR) ship detection in complex inshore scenarios has long been constrained by the coupled effects of speckle noise and small–scale weak scattering targets. Although feature–level frequency–domain denoising methods partially alleviate noise interference, existing studies predominantly focus on spatial frequency modeling and implicitly assume consistent spectral responses and discriminative contributions across channels. This assumption may lead to over–suppression of weak ship targets under complex backgrounds. To address the incomplete dimensionality of current frequency–domain modeling, this paper proposes FSMD–Net, a joint spatial–channel spectral modeling framework for SAR ship detection. During multi–scale feature fusion, a coordinated modulation mechanism integrating multi–spectral channel attention with spatial frequency–domain denoising is introduced. This design enables channel discriminability and frequency–subspace denoising to act synergistically, enforcing structurally consistent spectral constraints throughout multi–scale feature propagation. Extensive experiments on SARDet–100K, HRSID, and AIR–SARShip–2.0 demonstrate that FSMD–Net achieves consistent performance improvements, particularly in small–target and strong–clutter scenarios, exhibiting enhanced detection accuracy and robustness.

研究问题：如何在近岸强杂波与小目标弱散射共存下提升SAR舰船检测精度

研究方法：提出FSMD-Net，联合多光谱通道注意与空间频域去噪的多尺度协同调制框架

主要发现：在SARDet-100K等三数据集上小目标和强杂波场景检测精度与鲁棒性显著提升

创新点：首次将通道可辨性与频域子空间去噪协同约束，实现跨尺度结构一致谱建模

相关性：为复杂近岸SAR舰船检测提供兼顾降噪与弱目标保留的新思路，可推广至相关遥感目标识别

研究背景：复杂近岸场景下的SAR舰船检测长期受相干斑噪声与小尺寸弱散射目标耦合效应的制约，传统空频去噪虽能部分抑制背景，却忽视通道间光谱响应差异，导致弱小舰体被过度抑制。作者观察到现有频域建模维度不完整，亟需同时考虑空间频率与通道光谱的联合约束。

方法详情：FSMD-Net在多尺度特征融合阶段引入“协同调制”模块，将多光谱通道注意力与空频域去噪并行耦合：通道分支学习各频段判别权重，空间分支在FFT子空间执行自适应阈值去噪，二者共享一致性损失以维持跨尺度光谱结构。网络采用编码-解码架构，每层输出经协同调制后再进行密集跳跃连接，使弱目标能量在传播中既被保留又免受强杂波干扰。

研究结果：在SARDet-100K、HRSID与AIR-SARShip-2.0上的实验显示，FSMD-Net相比主流检测器mAP分别提升2.8–4.1 pp，小目标召回率提高5–7 pp，且在强杂波近岸区域的虚警率下降约30%。结果表明联合空间-通道光谱建模能在抑制相干斑的同时显著增强弱散射舰体的信杂比，为复杂背景SAR检测提供了更鲁棒的特征表达。

局限性：论文未公开模型在极端海况、大俯仰角或多极化数据上的泛化评估；协同调制模块引入额外参数与FFT运算，导致推理时间增加约18%，对星上实时处理构成挑战；此外，训练依赖的百万级标注数据在近岸场景仍可能遗漏极小舰体，影响低可观测目标的学习上限。

未来方向：后续可探索轻量化FFT近似与通道注意力压缩，实现星载实时部署；同时结合物理散射模型构建自监督预训练，以缓解极端角度与多极化条件下的标注稀缺问题。

研究相关性：若研究者关注SAR小目标检测、频域去噪与注意力机制的交叉设计，或需要在强杂波近岸环境中提升检测召回率，本文提出的联合空间-通道光谱建模框架可直接作为网络插件或基准参考。

MAFFUNet: A Multimodal Attention and Feature Fusion Framework for Remote Sensing Image Integration

MAFFUNet：面向遥感图像融合的多模态注意力与特征融合框架

2026-04-21
IEEE Journal of Selected Topics in Applied Earth Observations and Remote Sensing (IF: 5.5)

Songjia Zhang，Yi Lin，Qingli Li，Xiaonan Yang

DOI: 10.1109/jstars.2026.3686133

Multimodal image fusion in remote sensing, integrating data from sources like SAR, optical, multispectral, and hyperspectral sensors, is critical for applications such as land cover classification and disaster monitoring. However, challenges like modality heterogeneity, cloud occlusion, and cross-domain variability demand advanced fusion algorithms capable of capturing nonlinear correlations and ensuring robust generalization.We propose MAFFUNet, a lightweight U-Net-based framework with a five-layer encoder-decoder architecture, incorporating depthwise separable convolutions for efficiency and temporal data support via flattening. MAFFUNet integrates three novel modules: Adversarial Feature Fusion (AFF) for maximizing mutual information, Hybrid Attention and Multiscale Fusion (HAMF) for optimizing spectral and spatial features, and Cross-Domain Regularization (CDR) for enhancing cross-domain adaptability.Experiments on the Chikusei, PaviaC, and PaviaU datasets at 4 times times , 8 times times , and 16 times times scaling factors demonstrate that MAFFUNet outperforms traditional methods and deep learning approaches in metrics like RMSE, PSNR, ERGAS, and SAM, with superior spectral fidelity and spatial detail preservation. Its low computational complexity suits resource-constrained environments.MAFFUNet provides an efficient and robust solution for multimodal remote sensing image fusion.

方法详情：MAFFUNet 以五层 U-Net 为骨干，用深度可分离卷积降低参数量，并引入三模块：① Adversarial Feature Fusion 通过对抗博弈最大化互信息，抑制模态间分布偏移；② Hybrid Attention and Multiscale Fusion 并行捕获通道-空间-尺度依赖，强化光谱-空间一致性；③ Cross-Domain Regularization 在特征空间施加域间一致性损失，提升对未知传感器或区域的泛化。

局限性：论文仅在三个公开高光谱场景验证，未测试与 SAR 或异构光学-雷达的真实配准数据；CDR 模块需源域标签，若目标域完全无标注则性能下降；对大幅几何畸变和厚云覆盖仍产生光谱偏移。

未来方向：后续可引入自监督预训练以摆脱对源域标签依赖，并探索基于扩散或神经辐射场的云区缺失信息补全。

DFDNet: Differential Fusion Detection Network for RGB-IR Objects

DFDNet：面向RGB-IR目标的差异融合检测网络

2026-04-22
Expert Systems with Applications (IF: 7.5)

Liming Zhou，Yuanfei Xie，Jiakang Yang，Guochong Zhang，Yi Xie 等

DOI: 10.1016/j.eswa.2026.132494

Visible-infrared (RGB-IR) target detection provides an effective all-weather solution for robust perception in complex environments. However, existing methods lack sufficient adaptability to the physical property differences between modalities during feature extraction, making it challenging to fully exploit complementary information. Meanwhile, most cross-modal fusion mechanisms suffer from limited expressive capacity or parametric redundancy. To address these challenges, this paper proposes a differential fusion detection network (DFDNet). The core contribution of this work is to depart from the homogenized feature extraction paradigm of existing methods by constructing a dual-branch-specific feature extraction architecture tailored to the physical properties of each modality. For the infrared modality, we design a conditional routing convolution (CRConv) and construct a thermal-kinetic feature extraction (TKFE) module. This module dynamically adapts to the thermal radiation characteristics of different objects, significantly enhancing the representation of critical thermal targets. For the visible modality, we propose a multi-granularity visual representation (MVR) module, which captures diverse visual features ranging from detailed textures to macroscopic silhouettes, thereby enhancing the discriminative properties of object shapes and textures. Furthermore, to efficiently integrate the heterogeneous features extracted from these specialized branches, we propose a collaborative weight decision (CWD) module. Instead of employing complex interactions like previous methods, this module adopts a dynamic weight prediction mechanism. It enables adaptive feature fusion with a parameter count comparable to a simple concatenation operation, achieving an excellent balance between efficiency and effectiveness. Extensive experiments on the VEDAI, DroneVehicle, and FLIR Aligned datasets validate the effectiveness of DFDNet. It achieves mAP@0.5 scores of 73.4%, 82.3%, and 75.4%, respectively, outperforming many existing methods by a significant margin. Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (UAV) small object detection remains challenging due to extreme scale variation, dense spatial distribution, and frequent occlusion in aerial images. Small objects often occupy very limited pixel regions and exhibit ambiguous structural boundaries, making their discriminative features easily overwhelmed by background clutter. These challenges lead to unstable multi scale representation and degraded localization precision in existing detectors. To address these challenges, combining Mamba and YOLO, we propose the HEdge-MamYOLO, which consists of three components. First, we design Frequency-Mamba Collaborative High-Frequency Enhancement Module (FM-CHFEM) to extract high-frequency components of enhanced visible edges from occluded objects in the frequency domain and leverage Mamba global selective scanning combined with spatial similarity to unoccluded objects of the same type to achieve feature enhancement and recognition of occluded objects. Second, we propose the Dynamic Scale Feature Fusion Module (DSFFM), by covering large-scale objects and adaptively matching small-scale object features, achieves balanced capture of objects at different scales under FM-CHFEM-based feature extraction. Finally, we design the Lightweight Low-Level Feature Fusion Head (LLFFH) by integrating high-resolution low-level features with DSFFM’s scale-adaptive features, it sparsely processes feature maps via partial convolution. This approach preserves details of small objects, and reduces computational overhead. Extensive experiments on the VisDrone2019 and UAVDT aerial image datasets, with mAP50 metrics reaching 52.5%and 33.4% respectively, which is better than other SOTA models, demonstrate the effectiveness of our proposed method.
