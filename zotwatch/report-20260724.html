SAR Vessel Detection and Gross Tonnage Estimation from Heterogeneous Datasets for Dark Vessel Identification

基于异构数据集的SAR舰船检测与总吨位估计用于暗船识别

2026-07-20
arXiv

Davide Paltrinieri，Andrea Diecidue，Roberto Basla，Daniele Casciani，Piero Fraternali 等

ID: http://arxiv.org/abs/2607.18051v1

Detecting vessels engaging in illegal activities is of paramount importance for maritime security. One of the major goals is to detect dark vessels, ships that disable their transponders to evade surveillance. Deep Learning (DL) models can detect vessels in Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) images, enabling maritime traffic analysis regardless of weather or visibility conditions. However, to detect potential dark vessels, a DL model must select only those that are required to carry a transponder based on their Gross Tonnage (GT). Unfortunately, no public SAR dataset is available for training an end-to-end DL model for vessel detection and GT regression. In this work, we present a framework that leverages heterogeneous image and tabular datasets to solve this task. Our solution combines a multi-task DL framework for predicting the location, vessel type, and physical dimensions of ships, cascaded with a non-parametric model for predicting GT from vessel size and category. We perform GT regression by a KNN that measures sample similarity using a hybrid Euclidean and categorical distance. Experiments show that our solution can predict multiple outputs while remaining competitive with state-of-the-art models on individual subtasks, thus enabling the identification of dark vessels. We publish our code on GitHub https://github.com/PaltrinieriDavide/vesseldetection.

Spectral-Spatial Synergistic Learning and Context-Aware Edge Enhancement for Efficient Oriented Object Detection in SAR Images

光谱-空间协同学习与上下文感知边缘增强用于SAR图像中的高效定向目标检测

2026-07-23
IEEE Journal of Selected Topics in Applied Earth Observations and Remote Sensing (IF: 5.5)

Yihui Zhan，Jincheng Li，Yuanyuan Shen，Xing Xie

DOI: 10.1109/jstars.2026.3716641

Oriented object detection in Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) imagery remains challenging because speckle noise, blurred target boundaries, strong clutter, and large-aspect-ratio objects often degrade localization accuracy and increase false alarms. Existing detectors frequently struggle to balance noise suppression, boundary preservation, and context modeling for reliable oriented localization. To address these issues, this paper proposes an oriented SAR object detector based on spectral spatial synergistic learning and context-aware edge enhancement. Specifically, a Smoothed Gradient Enhancement (SGE) module integrates differentiable Gaussian smoothing and Scharr gradient extraction to enhance blurred target boundaries while reducing spurious edge responses. A Dual-Domain Denoising (D3) block jointly models spatial- and frequency-domain features through reparameterized spatial convolutions and Spectral Feature Modulation (SFM), thereby suppressing clutter while preserving structural information. In addition, an existing Large Selective Kernel (LSK) block is incorporated to adaptively model long-range contextual dependencies for multi-scale and large aspect-ratio targets. Extensive experiments are conducted on RSAR, SSDD, and HRSID. On RSAR, the proposed method achieves 92.88% mAP50 and 64.70% mAP50:95 with 32.94 M parameters and 14.08 FPS on the test set. On SSDD and HRSID, it further obtains 99.1%/81.0% and 92.0%/66.6% in terms of mAP50/mAP50:95, respectively. Additional per-class analysis, ablation studies, hyperparameter sensitivity experiments, and failure-case visualizations demonstrate that the proposed design improves oriented localization robustness while revealing remaining limitations under extremely strong clutter, very small targets, and dense target distributions. Code is available at https://github.com/ZhanYiHui06/JSTARS.

Manifold-aware Multiview Evidence Fusion for Robust PolSAR Image Classification

流形感知的多视图证据融合用于鲁棒PolSAR图像分类

2026-07-23
IEEE Transactions on Circuits and Systems for Video Technology (IF: 11.1)

Junfei Shi，Haojia Zhang，Haiyan Jin，Junhuai Li，Maoguo Gong 等

DOI: 10.1109/tcsvt.2026.3716446

Deep learning excels at automatic feature extraction from Polarimetric Synthetic Aperture Radar (PolSAR) data, yet it ignores the derived multi-features (e.g., scattering angle, entropy, texture), which can offer crucial, physically interpretable information for classification. Traditional fusion methods, relying on feature concatenation or standard network fusion followed by a softmax classifier, face two major limitations: 1) Geometric Incompatibility: Feature concatenation is inaccurate because PolSAR covariance matrices reside on a Hermitian Positive Definite (HPD) manifold, while the derived multi-features are best represented on a Grassmann manifold. 2) Decision Instability: The softmax classifier treats all features equally, leading to unreliable predictions, especially under speckle noise. To overcome these challenges, we propose a novel Manifold-aware Multiview Evidence Fusion Framework (MMEFNet) to integrate these two complementary feature views in a geometry-consistent and uncertainty-aware manner. To learn geometric structures, we construct two superpixel-based graph convolutional networks—HPD-sGCN and Grassmann-sGCN—each operating on its native manifold using dedicated kernel metrics. To enhance decision reliability, the conventional softmax layer is replaced with a Dempster–Shafer Theory (DST)–based evidence fusion module, which jointly models class belief and uncertainty while aggregating evidence from both views. Experimental results on three real-world PolSAR datasets demonstrate that the MVGCN framework achieves superior performance in terms of accuracy, robustness, and interpretability compared to state-of-the-art PolSAR classification methods.

创新点：首次在流形感知框架中结合HPD和Grassmann图卷积及Dempster-Shafer融合。

相关性：为遥感领域提供了一种几何一致、不确定性感知的多视图融合范式。

研究背景：极化合成孔径雷达（PolSAR）数据分类中，深度学习擅长自动特征提取，但忽视了散射角、熵、纹理等多特征提供的物理可解释信息。传统融合方法面临几何不兼容（PolSAR协方差矩阵位于Hermitian正定流形，而多特征位于Grassmann流形）和决策不稳定（softmax分类器对所有特征平等对待，易受散斑噪声影响）两大问题。

方法详情：提出了一种流形感知的多视图证据融合框架（MMEFNet），通过构建两个基于超像素的图卷积网络（HPD-sGCN和Grassmann-sGCN）分别在其原生流形上学习几何结构。用Dempster–Shafer理论（DST）证据融合模块替代传统softmax层，联合建模类别信念和不确定性，从两个视图聚合证据。

研究结果：在三个真实PolSAR数据集上的实验表明，所提框架在分类精度、鲁棒性和可解释性方面均优于现有方法。通过流形感知和不确定性建模，有效解决了特征几何不匹配和决策不可靠问题。

局限性：超像素分割的质量可能影响图卷积网络的性能；DST融合的计算复杂度较高，可能限制在大规模实时场景中的应用。此外，方法对多特征选择的依赖未充分探讨。

未来方向：未来可探索更高效的不确定性融合机制，或引入自适应超像素生成策略；也可将框架扩展到其他遥感数据（如多光谱或高光谱图像）的流形融合问题。

HARC-Net: Hierarchical Multi-Axis Representation and Adaptive Residual Calibration for End-to-End SAR-to-Optical Image Translation

HARC-Net：层次化多轴表示与自适应残差校准用于端到端SAR到光学图像翻译

2026-07-22
IEEE Journal of Selected Topics in Applied Earth Observations and Remote Sensing (IF: 5.5)

Yanghang Zhu，Zhijie Zhang，Mingsheng Huang，Yong Zhang

DOI: 10.1109/jstars.2026.3715999

Synthetic aperture radar (SAR) enables all-weather Earth observation; however, its inherent multiplicative speckle noise and geometry-dependent distortions pose significant challenges for SAR-to-optical image translation (S2OIT), often leading to structural deformation and degraded texture fidelity. To address these issues, this paper presents HARC-Net, an end-to end Transformer-based regression framework that combines hierarchical multi-axis representation learning with statistics guided skip-feature calibration to improve robustness and reconstruction quality. At the core of the proposed approach is a Variable-Axis Sparse Transformer (VASTormer) encoder, which integrates convolutional inductive bias with hierarchical multi axis attention, including local block attention and sparse grid attention. This task-oriented encoder design enables efficient modeling of long-range dependencies while maintaining stable feature representations under speckle perturbations. To mitigate noise propagation in U-shaped architectures, we further introduce an Adaptive Dual Attention and Residual Calibration (ADARC) module for skip connections.
