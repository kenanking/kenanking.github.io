Robust SAR Ship Detection with Texture Perception Convolution and Region Balanced Sampler

纹理感知卷积和区域平衡采样器的鲁棒SAR舰船检测

2026-06-02
Remote Sensing (IF: 4.1)

Hangyu Cao，Zhao Chen

DOI: 10.3390/rs18111807

High-resolution synthetic aperture radar (SAR) ship detection plays a pivotal role in maritime surveillance and ocean monitoring. However, it remains challenging in practice because of the single-channel imaging modality, severe multiplicative speckle noise, and the pronounced scale imbalance in which sparse large vessels are easily under-optimized compared to the dominant small and medium instances. In this paper, we propose a Texture Perception Convolution Network (TPCNet), a practical and reproducible detection framework to improve feature extraction robustness and high-IoU localization under a unified strict-COCO evaluation protocol. TPCNet begins with a lightweight texture perception convolution (TPConv) that augments the raw SAR intensity with a local fluctuation cue to stabilize early feature representations for SAR images affected by strong multiplicative speckle noise (speckle-rich imagery). To address scale skew during training without modifying dataset splits, a region-balanced sampler (RBS) is introduced to increase the sampling probability of images, thereby improving the effective exposure of informative large-target structures. A background similarity augmentation (BSA) is proposed to enrich medium and large instances while reducing unrealistic boundary artifacts via compatible background selection and soft blending. Beyond component-level designs, the high-IoU localization is highly sensitive to geometric perturbations. Accordingly, TPCNet adopts a two-stage localization-oriented training strategy that first learns robust multi-scale representations and then refines box regression by tightening translation and scale ranges during fine-tuning. Under strict-COCO settings, TPCNet achieves SOTA performance with an AP of 71.20% on HRSID and an AP of 72.7% on SSDD. Comprehensive ablation studies demonstrate that TPConv, RBS, BSA, and the proposed finetuning strategy contribute complementary gains, providing a transparent baseline and a strong recipe for future SAR ship detection research.

Phy-OSShip-MS Dataset: A Paired Optical-SAR Ship Imagery Generation Method Via Hierarchical Physics-Guided Style Transfer

Phy-OSShip-MS数据集：一种基于分层物理引导风格迁移的光学- SAR舰船图像对生成方法

2026-06-02
IEEE Journal of Selected Topics in Applied Earth Observations and Remote Sensing (IF: 5.5)

Jing Li，Yan Zhang，Zhuanze Yang，Chao Song，Ranhui Yuan 等

DOI: 10.1109/jstars.2026.3699023

High-quality spatiotemporally paired optical and Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) datasets are fundamental for cross-modal ship detection tasks. However, due to distinct imaging mechanisms, difficulties in acquiring spatiotemporal consistency, and data privacy concerns, such public datasets are extremely scarce. Current cross-modal SAR image synthesis methods often struggle to balance physical consistency and perceptual fidelity, resulting in images with scattering distributions that violate imaging mechanisms and speckle statistics that deviate from real data. To address these challenges, this article proposes a Hierarchical Physics-Guided Style Transfer framework for Optical-to-SAR image generation. In this architecture, style transfer serves as an underlying generative backbone to ensure perceptual fidelity, while a novel “Radiometric-Geometric-Fusion” three-level physical guidance strategy is introduced to explicitly constrain the generation process. First, a scene-universal category-level radiometric prior is constructed to establish physical intensity references for different categories. Second, phase congruency features are introduced to compensate for the deficiency of radiometric priors in fine structural depiction, precisely preserving the critical geometric details of strong scattering targets like ships. Finally, a synergistic fusion mechanism based on physical attribute intersection is established to apply directional gain to strong scattering targets by coupling material attributes with geometric saliency, thereby ensuring intensity-level physical consistency. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms existing image-to-image translation models in terms of texture details and scattering characteristics. Consequently, we establish a Physics-guided Optical-SAR Ship Imagery dataset with Multi-Source attributes (Phy-OSShip-MS), providing target-level synthetically paired samples as an effective generative data augmentation strategy...

研究背景：光学和合成孔径雷达（SAR）图像由于成像机制不同，时空一致配对数据难以获取，且存在数据隐私问题，导致跨模态船只检测所需的高质量配对数据集极度稀缺。现有SAR图像合成方法在物理一致性（如散射分布和散斑统计）与感知保真度之间难以平衡，亟需一种既能保证视觉真实感又能严格遵循物理成像规律的生成框架。

方法详情：提出分层物理引导风格迁移（Hierarchical Physics-Guided Style Transfer）框架，以风格迁移作为生成骨干确保感知保真度，并引入“辐射-几何-融合”三级物理引导策略：首先构建场景通用的类别级辐射先验，为不同地物提供物理强度参考；其次利用相位一致性特征补偿辐射先验在精细结构描述上的不足，保留船只等强散射目标的几何细节；最后通过物理属性交集协同融合机制，耦合材料属性与几何显著性对强散射目标施加方向增益，保证强度级物理一致性。

研究结果：实验表明，该方法在纹理细节和散射特性上显著优于现有图像翻译模型，生成的SAR图像在散斑统计和散射分布上更接近真实数据。基于此建立了Phy-OSShip-MS数据集，提供目标级合成配对样本，作为一种有效的数据增强策略，有助于提升跨模态船只检测性能。

局限性：物理引导策略依赖先验知识的准确性和完备性，对于非常见场景或新型目标可能存在偏差；生成图像与真实SAR图像在复杂环境（如海杂波、极化信息）下的差异仍有待进一步缩小。此外，框架的计算复杂度较高，可能限制其在大规模应用中的效率。

未来方向：未来可探索更精细的物理模型（如电磁散射仿真）与深度学习融合，提升物理一致性；扩展至多时相、多极化SAR图像生成，并研究生成数据与真实数据混合训练的最优策略。

DarkVesselNet: Multi-Modal Remote Sensing and Trajectory Reasoning for Dark Vessel Detection

DarkVesselNet: 基于多模态遥感与轨迹推理的暗船检测

2026-05-30
arXiv

Arun Sharma

ID: http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.00445v1

Dark vessel detection requires fusing what vessels report through AIS with what satellites observe through radar and optical sensors. DarkVesselNet is a multi-modal remote sensing stack that combines Sentinel-1 SAR, Sentinel-2 optical imagery, geospatial foundation model backbones, AIS trajectory reasoning, TGARD-style gap detection, and a Pi-DPM-inspired anomaly head. The repository exposes the system as a tested Python package and a public Hugging Face Space. The paper presents the sensor stack, backbone abstraction, fusion path, anomaly head, and current validation. The evidence currently available is software-grounded: tests for SAR speckle filtering, optical band ratios, Haversine distance, TGARD gap emission, sensor coregistration, backbone token shapes, and differentiable anomaly scoring.
