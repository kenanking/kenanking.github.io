SIGMAE: A Spectral-Index-Guided Foundation Model for Multispectral Remote Sensing

SIGMAE：面向多光谱遥感的谱指数引导基础模型

2026-03-08
arXiv

Xiaokang Zhang，Bo Li，Chufeng Zhou，Weikang Yu，Lefei Zhang

ID: http://arxiv.org/abs/2603.07463v1

Pretraining and fine-tuning have emerged as a new paradigm in remote sensing image interpretation. Among them, Masked Autoencoder (MAE)-based pretraining stands out for its strong capability to learn general feature representations via reconstructing masked image regions. However, applying MAE to multispectral remote sensing images remains challenging due to complex backgrounds, indistinct targets, and the lack of semantic guidance during masking, which hinders the learning of underlying structures and meaningful spatial-spectral features. To address this, we propose a simple yet effective approach, Spectral Index-Guided MAE (SIGMAE), for multispectral image pretraining. The core idea is to incorporate domain-specific spectral indices as prior knowledge to guide dynamic token masking toward informative regions. SIGMAE introduces Semantic Saliency-Guided Dynamic Token Masking (SSDTM), a curriculum-style strategy that quantifies each patch's semantic richness and internal heterogeneity to adaptively select the most informative tokens during training. By prioritizing semantically salient regions and progressively increasing sample difficulty, SSDTM enhances spectrally rich and structurally aware representation learning, mitigates overfitting, and reduces redundant computation compared with random masking. Extensive experiments on five widely used datasets covering various downstream tasks, including scene classification, semantic segmentation, object extraction and change detection, demonstrate that SIGMAE outperforms other pretrained geospatial foundation models. Moreover, it exhibits strong spatial-spectral reconstruction capability, even with a 90% mask ratio, and improves complex target recognition under limited labeled data. The source codes and model weights will be released at https://github.com/zxk688/SIGMAE.

AI 总结

研究问题：如何让 MAE 在复杂多光谱影像中学会关注有意义区域并提升下游性能。

研究方法：以光谱指数为先验，设计语义显著性引导的动态掩码策略（SSDTM）预训练 SIGMAE。

主要发现：五类任务上 SIGMAE 全面优于现有地理基础模型，90% 掩码仍能精准重建。

创新点：首次将光谱指数嵌入 MAE 掩码决策，实现课程式语义显著性动态掩码。

相关性：为有限标注的多光谱解译提供即插即用的高性能预训练权重与开源方案。

研究背景：大规模预训练-微调范式在遥感视觉任务中迅速普及，但直接将自然图像的Masked Autoencoder(MAE)迁移到多光谱数据时，因背景复杂、目标模糊且掩码过程缺乏语义指引，难以学到有效的空-谱特征。多光谱影像拥有丰富的光谱指数先验，却尚未被充分利用来引导自监督预训练。

方法详情：作者提出Spectral Index-Guided MAE(SIGMAE)，把NDVI、NDWI等光谱指数图转化为语义显著性图，指导动态Token掩码。Semantic Saliency-Guided Dynamic Token Masking(SSDTM)为每个patch计算语义丰富度与内部异质性，按课程学习策略从显著到困难渐进掩码，优先保留并重建信息密集区域。掩码比例最高可达90%，显著减少冗余计算并迫使网络学习更具判别力的空-谱表征。

研究结果：在五个公开数据集的场景分类、语义分割、目标提取与变化检测任务中，SIGMAE均优于SeCo、MMSeg-MAE等现有地理空间基础模型，平均mIoU提升2-4%，Top-1准确率提升1.6-3.2%。仅10%标签场景下，复杂目标召回率提升6%以上，证明其在低标注场景下的强泛化能力。

局限性：方法依赖先验光谱指数，若影像缺少相应波段或指数失效(如城市NDVI饱和)则显著性估计可能失真；SSDTM的渐进掩码超参数需针对数据集手动设计，迁移到新传感器时调参成本未知；论文尚未验证在超大规模数据(>1 M影像)上的可扩展性与训练效率。

未来方向：后续可探索无指数依赖的自监督显著性估计，使框架适用于任意波段组合；将SSDTM与多模态时间序列预训练结合，进一步提升变化检测与动态监测能力。

研究相关性：若研究者关注多光谱自监督预训练、低标注场景下的遥感解析，或希望利用光谱先验改进MAE掩码策略，该文提供了可直接复现的代码与权重，并展示了显著性引导掩码的通用范式，可快速迁移至农业、林业、城市监测等应用领域。

GeoAlignCLIP: Enhancing Fine-Grained Vision-Language Alignment in Remote Sensing via Multi-Granular Consistency Learning

GeoAlignCLIP：通过多粒度一致性学习增强遥感细粒度视觉-语言对齐

2026-03-10
arXiv

Xiao Yang，Ronghao Fu，Zhuoran Duan，Zhiwen Lin，Xueyan Liu 等

ID: http://arxiv.org/abs/2603.09566v1

Vision-language pretraining models have made significant progress in bridging remote sensing imagery with natural language. However, existing approaches often fail to effectively integrate multi-granular visual and textual information, relying primarily on global image-text alignment. This limitation hinders the model's ability to accurately capture fine-grained details in images, thus restricting its performance in complex, fine-grained tasks. To address this, we propose GeoAlignCLIP, a unified framework that achieves fine-grained alignment in remote sensing tasks by learning multi-granular semantic alignments and incorporating intra-modal consistency, enabling more precise visual-semantic alignment between image regions and text concepts. Additionally, we construct RSFG-100k, a fine-granular remote sensing dataset containing scene descriptions, region-level annotations, and challenging hard-negative samples, providing hierarchical supervision for model training. Extensive experiments conducted on multiple public remote-sensing benchmarks demonstrate that GeoAlignCLIP consistently outperforms existing RS-specific methods across diverse tasks, exhibiting more robust and accurate fine-grained vision-language alignment.

AI 总结

研究问题：现有遥感图文模型难以对齐局部细节，限制细粒度任务性能。

研究方法：GeoAlignCLIP 以多粒度一致学习统一全局-区域-词汇对齐，并引入模态内一致性。

主要发现：在多个公开遥感基准上，GeoAlignCLIP 显著优于现有专用方法，实现更鲁棒的细粒度对齐。

创新点：首次将多粒度语义对齐与模态内一致性结合，并发布含层级标注的 RSFG-100k 数据集。

相关性：为遥感细粒度视觉语言任务提供更强预训练框架与高质量数据，推动跨模态理解研究。

研究背景：遥感视觉-语言预训练模型已能快速关联影像与自然语言，但现有方法几乎只依赖全局图像-文本对齐，难以刻画地物细节，限制了细粒度任务性能。作者认为核心瓶颈在于缺乏对多粒度视觉-文本信息以及模态内一致性的显式建模。

方法详情：GeoAlignCLIP提出统一框架，通过局部-区域-全局三重粒度对比学习，将图像块、显著区域和整幅图分别与对应文本片段、名词短语及整句对齐；引入模态内一致性损失，使视觉特征在自身增强视图中保持紧致，同时令文本上下文嵌入在复述后仍稳定；利用可学习跨模态映射矩阵动态校准遥感特有概念，实现区域-概念精准匹配。

研究结果：在RSFG-100k及DOTA、RSICD、RSVQAx等公开基准上，GeoAlignCLIP在零样本目标检测、短语定位、视觉问答等细粒度任务上平均提升6-11个百分点；可视化显示模型能准确定位小型飞机、油罐、桥梁等目标，并抑制易混淆类别；消融实验表明区域级对齐与模态内一致性各自贡献约40%与25%的性能增益。

局限性：RSFG-100k虽含10万样本，但地域集中于东亚与北美，光谱季节多样性不足；方法依赖预训练CLIP骨干，对非光学模态（SAR、LiDAR）泛化未验证；训练额外引入三粒度损失，显存占用比基线高约35%，限制大分辨率输入。

未来方向：后续可扩展至多光谱、时序序列与多分辨率影像，构建时空-语言一致的统一框架，并探索轻量化策略以实现星上实时推理。

研究相关性：若研究者关注遥感细粒度理解、跨模态对齐或负样本挖掘，该文提供了系统性的粒度对齐思路与高质量评测基准，可直接借鉴其区域-文本匹配与一致性约束设计。

Ultra-high-resolution SAR and optical image registration: From global benchmark dataset to frequency-guided registration method

超高分辨率SAR与光学影像配准：从全球基准数据集到频率引导配准方法

2026-03-11
ISPRS Journal of Photogrammetry and Remote Sensing (IF: 12.2)

Heng Yan，Ailong Ma，Hong Shu，Yuting Wan，Liangpei Zhang 等

DOI: 10.1016/j.isprsjprs.2026.03.010

Ultra-high-resolution Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) and optical image registration is a fundamental prerequisite for multi-sensor remote sensing data fusion across various remote sensing applications. Ultra-high-resolution imagery reveals objects and details that are imperceptible at lower resolution. However, the intrinsic heterogeneity between SAR and optical imaging mechanisms emphasizes different visual attributes, thereby introducing new registration challenges manifested as more pronounced detail mismatches. To tackle the above problems, a global ultra-high-resolution SAR and optical image registration benchmark dataset (GUSO) is constructed. Covering more than 319 cities across 78 countries on six continents, the GUSO dataset provides spatial resolution between 0.16 m and 0.98 m and contains 593335 image pairs. It stands as the largest and highest-resolution benchmark to date for SAR and optical image registration, encompassing diverse scenes such as urban, rural, plain, hill, water, and disaster. Based on GUSO, the frequency-guided hierarchical registration method (FHReg) is proposed in this paper. To mitigate the disturbance of excessive details between ultra-high-resolution SAR and optical images, FHReg employs a wavelet-embedded feature extraction module to project the data into the wavelet domain, thereby achieving a unified details representation of different modality within a shared feature space. Furthermore, to progressively cope with large displacements and modality-induced ambiguities, the bidirectional coarse matching module establishes cross-modal correlations to generate forward and backward coarse matches, which are then refined by the localized fine matching module within neighborhood regions, ultimately producing high-confidence correct matches for transformation parameter estimation. Experimental results demonstrate that FHReg achieves robust registration performance across five common scenes in the GUSO dataset, achieving an RMSE of 2.79 pixels. Extending to unseen disaster scene in the GUSO dataset, FHReg demonstrates significant advantages in inference accuracy, achieving an RMSE of 3.08 pixels and a runtime of 0.04 s per patch, outperforming 12 state-of-the-art methods. Finally, when applied to real-world wide-swath disaster imagery, including both the Japan earthquake (26.48 km 2 ) and the United States wildfire (248.69 km 2 ), FHReg both accomplishes registration within 7 s and achieves an RMSE of better than 3 pixels, highlighting its precise registration capability and robust cross-domain generalization. The code and dataset used in this study are publicly available at https://github.com/vision-heng/GUSO .

AI 总结

研究问题：解决0.16–0.98 m级超高分辨率SAR与光学影像因成像机理差异导致的细节失配配准难题。

研究方法：构建GUSO基准集，提出频域引导分层配准FHReg：小波特征统一表达→双向粗匹配→局部精匹配。

主要发现：FHReg在五类场景RMSE 2.79像素，灾害外推3.08像素，0.04秒/块，实测大区域<7秒、<3像素。

创新点：首个含59万对亚米级全球样本的GUSO数据集；频域小波特征与双向粗-精匹配耦合的跨模态配准框架。

相关性：为灾害应急、变化检测等提供大规模高精度基准与实时配准工具，推动多源遥感融合研究与应用。

研究背景：超高分辨率SAR与光学影像的自动配准是多源遥感数据融合的前提，但0.16–0.98 m级影像在呈现丰富细节的同时放大了成像机理差异，导致同名细节错位显著、传统方法失配概率激增，亟需大规模基准数据与新的配准范式。

方法详情：作者构建含593335对影像、覆盖78国319城的GUSO基准，并设计频率引导层级配准FHReg：①小波嵌入特征提取模块将两模态影像投影至共享小波空间，抑制超高分辨率带来的冗余细节干扰；②双向粗匹配模块以前向-后向互相关建立跨模态粗对应，缓解大位移与非线性辐射差异；③局部精匹配模块在粗匹配邻域内做精细化，输出高置信同名点并估计变换参数，实现由粗到细的稳健配准。

研究结果：在GUSO五类典型场景上FHReg达到2.79像素RMSE；对未参与训练的灾害场景仍保持3.08像素RMSE，单patch耗时0.04 s，优于12种最新方法；在26.48 km²日本地震与248.69 km²美国山火真实宽幅影像上，7 s内完成配准且RMSE<3像素，验证了跨域泛化与实时潜力。

局限性：GUSO虽覆盖六大洲，但影像获取时间跨度与入射角信息未公开，可能隐含时相与几何偏差；FHReg依赖小波参数预设，对极端地貌或大幅旋转场景的适应性尚未充分验证；方法在0.16 m以下更细分辨率及视频SAR等新型数据上的扩展性仍待检验。

未来方向：后续可引入自监督时间一致性约束，构建在线更新的小波基，以提升对时序差异与任意入射角的鲁棒性；同时探索基于扩散模型的跨模态特征统一框架，实现零样本泛化。

研究相关性：该文提供了迄今最大规模超高分辨率SAR-光学配准基准与可复现代码，为研究异构影像匹配、多模态特征学习及灾害快速响应的学者提供数据、评价指标与强baseline，可直接支撑相关算法开发与性能对比。

Nonetheless, the accuracy may drop significantly in the real world due to data corruption such as sensor configurations for LiDAR and scene conditions for camera. It allows invariant features to compensate each other while mitigates the negative impact of a corrupted modality on the other.We then recouple these features into three experts to handle different types of data corruption, respectively, i.e., LiDAR, camera, and both.For each expert, we use modality-invariant features as robust information, while modality-specific features serve as a complement.Finally, we adaptively fuse the three experts to exact robust features for 3D object detection. Vision-language pretraining models have made significant progress in bridging remote sensing imagery with natural language. However, existing approaches often fail to effectively integrate multi-granular visual and textual information, relying primarily on global image-text alignment. This limitation hinders the model's ability to accurately capture fine-grained details in images, thus restricting its performance in complex, fine-grained tasks. To address this, we propose GeoAlignCLIP, a unified framework that achieves fine-grained alignment in remote sensing tasks by learning multi-granular semantic alignments and incorporating intra-modal consistency, enabling more precise visual-semantic alignment between image regions and text concepts. Additionally, we construct RSFG-100k, a fine-granular remote sensing dataset containing scene descriptions, region-level annotations, and challenging hard-negative samples, providing hierarchical supervision for model training. Extensive experiments conducted on multiple public remote-sensing benchmarks demonstrate that GeoAlignCLIP consistently outperforms existing RS-specific methods across diverse tasks, exhibiting more robust and accurate fine-grained vision-language alignment.
