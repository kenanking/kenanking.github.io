FEDA-Net: A Feature Enhancement and Dynamic Alignment Network for SAR Ship Detection

FEDA-Net：用于SAR船舶检测的特征增强与动态对齐网络

2026-03-23
IEEE Journal of Selected Topics in Applied Earth Observations and Remote Sensing (IF: 5.5)

Shuaiqi Liu，Yue Yu，Ruiyi Feng，Bing Li，Yudong Zhang 等

DOI: 10.1109/jstars.2026.3676630

Due to the imaging mechanism of synthetic aperture radar (SAR), speckle noise often obscures details of the ship and leads to the loss of semantic information. Meanwhile, the presence of targets at extremely diverse scales challenges the capacity of single-feature representation. In addition, geometric deformations of ships tend to induce localization inaccuracies and reduce classification confidence. These issues collectively aggravate the competition and imbalance between classification and localization tasks. In response to these challenges, we propose a feature enhancement and dynamic alignment network (FEDA-Net) to improve ship detection in SAR images. First, a lightweight co-attention network (LCA-Net) is designed, which synergistically captures both local and global multi-scale features through a multi-level convolutional architecture and a dual-dimensional attention mechanism, thereby enhancing the extraction of noise-robust multi-scale features. Second, a cross-stage fusion pyramid network (CSFPN) is constructed to achieve complementary integration of semantic and detailed information via bidirectional feature fusion. During training, the fusion module adopts a multi-branch topology to enrich multi-scale feature information, while during inference, it is converted into a single-branch structure through structural re-parameterization, effectively reducing parameter redundancy. Finally, we innovatively propose a dynamic alignment detection head (DADH), which adopts parameter sharing and adaptive task-decoupling strategies to separate the classification and localization branches, while incorporating a bidirectional interaction mechanism to effectively balance the competition between the two tasks. On the SSDD and HRSID datasets, the proposed method achieves accuracies of 99.1% and 93.2%, respectively, while achieving a reduction in model parameters. Furthermore, the model demonstrates superior generalization performance on the large-scale LS-SSDD-v1.0 dataset.

研究结果：在SSDD与HRSID公开数据集上，FEDA-Net分别取得99.1%与93.2%的检测精度，同时参数量低于对比方法；在更大规模的LS-SSDD-v1.0上也展现出良好的泛化能力，验证了其在复杂场景下的鲁棒性与可扩展性。

局限性：论文未讨论极端侧视、高海况或密集停靠场景下的性能，且结构重参数化带来的推理加速与量化部署兼容性尚未评估；另外，动态对齐头引入的额外计算在星载或边缘终端的实时性需求可能受限。

未来方向：后续可探索基于神经架构搜索的轻量化设计，并引入自监督预训练以进一步降低对标注数据的依赖；同时结合量化与剪枝技术，实现星载实时检测。

Rethinking SAR ATR: A Target-Aware Frequency-Spatial Enhancement Framework with Noise-Resilient Knowledge Guidance

重新审视 SAR ATR：一种具备抗噪知识引导的目标感知频-空增强框架

2026-03-23
arXiv

Yansong Lin，Zihan Cheng，Jielei Wang，Guoming Lua，Zongyong Cui

ID: http://arxiv.org/abs/2603.21565v1

Synthetic aperture radar automatic target recognition (SAR ATR) is of considerable importance in marine navigation and disaster monitoring. However, the coherent speckle noise inherent in SAR imagery often obscures salient target features, leading to degraded recognition accuracy and limited model generalization. To address this issue, this paper proposes a target-aware frequency-spatial enhancement framework with noise-resilient knowledge guidance (FSCE) for SAR target recognition. The proposed framework incorporates a frequency-spatial shallow feature adaptive enhancement (DSAF) module, which processes shallow features through spatial multi-scale convolution and frequency-domain wavelet convolution. In addition, a teacher-student learning paradigm combined with an online knowledge distillation method (KD) is employed to guide the student network to focus more effectively on target regions, thereby enhancing its robustness to high-noise backgrounds. Through the collaborative optimization of attention transfer and noise-resilient representation learning, the proposed approach significantly improves the stability of target recognition under noisy conditions. Based on the FSCE framework, two network architectures with different performance emphases are developed: lightweight DSAFNet-M and high-precision DSAFNet-L. Extensive experiments are conducted on the MSTAR, FUSARShip and OpenSARShip datasets. The results show that DSAFNet-L achieves competitive or superior performance compared with various methods on three datasets; DSAFNet-M significantly reduces the model complexity while maintaining comparable accuracy. These results indicate that the proposed FSCE framework exhibits strong cross-model generalization.

LSDN: A Lightweight Dual-Domain Attention Network with Adaptive Anchors for Ship Detection in Remote Sensing Scenarios

LSDN：面向遥感场景船舶检测的轻量级双域注意力网络与自适应锚框

2026-03-23
IEEE Transactions on Geoscience and Remote Sensing (IF: 8.6)

Shounan Wang，Chen Jia，Hui Liu，Yifan Yin，Xu Cheng 等

DOI: 10.1109/tgrs.2026.3676813

Detecting small target ships (with pixel dimensions below 32×32) in remote sensing imagery is crucial for maintaining maritime traffic safety. However, achieving high accuracy with low computational cost remains challenging due to the dense scale distribution and the weak visual cues of small ship targets. To address these limitations, we propose a novel Lightweight Ship Detection Network (LSDN), which is capable of accurately localizing small ships in multi-scale scenarios within remote sensing images, while maintaining a low parameter count and computational cost. Specifically, LSDN integrates three core components: First, the Adaptive Feature Enhancement Module (AFEM) employs a multi-level feature fusion strategy to enhance shallow feature representation, effectively preserving relevant information for small targets. Second, a lightweight backbone network MESA-ShipDetection incorporates a Multi-scale Efficient Strengthened Attention (MESA) to dynamically modulate feature importance across spatial and scale domains. Third, a dynamic anchor generation strategy constructs three sets of learnable anchor parameters based on target size distributions, thereby optimizing bounding box matching and localization accuracy. Experiments on the Airbus-ship dataset demonstrate that LSDN achieves a detection accuracy of mAP at 81.60% with only 3.65M parameters, representing a 9.75% improvement over existing SOTA methods. Additionally, ablation studies further validate the effectiveness of each module, with the AFEM increasing the recall rate of small targets by 7.83%, and the dynamic anchor strategy improving localization accuracy by 5.49%. This study offers a practical and efficient solution for real-time ship detection in resource-constrained scenarios.

ALADIN:Attribute-Language Distillation Network for Person Re-Identification

ALADIN：用于行人重识别的属性-语言蒸馏网络

2026-03-23
arXiv

Wang Zhou，Boran Duan，Haojun Ai，Ruiqi Lan，Ziyue Zhou

ID: http://arxiv.org/abs/2603.21482v1

Recent vision-language models such as CLIP provide strong cross-modal alignment, but current CLIP-guided ReID pipelines rely on global features and fixed prompts. This limits their ability to capture fine-grained attribute cues and adapt to diverse appearances. We propose ALADIN, an attribute-language distillation network that distills knowledge from a frozen CLIP teacher to a lightweight ReID student. ALADIN introduces fine-grained attribute-local alignment to establish adaptive text-visual correspondence and robust representation learning. A Scene-Aware Prompt Generator produces image-specific soft prompts to facilitate adaptive alignment. Attribute-local distillation enforces consistency between textual attributes and local visual features, significantly enhancing robustness under occlusions. Furthermore, we employ cross-modal contrastive and relation distillation to preserve the inherent structural relationships among attributes. To provide precise supervision, we leverage Multimodal LLMs to generate structured attribute descriptions, which are then converted into localized attention maps via CLIP. At inference, only the student is used. Experiments on Market-1501, DukeMTMC-reID, and MSMT17 show improvements over CNN-, Transformer-, and CLIP-based methods, with better generalization and interpretability.

No-reference image quality assessment (NR-IQA) models are critically vulnerable to adversarial attacks, posing significant risks to downstream vision systems. Extensive experiments on synthetic (LIVE, TID2013) and authentic (CLIVE) datasets demonstrate DOR’s strong attack performance and superior transferability against leading NR-IQA models that cover diverse deep neural network architectures. Our work pioneers a diffusion model-based “completely blind” attack paradigm, offering a practical, MOS-free solution for adversarial robustness assessment of NR-IQA models in real-world deployments.
