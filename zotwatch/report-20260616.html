DEADet: Detail Enhancement with Multi-level Feature Joint Perception for Aircraft Fine-grained Object Detection

2026-06-15
IEEE Transactions on Circuits and Systems for Video Technology (IF: 11.1)

Fulin Luo，Yan Guan，Chuan Fu，Tan Guo，Yong Li 等

DOI: 10.1109/tcsvt.2026.3703851

Fine-grained aircraft detection in remote sensing images plays a crucial role in the field of military security, providing critical technical support for military reconnaissance, target tracking, and air defense early warning. However, the task suffers from both inadequate preservation of local features, which causes a loss of discriminative details, and a reliance on layer-specific feature interactions that fails to leverage cross-scale complementary information. To address these challenges, we propose a Detail Enhancement Aircraft Fine-grained Object Detection (DEADet), which is an efficient one-stage detector based on multi-level feature joint perception. First, to preserve fine-grained details, we design the Detail-Enhanced Feature Representation (DEFR) module based on a compress-enhance-reconstruct principle. Second, to achieve comprehensive cross-level feature perception and enhancement, we introduce the Multi-level Joint Feature Refiner (MJFR), which consists of a Multi-level Feature Fusion (MLFF) module and a Multi-level Feature Enhance (MLFE) module. Third, we propose the Normalized Angle and Center Loss (NAC Loss), incorporating both angle penalty and center penalty to improve orientation estimation and spatial localization, thereby guiding the model toward more precise feature learning. Extensive experiments on the FAIR1M-aircraft and MAR20 datasets demonstrate that our method achieves state-of-the-art performance with 88.87% and 85.51% mAP respectively, while maintaining high efficiency with only 23.07M parameters and 84.19G FLOPs, providing an effective solution for military remote sensing applications. The code is available at https://github.com/mysort/DEADet.

研究问题：解决遥感图像中飞机细粒度检测时局部细节丢失和跨层特征交互不足的问题。

研究方法：提出DEADet，包含细节增强特征表示、多层级联合特征优化和归一化角度中心损失。

主要发现：在FAIR1M-aircraft和MAR20数据集上分别达到88.87%和85.51% mAP，参数仅23.07M。

创新点：设计DEFR模块保留细节，MJFR模块融合多级特征，NAC Loss提升定位精度。

相关性：为军事遥感中高精度、高效率的细粒度目标检测提供了有效解决方案。

研究背景：针对遥感图像中飞机细粒度检测任务面临局部特征保留不足导致判别细节丢失、以及依赖特定层特征交互无法利用跨尺度互补信息的问题，本文提出了一种基于多级特征联合感知的细节增强飞机细粒度目标检测方法。

方法详情：首先，设计了基于压缩-增强-重建原理的细节增强特征表示模块来保留细粒度细节；其次，引入由多级特征融合模块和多级特征增强模块组成的多级联合特征细化器，实现全面的跨层特征感知与增强；最后，提出归一化角度和中心损失，通过角度惩罚和中心惩罚改进方向估计与空间定位，引导模型进行更精确的特征学习。

研究结果：在FAIR1M-aircraft和MAR20数据集上，该方法分别达到了88.87%和85.51%的mAP，同时仅需23.07M参数和84.19G FLOPs，实现了最优性能与高效率的平衡，为军事遥感应用提供了有效解决方案。

局限性：尽管在现有数据集上表现优异，但该方法可能对极端尺度变化或高度遮挡的飞机目标仍存在检测挑战；另外，归一化角度和中心损失的设计依赖于标注框的精确性，在噪声标注下可能影响性能。

LR-Depth: Enhancing Self-Supervised Monocular Depth Estimation in Low-Resolution Scenarios

2026-06-15
IEEE Transactions on Circuits and Systems for Video Technology (IF: 11.1)

Xiao Ke，Chaoying Chen，Jingyi Fang，Huanqi Wu，Yuzhen Niu

DOI: 10.1109/tcsvt.2026.3703327

Existing self-supervised monocular depth estimation methods have achieved impressive results by reducing reliance on expensive labeled data. However, their performance degrades significantly in low-resolution scenes due to challenges like missing edge details, difficulty in modeling blur regions, and inaccuracies in upsampling geometric structure reconstruction. To address this issue, we propose LR-Depth, an efficient self-supervised monocular depth estimation framework tailored for low-resolution scenes. Firstly, we propose a multi-scale wavelet edge enhancement that captures high-frequency edge information to improve depth estimation in edge regions. Meanwhile, to improve blurred regions, we leverage wavelet blur mapping to refine low-frequency component representation and enhance the modeling accuracy of depth distributions. Finally, we introduce geometric reconstruction upsampling that integrates position initialization with local offset constraints, enabling precise recovery of complex geometric structures while ensuring geometric consistency of the depth map. Extensive experiments on the KITTI dataset demonstrate that LR-Depth achieves state-of-the-art performance in low-resolution self-supervised monocular depth estimation, particularly excelling in edge clarity and depth prediction for blurred regions. Additionally, generalizability experiments on the Make3D dataset further confirm the framework’s robustness and broad applicability.

AI 总结

研究问题：解决自监督单目深度估计在低分辨率场景下性能退化的问题。

研究方法：提出LR-Depth框架，含多尺度小波边缘增强、小波模糊映射和几何重建上采样。

主要发现：在KITTI低分辨率场景下达到最先进性能，边缘清晰度和模糊区域预测显著提升。

创新点：首次将小波变换用于低分辨率自监督深度估计的边缘和模糊区域处理。

方法详情：LR-Depth框架包含三个关键模块：首先，多尺度小波边缘增强模块通过捕获高频边缘信息改善边缘区域的深度估计；其次，小波模糊映射模块利用小波变换优化低频成分表示，提高模糊区域的深度分布建模精度；最后，几何重建上采样模块结合位置初始化与局部偏移约束，在保持深度图几何一致性的同时精确恢复复杂几何结构。

研究结果：在KITTI数据集上的大量实验表明，LR-Depth在低分辨率自监督单目深度估计中达到了最先进性能，尤其在边缘清晰度和模糊区域深度预测方面表现优异。在Make3D数据集上的泛化实验进一步验证了框架的鲁棒性和广泛适用性。

局限性：论文未明确讨论低分辨率下极端模糊或遮挡场景的鲁棒性，且方法依赖小波变换，可能对噪声敏感。此外，计算复杂度未详细分析，可能影响实时应用。

未来方向：未来可探索将LR-Depth与其他自监督任务联合学习，或引入注意力机制进一步优化模糊区域建模，同时考虑在更复杂的真实低分辨率场景（如夜间、雨雾）中验证泛化能力。

LW-ResUNet: A Lightweight SAR Target Recognition Network Based on ResUNet

2026-06-15
IEEE Geoscience and Remote Sensing Letters (IF: 4.4)

Haoye Chen，Yuying Zhu

DOI: 10.1109/lgrs.2026.3703955

Efficient target recognition in Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) imagery demands models with low computational cost. Developing lightweight SAR image recognition models has therefore become a significant research focus. This paper proposes LW-ResUNet, an improved lightweight network based on the ResUNet architecture, which maintains high recognition accuracy while drastically reducing parameters. The network introduces an asymmetric encoder-decoder that concentrates computational resources on feature extraction, a Parameter-Free Cross-Branch Connection (PF-CBC) that eliminates 1×1 convolutions via channel dimension matching, and a Self-Adaptive Channel Fusion (SCAF) module embedded in depthwise separable residual blocks for adaptive feature learning. To validate the method's effectiveness, we construct the FAIR-21 dataset. Experimental results show that with only 0.895M parameters, LW-ResUNet achieves 73.2% overall accuracy on FAIR-21 and 99.13% on MSTAR, outperforming mainstream lightweight models, outperforming mainstream lightweight models. The FAIR-21 dataset is publicly available at https://github.com/angelandeagle/LW-ResUnet-dataset.

Uncertainty-Aware Keypoint Guidance and Fractional Fourier Feature Enhancement for Multi-Class SAR Aircraft Detection

2026-06-15
Remote Sensing (IF: 4.1)

Yu Qiu，Bin Zou，Fangzhou Han，Lamei Zhang，Jordi J. Mallorqui

DOI: 10.3390/rs18121969

Aircraft targets in SAR imagery often exhibit discrete scattering characteristics, significant variations in pose and scale, strong speckle noise in background clutter, and complex background interference, which jointly hinder stable structural feature extraction and accurate target localization. Existing detectors for SAR aircraft recognition primarily rely on bounding-box regression and classification; they do not completely exploit target structural cues, spatial attention, and frequency-domain information. To address these limitations, we propose a collaborative detection framework that integrates an uncertainty-aware keypoint-driven module (UAKM) with a fractional Fourier convolution backbone (S-FRConv). UAKM introduces a center-keypoint regression branch that jointly predicts keypoint coordinates and Laplacian scale parameters and employs a 2D Laplace negative log-likelihood loss to estimate uncertainty. The derived dense uncertainty heatmap is then used as spatial attention weights to guide distribution-based regression and multi-scale feature re-weighting, without requiring any additional annotations. S-FRConv embeds the Fractional Fourier Transform into shallow backbone layers and C2f modules, enabling joint spatial–spectral feature modeling that suppresses speckle noise and enhances edge and orientation representations. Experiments on the public SAR-AIRcraft-1.0 dataset demonstrate that the proposed method systematically improves the detection performance. For the Nano model, the overall mAP50 increases from 0.810 to 0.867, and the mAP 50:95 improves from 0.637 to 0.655 compared with the baseline, corresponding to gains of 5.7 and 1.8 percentage points, respectively. These results validate the effectiveness and generalization potential of combining uncertainty-driven spatial attention with fractional spectral feature enhancement for SAR aircraft target detection.

AI 总结

研究问题：如何提升SAR图像中多类飞机目标检测的性能，克服散射、噪声和干扰？

研究方法：提出UAKM关键点回归与不确定性热图注意力，及S-FRConv分数阶傅里叶卷积。

主要发现：在SAR-AIRcraft-1.0数据集上，Nano模型mAP50提升5.7个百分点至0.867。

创新点：首次结合不确定性驱动的空间注意力与分数阶傅里叶频谱增强进行SAR飞机检测。

相关性：为SAR目标检测提供新范式，改进结构特征提取与噪声抑制，具通用潜力。

研究背景：合成孔径雷达（SAR）图像中的飞机目标存在离散散射特性、姿态和尺度显著变化、背景杂波强散斑噪声以及复杂背景干扰，这些因素阻碍了稳定结构特征提取和精确定位。现有检测器主要依赖边界框回归和分类，未充分利用目标结构线索、空间注意力和频域信息，因此需要更鲁棒的特征融合方法。

Curriculum learning has demonstrated substantial effectiveness in robot learning. However, it still faces limitations when scaling to complex, wide-ranging task spaces. Such task spaces often lack a well-defined difficulty structure, making the difficulty ordering required by previous methods challenging to define. We propose a Learning Progress-based Automatic Curriculum Reinforcement Learning (LP-ACRL) framework, which estimates the agent's learning progress online and adaptively adjusts the task-sampling distribution, thereby enabling automatic curriculum generation without prior knowledge of the difficulty distribution over the task space. Policies trained with LP-ACRL enable the ANYmal D quadruped to achieve and maintain stable, high-speed locomotion at 2.5 m/s linear velocity and 3.0,/s angular velocity across diverse terrains, including stairs, slopes, gravel, and low-friction flat surfaces–whereas previous methods have generally been limited to high speeds on flat terrain or low speeds on complex terrain. Experimental results demonstrate that LP-ACRL exhibits strong scalability and real-world applicability, providing a robust baseline for future research on curriculum generation in complex, wide-ranging robotic learning task spaces.
